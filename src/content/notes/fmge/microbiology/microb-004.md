## General Virology

### Viral Structure
- **Nucleic acid**: DNA or RNA (never both)
- **Capsid**: Protein coat (made of capsomeres)
- **Envelope**: Lipid bilayer (present in some viruses — **enveloped** vs **naked**)
- **Size**: 20-300 nm (much smaller than bacteria)

### Viral Classification

| Feature | DNA Viruses | RNA Viruses |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Replication | In nucleus (most) | In cytoplasm (most) |
| Genome | dsDNA or ssDNA | dsRNA or ssRNA (+ or - sense) |
| Examples | Herpes, HBV, Adenovirus | HIV, Influenza, Polio |

### Replication
1. **Attachment**: Virus binds to host cell receptor
2. **Penetration**: Endocytosis or fusion
3. **Uncoating**: Genome released
4. **Replication**: Viral genome and proteins synthesized
5. **Assembly**: New virions assembled
6. **Release**: Budding or cell lysis

## DNA Viruses

### Herpesviridae Family

**Common features:**
- **dsDNA**, icosahedral, **enveloped**
- Latency in nerve ganglia
- Reactivation possible
- **Nuclear inclusion bodies** (Cowdry type A)

| Virus | Abbreviation | Disease |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| HSV-1 | Herpes simplex | Oral herpes (cold sores) |
| HSV-2 | Herpes simplex | Genital herpes |
| VZV | Varicella zoster | Chickenpox, shingles |
| EBV | Epstein-Barr | Infectious mononucleosis, Burkitt's lymphoma |
| CMV | Cytomegalovirus | Congenital infection, mononucleosis |
| HHV-6, 7 | Human herpes | Roseola infantum |

#### HSV-1 and HSV-2

**Characteristics:**
- **Vesicular lesions** on erythematous base
- **Tzanck smear**: Multinucleated giant cells (herpes group — not distinguishing HSV-1 from VZV)
- **Cowdry type A inclusions**: Viral inclusions in nucleus

**HSV-1:**
- Oral lesions (cold sores/fever blisters)
- Keratoconjunctivitis
- Encephalitis (temporal lobe)

**HSV-2:**
- Genital herpes
- Neonatal herpes (if mother infected at delivery)

**Treatment:**
- **Acyclovir** (or valacyclovir, famciclovir)
- Topical for mild cases, IV for severe/encephalitis

#### VZV (Varicella Zoster)

**Primary infection:** Chickenpox (Varicella)
- **Vesicular rash**: "Dewdrops on rose petal"
- Starts on trunk → spreads centrifugally
- Crops of lesions in different stages

**Reactivation:** Shingles (Herpes zoster)
- Painful vesicular rash in **dermatomal distribution**
- **Post-herpetic neuralgia**: Pain persists after rash heals

**Congenital VZV:** Limb hypoplasia, cicatricial scars, microcephaly

**Treatment:** Acyclovir

#### EBV (Epstein-Barr Virus)

**Disease:** Infectious mononucleosis ("kissing disease")
- Fever, pharyngitis, lymphadenopathy (classic triad)
- **Atypical lymphocytosis** (CD8+ T cells)
- **Heterophile antibodies** (Monospot test/Paul-Bunnell): Positive
- **Heterophile negative mono**: CMV

**Associations:**
- **Burkitt's lymphoma** (endemic in Africa — associated with malaria)
- **Nasopharyngeal carcinoma** (China)
- **Post-transplant lymphoproliferative disorder**

**Treatment:** Supportive (self-limiting)

#### CMV (Cytomegalovirus)

**Infectious mononucleosis-like syndrome** (heterophile-negative)

**Congenital CMV:**
- **Periventricular calcifications** in brain
- Microcephaly, hepatosplenomegaly, thrombocytopenia ("blueberry muffin" rash)
- **Leading infectious cause of congenital sensorineural hearing loss**

**In immunocompromised:**
- Retinitis (AIDS)
- Colitis, pneumonitis

**Laboratory:**
- **Owl's eye** inclusions in infected cells (CMV)
- **PP65 antigen** in blood (for monitoring)

### Hepatitis B Virus (HBV)

**Characteristics:**
- **dsDNA virus** (partially circular — relaxed circular DNA)
- **Hepadnavirus** family
- **HBsAg**: Surface antigen (outer envelope)
- **HBcAg**: Core antigen (inside)
- **HBeAg**: Pre-core protein (indicates active replication)

**Hepatitis B Serology:**

| Marker | Interpretation |
|--------|---------------|
| **HBsAg** | Active infection or chronic carrier |
| **Anti-HBs** | Immunity (natural infection or vaccine) |
| **HBeAg** | High infectivity, active replication |
| **Anti-HBe** | Low infectivity |
| **Anti-HBc IgM** | Acute infection |
| **Anti-HBc IgG** | Past or chronic infection |

**Interpretation of Serological Patterns:**

| HBsAg | Anti-HBs | Anti-HBc | Interpretation |
|-------|----------|----------|----------------|
| + | − | IgM+ | **Acute HBV** |
| + | − | IgG+ | **Chronic HBV** |
| − | + | + | **Resolved infection (immune)** |
| − | + | − | **Vaccination (immune)** |
| + | + | + | **Mutant HBV** |

**Clinical Phases of HBV:**
1. **Immune tolerance**: HBsAg+, HBeAg+, high viral load, normal ALT
2. **Immune clearance**: HBeAg+, elevated ALT, active hepatitis
3. **Inactive carrier**: HBsAg+, HBeAg-, low viral load
4. **HBsAg clearance**: Resolved infection

**Complications:**
- **Cirrhosis**
- **Hepatocellular carcinoma** (HCC) — third leading cause of cancer death worldwide
- **Co-infection with HDV** (delta hepatitis) — worse prognosis

**Prevention:**
- **HBV vaccine** (recombinant HBsAg)
- **Hepatitis B immunoglobulin (HBIG)**: Post-exposure prophylaxis

**Treatment:**
- **Nucleos(t)ide analogues**: Tenofovir, Entecavir (first-line)
- **Interferon-alpha**: Not commonly used now

### Hepatitis A Virus (HAV)

- **ssRNA, Picornavirus** family
- **Fecal-oral transmission**
- **Acute infection only** — NO chronic carrier state
- **Self-limiting** (rarely fatal)
- **Anti-HAV IgM**: Acute infection (diagnostic)
- **Anti-HAV IgG**: Past infection or vaccine (immunity)
- **Vaccine available**: Inactivated HAV

### Hepatitis C Virus (HCV)

- **ssRNA, Flavivirus** family
- **Blood-borne transmission** (IV drug use, blood transfusion)
- **High rate of chronic infection** (70-80%)
- **Leading cause of liver transplantation** in developed countries
- **Associated with**: Mixed cryoglobulinemia, B-cell lymphoma

**Diagnosis:**
- **Anti-HCV antibodies** (screening)
- **HCV RNA PCR** (confirmatory, quantifies viral load)
- **Genotyping**: Important for treatment (genotypes 1-6)

**Treatment:**
- **Direct-acting antivirals (DAAs)**: Sofosbuvir, Ledipasvir, etc.
- High cure rates (>95%)

## RNA Viruses

### HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus)

**Family:** Retroviridae ( Lentivirus)
**Genome:** **ssRNA (+ sense)**, diploid (2 copies)
**Key enzyme:** **Reverse transcriptase**

**Structure:**
- **Envelope**: gp120 and gp41 (target of antibodies, entry)
- **Core**: p24 (capsid), p17 (matrix)
- **RNA genome** + **reverse transcriptase**

### HIV Life Cycle
1. **Fusion**: gp120 binds CD4 + CCR5/CXCR4 on T cells
2. **Uncoating**: Viral core enters cytoplasm
3. **Reverse transcription**: RNA → DNA (reverse transcriptase)
4. **Integration**: DNA → host genome (integrase)
5. **Replication**: Proviral DNA transcribed → new RNA + proteins
6. **Assembly & Budding**

### HIV Infection Stages

| Stage | CD4 Count | Features |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Acute infection** | Variable | Flu-like syndrome, high viral load |
| **Clinical latency** | 200-500 | Asymptomatic, gradual decline |
| **AIDS** | <200 cells/μL | Opportunistic infections |

**AIDS-defining illnesses:**
- Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia (PCP)
- Candida esophagitis
- Cryptococcal meningitis
- Toxoplasmosis
- CMV retinitis
- Kaposi's sarcoma (HHV-8)
- Mycobacterium avium complex (MAC)
- Cryptosporidiosis

### HIV Transmission
- Sexual (heterosexual most common globally)
- Blood (IV drug use, blood transfusion)
- **Perinatal** (vertical transmission — during delivery or breastfeeding)

### HIV Diagnosis

**Screening:** 
- **Anti-HIV 1/2 antibodies** (ELISA/rapid tests)
- **4th generation**: Also detects p24 antigen (acute infection — window period reduced to 2 weeks)

**Confirmatory:**
- **Western blot** (traditional — not routinely used now)
- **HIV-1/HIV-2 differentiation assay**
- **HIV RNA PCR** (for early infection, infants born to HIV+ mothers)

### CD4 Count and Viral Load
- **CD4 count**: Monitor disease progression, treatment need
- **Viral load**: Treatment response (undetectable = successful treatment)

### HIV Treatment (ART — Antiretroviral Therapy)

**Goal**: Suppress viral load to undetectable levels

**Drug classes:**
| Class | Mechanism | Examples |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **NRTIs** | Block reverse transcriptase (chain terminators) | Tenofovir, Emtricitabine, Zidovudine |
| **NNRTIs** | Block reverse transcriptase (non-competitive) | Efavirenz, Nevirapine |
| **Integrase inhibitors** | Block integrase | Raltegravir, Dolutegravir |
| **Protease inhibitors** | Block protease | Ritonavir (booster), Atazanavir |
| **Entry inhibitors** | Block fusion/CCR5 | Enfuvirtide, Maraviroc |

**Standard regimen**: 2 NRTIs + 1 NNRTI OR 2 NRTIs + 1 Integrase inhibitor

**PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis):**
- Tenofovir + Emtricitabine (daily)
- For high-risk individuals

**PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis):**
- ART for 28 days after exposure
- Start as soon as possible (within 72 hours)

## Other Important RNA Viruses

### Influenza Virus

**Family:** Orthomyxoviridae
**Genome:** **8 segments of ssRNA (- sense)**
**Types**: A, B, C (A and B cause human disease)
**Envelope spikes**: Hemagglutinin (HA) + Neuraminidase (NA)

**Antigenic variation:**
- **Antigenic drift**: Point mutations → seasonal epidemics
- **Antigenic shift**: Gene reassortment → pandemics (e.g., H1N1 2009)

**Treatment:**
- **Oseltamivir** (Tamiflu) — Neuraminidase inhibitor
- **Zanamivir** — Inhaled
- **M2 inhibitors** (Amantadine, Rimantadine) — obsolete due to resistance

**Prevention:**
- **Influenza vaccine** (trivalent: H1N1 + H3N2 + B)
- Annual vaccination recommended

### Poliovirus

**Family:** Picornaviridae
**Type:** ssRNA (+ sense)
- **Types 1, 2, 3** (Sabin vaccine covers all 3)

**Disease:** Poliomyelitis (polio)
- **Aseptic meningitis**
- **Flaccid paralysis** (anterior horn cell destruction)
- **Bulbar polio** (respiratory failure)

**Vaccine:**
- **IPV** (Inactivated — Salk): Injectable
- **OPV** (Oral — Sabin): Live attenuated, provides gut immunity
- OPV rarely causes **vaccine-associated paralytic polio (VAPP)**

**Eradication:** WHO eradication program — Type 2 eradicated (2015), Type 3 eradicated (2019)

### Rabies Virus

**Family:** Rhabdoviridae
**Genome:** ssRNA (- sense)
**Shape**: Bullet-shaped

**Transmission:** Animal bite (dog, bat, etc.)
**Incubation:** Weeks to months (depends on distance to CNS)

**Pathogenesis:**
- Virus replicates in muscle at bite site
- Travels via peripheral nerves to CNS
- **Negri bodies** in neurons (pathognomonic — eosinophilic inclusions)

**Clinical:**
- **Furious rabies**: Hydrophobia, aerophobia, seizures, salivation
- **Paralytic rabies**: Ascending paralysis

**Diagnosis:** Skin biopsy (nerve endings), PCR of saliva

**Treatment:**
- **Post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP)**:
  - Wound cleaning
  - **Rabies immunoglobulin** (RIG) — at bite site
  - **Rabies vaccine** — Days 0, 3, 7, 14, 28

**Prevention:** Pre-exposure vaccine for high-risk (veterinarians, lab workers)

### Dengue Virus

**Family:** Flaviviridae
**Genome:** ssRNA (+ sense)
**Types:** 1, 2, 3, 4 (4 serotypes)

**Transmission:** Aedes aegypti mosquito

**Disease:**
- **Dengue fever**: Fever, headache, retro-orbital pain, myalgia ("breakbone fever")
- **Dengue hemorrhagic fever (DHF)**: Severe bleeding, plasma leakage
- **Dengue shock syndrome (DSS)**: Hypovolemic shock

**Pathology:** Antibody-dependent enhancement (ADE) — second infection with different serotype causes more severe disease

**Diagnosis:**
- **NS1 antigen** (early, first 5 days)
- **IgM/IgG antibodies** (ELISA)
- PCR (early)

**Treatment:** Supportive (fluid replacement, paracetamol — NOT NSAIDs/aspirin)

### Japanese Encephalitis Virus

**Family:** Flaviviridae
**Transmission:** Culex mosquito (amplified in pigs and birds)
**Endemic:** South and Southeast Asia

**Disease:** Encephalitis
- **Negri bodies NOT seen** (unlike rabies)
- **Pathology**: Perivascular cuffing, gliosis, neuronal necrosis

**Prevention:** **JE vaccine** (inactivated, live attenuated)

### Common FMGE Questions

**Q: Which virus causes Burkitt's lymphoma?**
A: EBV (Epstein-Barr virus)

**Q: CMV congenital infection features:**
A: Periventricular calcifications, sensorineural hearing loss

**Q: HBsAg positive + Anti-HBc IgM positive:**
A: Acute HBV infection

**Q: CD4 count below what defines AIDS?**
A: <200 cells/μL

**Q: HIV entry into cell requires:**
A: CD4 receptor + CCR5 or CXCR4 co-receptor

**Q: First-line HIV treatment:**
A: 2 NRTIs + Integrase inhibitor (e.g., Tenofovir + Emtricitabine + Dolutegravir)

**Q: Rabies diagnosis shows:**
A: Negri bodies (eosinophilic inclusions in neurons)

**Q: Dengue virus mechanism for severe disease:**
A: Antibody-dependent enhancement (ADE) on second infection with different serotype

**Q: Influenza treatment:**
A: Oseltamivir (neuraminidase inhibitor)
