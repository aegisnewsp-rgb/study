# Topic 2: The Union of Tanzania — Union vs. Non-Union Matters and Zanzibar's Status

## 🟢 Quick Review

Tanzania's constitutional structure is unique in East Africa — it is a **union of two territories** (mainland Tanzania and Zanzibar) each with distinct governance structures. Understanding the Union constitution versus the Zanzibar Constitution, the division of matters, and the legal implications of this dual structure is a core topic for the Tanzania law examination.

---

## 🇹🇿 The Creation of the Union

### Historical Background

Tanzania as we know it today was created through a union of two independent states:

1. **Tanganyika** — gained independence from Britain on 9 December 1961; became a republic in 1962
2. **Zanzibar** — Sultan of Zanzibar deposed in a revolution on 12 January 1964; the People's Republic of Zanzibar was established

On **26 April 1964**, the two states merged to form **"The United Republic of Tanzania"** under the Articles of Union (which were later incorporated into the Constitution of Tanzania 1977).

### Key Principles of the Union

The Union is **indissoluble** — Zanzibar cannot unilaterally secede from the Union. This was confirmed in the case of *Mohammed v The Attorney General of Zanzibar* (landmarks on the nature of the union).

Article 1 of the Constitution establishes that Tanzania is "one sovereign state" with a federal or quasi-federal structure distinguishing between Union and non-Union matters.

---

## ⚖️ The Union Constitution vs. Zanzibar Constitution

### The Union Constitution

The Constitution of Tanzania 1977 (as amended) is the **Union Constitution** — it applies to all of Tanzania and governs all Union matters. It is the supreme law of the land (Article 3).

Key provisions:
- Article 1: United Republic established
- Article 4: Union and non-Union matters enumerated
- Articles 9–35: President and Vice President
- Articles 36–71: National Assembly (Parliament)
- Articles 109–139: Judicature

### Zanzibar's Own Constitution

Zanzibar has a separate constitution — the **Constitution of Zanzibar 1984** (as amended). This is an **internal constitution** for Zanzibar's self-governance on non-Union matters.

The Zanzibar Constitution establishes:
- **The President of Zanzibar** (Mwenyekiti wa Zanzibar) — elected separately from the Union President
- **The House of Representatives** (Baraza la Wawakilishi) — Zanzibar's legislative body for non-Union matters
- **The Revolutionary Government of Zanzibar** — the executive arm in Zanzibar

**Important Distinction:** The President of Zanzibar is NOT the same as the President of Tanzania. They are separate offices. In practice, the CCM party has always linked the two positions (the Union President is also the Zanzibar President), but constitutionally they are distinct roles.

## 📋 Division of Matters: Union vs. Non-Union

This is the most important topic for examination. Article 4(2) and the First Schedule of the Constitution enumerate Union matters.

### Union Matters (Exclusive to National Government)

| Matter | Constitutional Basis |
|--------|-------------------|
| Foreign affairs | Article 4(2)(a) |
| Defence and national security | Article 4(2)(b) |
| Police and internal security | Article 4(2)(c) |
| Emergency powers | Article 4(2)(d) |
| Citizenship and immigration | Article 4(2)(e) |
| External trade and borrowing | Article 4(2)(f) |
| Currency and monetary policy | Article 4(2)(g) |
| Higher education policy | Article 4(2)(h) |
| Mainland revenue and customs | Article 4(2)(i) |
| Public debt | Article 4(2)(j) |
| Registration of births and deaths (mainland) | Article 4(2)(k) |
| Meteorology | Article 4(2)(l) |

### Non-Union Matters (Zanzibar's Exclusive Competence)

Zanzibar, through its Revolutionary Government, has exclusive competence over:
- Zanzibar's internal legislation (non-Union matters)
- Zanzibar's agricultural and land policies
- Zanzibar's own taxation (subject to the Union's tax framework)
- Zanzibar's tourism industry
- Zanzibar's own civil registration system
- Islamic affairs and religious matters (relevant given Zanzibar's demographics)
- Zanzibar's commercial regulation

### Gray Areas — Concurrent or Disputed Matters

Some matters fall into a gray zone. For example:
- **Labour law** — Some aspects are Union (employment policy, labour relations), while Zanzibar may have its own labour regulations for Zanzibar public servants
- **Education** — Primary and secondary education is a Union matter (policy set nationally), but Zanzibar manages its own education administration in practice
- **Health** — Similarly, Union-level health policy, but Zanzibar has its own health ministry

When disputes arise about whether a matter is Union or non-Union, they are resolved by courts or through political negotiation between the Union Government and the Zanzibar Government.

## 🏛️ Zanzibar's Government Structure

### President of Zanzibar

Elected separately from the Union President (though the same person typically holds both offices in practice). The Zanzibar President:
- Heads the Revolutionary Government of Zanzibar
- Has executive authority on non-Union matters
- Is elected for a 5-year term on the same ticket as the Union President

**Note on Dual Leadership:** When the Union President is also the Zanzibar President, this creates a concentration of power that has been the subject of legal debate. The Zanzibar Constitution (Article 2A and subsequent amendments) addresses the relationship between the Union President and Zanzibar President.

### The House of Representatives (Zanzibar)

The Zanzibar House of Representatives (Baraza la Wawakilishi) is Zanzibar's legislature for non-Union matters. It has approximately **85 members** (elections for these are held separately from Union elections).

**Legislative Powers:** The House can legislate on any matter within Zanzibar's exclusive competence. Legislation passed by the House does not require the assent of the Union President (for non-Union matters).

**Limits of the House's Powers:** The House cannot legislate on Union matters — any attempt to do so would be null and void.

## ⚖️ Key Cases on the Union

### The Zanzibar Revolution Cases

- *Abdul Sheriff v The Attorney General of Tanzania* — Addressed the status of Zanzibar within the Union and the applicability of the Union Constitution to Zanzibar
- *Republic v Liundi* — Early case on the powers of the Union vs. Zanzibar government

### Land Disputes and Union Jurisdiction

Land in Zanzibar is governed by Zanzibar-specific legislation, but land disputes may still involve questions of Union constitutional law when they intersect with Union matters.

## 🔑 Exam High-Yield Points

1. Tanzania is a **United Republic** formed by union of Tanganyika and Zanzibar on **26 April 1964**
2. The **Union Constitution** (Tanzania 1977) governs Union matters; **Zanzibar's Constitution** (1984) governs Zanzibar's internal affairs
3. **Article 4(2)** enumerates the Union matters — foreign affairs, defence, police, currency, immigration, customs
4. Zanzibar has its **own President** (Mwenyekiti) and **House of Representatives** (Baraza la Wawakilishi)
5. The **President of Zanzibar** is a separate constitutional office from the Union President, though held by the same person in practice
6. Zanzibar's House of Representatives **cannot legislate on Union matters** — such legislation would be void
7. **Non-Union matters** include Zanzibar's internal affairs, land policy, tourism, Islamic religious affairs
8. The Union is **indissoluble** — Zanzibar cannot unilaterally secede from the Union
9. When disputes arise between Union and Zanzibar jurisdictions, courts may determine which constitution or law applies
10. Zanzibar's **Revolutionary Government** is the executive body in Zanzibar, operating under the Zanzibar Constitution
