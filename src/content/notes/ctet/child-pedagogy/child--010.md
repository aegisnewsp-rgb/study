# Assessment and Evaluation

## Key Distinctions

Before diving into types, CTET candidates must clearly distinguish three related but different concepts:

**Measurement**: The process of assigning numbers or symbols to attributes according to rules (e.g., a test score of 85/100). It is quantitative — gives a numerical value.

**Assessment**: The broader process of collecting information about student learning — includes tests but also observations, projects, portfolios. It answers: "What does this student know and can do?"

**Evaluation**: The process of making judgments about the quality or value of student performance, often using criteria and benchmarks. It answers: "How good is this performance?"

> **Simple memory**: Measurement = numbers; Assessment = collecting evidence; Evaluation = judging quality against standards.

## Types of Assessment

### Formative Assessment (Assessment FOR Learning)
Conducted **during** instruction to monitor learning progress and provide ongoing feedback. The goal is to identify learning gaps and adjust teaching before the final evaluation.

**Characteristics:**
- Continuous, in-process
- Diagnostic — identifies what's going wrong
- Feedback-rich — tells students exactly where they went wrong
- Low-stakes — doesn't carry heavy weight in final grades

**Examples:**
- Exit slips (one-minute reflection at end of class)
- Quiz at end of lesson
- Teacher circulating during group work, asking questions
- Peer assessment

**Benefits for CTET**: Formative assessment is closely linked to feedback — and effective feedback is the single most impactful factor in student learning (Hattie, 2009).

### Summative Assessment (Assessment OF Learning)
Conducted **at the end** of a unit, term, or year to evaluate learning against standards. The goal is to assign grades and certify learning.

**Characteristics:**
- End-point evaluation
- High-stakes (affects grades, promotion)
- Compares student to a standard or norm
- Result-oriented

**Examples:**
- Final examination
- End-of-term test
- Board examination
- Annual performance report

### Diagnostic Assessment
Conducted **before** instruction to identify prior knowledge, existing misconceptions, and learning gaps. It informs the teacher's preparation and differentiated planning.

**Examples:**
- Pre-unit test at the start of a new topic
- Baseline assessment at the beginning of academic year
- Diagnostic test for students showing learning difficulties

> **CTET tip**: Diagnostic assessment is similar to formative but happens BEFORE instruction, not during. Formative is during, diagnostic is pre.

## Continuous and Comprehensive Evaluation (CCE)

CCE was introduced by CBSE for Classes VI-IX (now modified under NEP 2020). It aimed at:

- **Continuous**: Assessment spread throughout the year, not just end-of-year exams
- **Comprehensive**: Covers both scholastic (academic) and co-scholastic (creative, affective, psychomotor) domains

**Scholastic areas**: All academic subjects (assessed via formative + summative)
**Co-scholastic areas**: Life skills, work education, art, health & physical education, ICT, attitude and values

**Features:**
- Formative assessment (formative tests, unit tests, projects, oral questions)
- Summative assessment (end-of-term examinations)
- Co-schedules maintained — report cards include narrative descriptions, not just marks
- No high-stakes board examinations for Classes VIII-X (at the time)

> NEP 2020 has since revised this with 5+3+3+4 curricular structure and changes to assessment at secondary level.

## Bloom's Taxonomy (Revised)

Bloom's taxonomy classifies educational objectives into three domains and six cognitive levels (lowest to highest):

**Cognitive Domain:**
| Level | Keyword | What it involves |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Remember | Recall | Memorizing, reciting, identifying |
| 2. Understand | Explain | Summarizing, interpreting, paraphrasing |
| 3. Apply | Use | Solving problems, using in new situations |
| 4. Analyze | Distinguish | Breaking into parts, finding relationships |
| 5. Evaluate | Justify | Critiquing, justifying, prioritizing |
| 6. Create | Design | Producing original work, designing solutions |

**Affective Domain**: Receiving → Responding → Valuing → Organizing → Characterizing
**Psychomotor Domain**: Perception → Set → Guided Response → Mechanism → Complex Response → Organization

**CTET exam focus**: Know the 6 levels in order (Remember → Create) — questions often give a teaching activity and ask which level it targets.

## Characteristics of a Good Assessment Tool

| Characteristic | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Validity | Measures what it claims to measure |
| Reliability | Gives consistent results across time and contexts |
| Objectivity | Same result regardless of who scores it |
| Comprehensiveness | Covers all important learning objectives |
| Feasibility | Practicable within available time and resources |
| Diagnostic utility | Clearly identifies what the student got wrong and why |
| Discrimination | Differentiates between high and low performers |

## Feedback for Learning

Effective feedback:
1. Is **specific** — "Your introduction doesn't state your thesis clearly" beats "Good job"
2. Is **timely** — given soon after the learning, while the student still remembers
3. Indicates **what to do next** — not just what's wrong, but how to improve
4. Focuses on **task, not person** — "The essay has weak arguments" not "You are lazy"
5. Is **matched to the learner's developmental level** — scaffolding in feedback
