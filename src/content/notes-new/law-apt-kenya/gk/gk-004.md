# Topic 4: Current Affairs & Governance in Kenya — Elections, IEBC, Devolution, County Governments

## 🟢 Quick Review

Kenya's governance framework was transformed by the Constitution of Kenya 2010, which introduced devolution — 47 county governments sharing power with the national government. Understanding the electoral system, the IEBC, devolution structures, and current governance issues is essential for the law aptitude examination.

---

## 🗳️ Elections in Kenya

### The Right to Vote (Article 38)

Every citizen who is 18 years or older has the right to vote in elections (presidential, parliamentary, and county elections). Voting is by secret ballot.

### Types of Elections

1. **Presidential Election** — Held every 5 years. A candidate must receive more than 50% of all votes cast AND at least 25% of votes in more than half of the 47 counties to be elected. If no candidate meets these thresholds, a run-off is held within 30 days (this occurred in the 2017 elections, resulting in the famous *Raila Odinga v IEBC* Supreme Court case).

2. **Parliamentary Elections** — For the National Assembly (290 constituency seats + 47 women representatives from counties) and the Senate (47 seats — one per county).

3. **County Elections** — For the 47 County Governors and the County Assemblies.

### The Electoral Process

- **Nomination:** Political parties and independent candidates nominate candidates through the IEBC
- **Campaign period:** Regulated by the IEBC; starts upon official nomination
- **Voting:** By physical ballot (Kenya has not adopted electronic voting)
- ** tallying:** The IEBC tallies results at constituency and national levels
- **Disputed elections:** Presidential election disputes go to the Supreme Court (Article 163(4)); Parliamentary and county disputes go to the Election Court (a division of the High Court)

## 🏢 The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC)

### Constitutional Basis

**Article 88 of the Constitution** establishes the IEBC as an independent body responsible for:
- Conducting and supervising elections (presidential, parliamentary, county)
- Delimiting (boundary-setting) electoral units (constituencies, wards)
- Regulating political party nominations
- Voter registration and identification

### Composition and Structure

The IEBC consists of:
- A Chairperson (appointed by the President from persons nominated by the Judicial Service Commission)
- Vice Chairperson
- 5 other members
- A Secretariat headed by a CEO

**Current Context:** The IEBC has undergone significant changes after the 2017 election crisis. The *B挺* reforms followed the Supreme Court's nullification of the 2017 presidential election in *Raila Odinga v IEBC* [2017] (the first time a presidential election was annulled in Africa).

### Key Functions

| Function | Details |
|----------|---------|
| Voter registration | Citizens 18+ can register; National Integrated Identity Card (NIIMS) linked |
| Nomination management | Political parties submit candidate lists; IEBC verifies eligibility |
| Ballot security | Printing, distribution, and accounting for ballot papers |
| Tallying | Constituency tallying → National tallying |
| Boundary delimitation | Redrawing constituency and ward boundaries every 8-12 years |

### Landmark Cases on Elections

- ***Raila Odinga v IEBC* [2017] Supreme Court** — The Supreme Court, in a 4-2 decision, nullified the 2017 presidential election result for non-compliance with the Constitution. The IEBC chairman had declared results before completing the verification process. The court ordered a fresh election within 60 days. This case fundamentally changed how elections are conducted and verified.
- ***IEBC v Raila Odinga* (2007)** — The post-election violence case addressing the 2007 disputed election results
- ***Munya v IEBC* [2014]** — Issues around party primaries and nomination

## 🏡 Devolution and County Governments

### Constitutional Basis

**Chapter 11 (Articles 174–200)** of the Constitution establishes Kenya's devolved system of government.

### Why Devolution?

Article 174 sets out the objects of devolution:
1. Promotion of democratic and accountable exercise of power
2. Fostering national unity by recognizing diversity
3. Giving powers of self-governance to people
4. Enabling delivery of services
5. Facilitating economic development
6. Recognizing the right of communities to manage their affairs
7. Developing participatory governance
8. Providing checks against abuse of power

### The Two Levels of Government

**National Government** retains exclusive responsibility for: foreign affairs, defence, national security, citizenship, immigration, education (national policy), health (national policy), finance, transport, communications, water infrastructure, major utilities.

**County Governments** (47 counties) are responsible for:
- Agriculture, livestock, and fisheries
- County health services
- County transport and roads
- Trade development and regulation
- County planning and land use
- Pre-primary education (ECDE)
- Sports and cultural affairs
- Control of air pollution and noise
- Firefighting and disaster management
- Public works (county roads)

### Structures of County Government

Each county has:
- **County Executive Committee** — Headed by the Governor; implements county legislation and policies
- **County Assembly** — Legislative body with power to enact county laws, approve executive appointments, and oversight
- **County Public Service Board** — Manages county human resources

**County Governor** is elected for a single 5-year term (cannot serve more than 2 terms). The Governor and Deputy Governor form the executive.

### The Senate

The Senate represents the counties and serves as the house of sub-national governments. Its roles include:
- Ratifying appointments (e.g., members of the Cabinet, heads of state organs)
- Approving legislation touching on counties
- Oversight of county revenue allocation
- Hearing petitions concerning county matters

### The Consolidated Fund and County Revenue

The **Commission on Revenue Allocation (CRA)** recommends the equitable sharing of revenue between national and county governments (at least 15% of national revenue must go to counties).

## 🔑 Key Constitutional Amendments Relating to Governance

- **17th Amendment (2017):** Shifted original jurisdiction over presidential election disputes from the High Court to the Supreme Court
- **2017 Chronology:** The repeat presidential election (October 2017) saw Raila Odinga boycott; Uhuru Kenyatta was declared elected unopposed
- **2022:** The Political Parties Act was amended to address coalition governance

## 🔑 Exam High-Yield Points

1. **IEBC** is constitutionally independent (Article 88) — cannot be influenced by the Executive
2. Presidential election requires **50%+1 of all votes cast** AND **25% in at least 24 counties** — if not achieved, a run-off is triggered
3. Presidential election disputes go to the **Supreme Court**, NOT the Court of Appeal or High Court
4. There are **47 counties** in Kenya, each with a Governor and County Assembly
5. County governments get at least **15% of national revenue** (Article 203)
6. *Raila Odinga v IEBC* [2017] is the landmark case on electoral accountability — it established that the IEBC must verify results before declaring them
7. **Devolution's purpose** (Article 174) includes service delivery, democratic accountability, national unity, and prevention of centralization of power
8. The **County Assembly** can enact legislation on any matter within the county's competence; national legislation on county matters requires Senate approval
