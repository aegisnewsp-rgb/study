## United Nations — Special Days and Observances

### UN Day (October 24)
- Marks the day in 1945 when the UN Charter came into force
- **UN Charter** signed on June 26, 1945 in San Francisco; entered into force October 24, 1945
- Celebrated as the birthday of the United Nations
- 2023 marked the UN's 78th anniversary

### Human Rights Day (December 10)
- Commemorates the adoption of the **Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR)** by the UN General Assembly on December 10, 1948
- **UDHR** is the foundational human rights document — 30 articles guaranteeing civil, political, economic, social, and cultural rights
- Eleanor Roosevelt chaired the drafting committee
- Famous phrase: *"All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights"*

### International Day of Peace (September 21)
- Established by the UN General Assembly in 1981
- Theme varies each year (e.g., 2023: "Climate Action for Peace")
- Day of global ceasefire and non-violence

### International Women's Day (March 8)
- Celebrates women's achievements and raises awareness about gender inequality
- First observed in 1911 (following 1909 garment workers' strike in New York)
- 2023 Theme: "DigitALL: Innovation and technology for gender equality"

### World Environment Day (June 5)
- Largest global platform for environmental outreach
- Hosted by a different country each year
- **2023 Host:** Cote d'Ivoire (topic: solution-oriented solutions to plastic pollution)
- **2024 Host:** Saudi Arabia

### World Population Day (July 11)
- Commemorates the day in 1987 when the world reached 5 billion people
- Raises awareness about population issues (fertility, migration, family planning)

### International Day of Disabled Persons (December 3)
- Promotes inclusion and accessibility for persons with disabilities
- Commemorates 2012 adoption of the **UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD)**

## G7 and G20 Summits

### G7 (Group of Seven)

**Founded:** 1975 (originally G6: France, West Germany, Italy, Japan, UK, USA); Canada added in 1977
**Members:** Canada, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, United Kingdom, United States (+ EU as observer)
**2023 Host:** Hiroshima, Japan
**2024 Host:** Apulia, Italy

- Represents ~40% of global GDP and ~10% of world population
- Focus: Global economic governance, climate, health, security

### G20 (Group of Twenty)

**Founded:** 1999 (finance ministers); elevated to summit level in 2008
**Members (19 countries + EU):**
Argentina, Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, France, Germany, India, Indonesia, Italy, Japan, Mexico, Russia, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, South Korea, Turkey, United Kingdom, United States + **European Union**

- Represents **~80% of global GDP** and **~65% of world population**
- **2023 Host:** New Delhi, India (theme: "One Earth, One Family, One Future")
- **2024 Host:** Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

## Major International Summits and Conferences

### COP — Conference of the Parties (UNFCCC)
The supreme decision-making body of the **UN Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC)**.

| Summit | Year | Location | Key Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| COP3 | 1997 | Kyoto, Japan | **Kyoto Protocol** — first binding emissions targets |
| COP15 | 2009 | Copenhagen, Denmark | Climate pledges (non-binding) |
| COP21 | 2015 | Paris, France | **Paris Agreement** — limit warming to 1.5–2°C |
| COP26 | 2021 | Glasgow, UK | Glasgow Climate Pact; coal phase-down |
| COP27 | 2022 | Sharm El Sheikh, Egypt | Loss and Damage Fund agreed |
| COP28 | 2023 | Dubai, UAE | First Global Stocktake; fossil fuel "transitioning away" |

### World Economic Forum (WEF) — Davos

- Annual meeting held in **Davos, Switzerland** (January)
- Brings together global leaders from business, government, civil society
- Founded by **Klaus Schwab** (1971)
- 2024 theme: "Rebuilding Trust"

### Commonwealth Heads of Government Meeting (CHOGM)
- Held annually among Commonwealth nations (~56 member states)
- Pakistan attends as a member (suspended briefly after 1999 coup; rejoined after Zia's death)

## Nobel Prize — Historical Context

### Alfred Nobel (1833–1896)
- Swedish chemist, engineer, inventor of dynamite (1867)
- His fortune was used to establish the Nobel Prizes through his will
- The will stipulated awards for those who "shall have conferred the greatest benefit to mankind"

### Pakistani Nobel Laureates:
- **Dr. Abdus Salam (1979):** Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on electroweak theory (unified electromagnetic and weak nuclear forces)
  - He remains the **only Pakistani Nobel laureate** and the **first Muslim to receive a Nobel in Science**
  - Founded the International Centre for Theoretical Physics (ICTP) in Trieste, Italy

## Key Formula for NABE

- **UN Security Council formula:** 5 permanent members + 10 elected members = 15 total (2/3 majority needed to pass substantive resolutions)
- **Nobel Prize formula:** Alfred Nobel's will → 5 original prizes → 6th (Economic Sciences) added 1968
- **SDG formula:** 17 goals × 169 targets × 232 indicators = 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development
