#### 1. Cricket — Bangladesh's National Obsession

Cricket is far and away the most popular sport in Bangladesh. It is not merely a game — it is a cultural phenomenon that united a young nation.

**Rise of Cricket in Bangladesh:**
- Cricket was introduced during British colonial rule
- The 1990s saw the rise of Bangladesh's domestic cricket talent
- Bangladesh was granted **ODI status** in 1999 and played its first World Cup that year (England)
- In **2000**, Bangladesh was elevated to **Test cricket** status by the ICC

**First Test Match:**
Bangladesh played its first Test match against India at the Bangabandhu National Stadium in Dhaka from 10–13 November 2000. Bangladesh was dismissed for 400 and 91, losing by 9 wickets. Despite the loss, the match was a historic milestone.

**Key Achievements:**

**1999 Cricket World Cup:**
Bangladesh's debut World Cup. They defeated **Zimbabwe** in their opening match — their first World Cup victory. This win helped cement Bangladesh's place in international cricket.

**2007 ICC Cricket World Cup — The Upset:**
Bangladesh defeated **Australia** (the reigning champions) in the 2007 World Cup in the West Indies. This shock victory confirmed Bangladesh as a serious cricketing nation and humiliated Australia. Shakib Al Hasan was instrumental.

**2015 Cricket World Cup:**
Bangladesh reached the quarter-finals (郂ashmir), a historic achievement. They defeated England and Afghanistan in the group stage.

**2019 Cricket World Cup:**
Bangladesh finished 7th in the points table with notable wins over West Indies and Afghanistan.

**Key Players:**

**Shakib Al Hasan:**
- Considered one of the best all-rounders in world cricket
- No. 1 in ICC all-rounder rankings (both Test and ODI)
- First Bangladeshi to score 5,000 Test runs and take 200 Test wickets
- Captained Bangladesh in all three formats
- Named **ICC's Best All-rounder** multiple times

**Tamim Iqbal:**
- Bangladesh's highest ever ODI run scorer (over 8,000 runs)
- Opening batsman; known for his elegance and consistency
- Scored Bangladesh's first ever World Cup century (2015 vs. England)

**Mushfiqur Rahim:**
- Wicket-keeper batsman; one of Bangladesh's most consistent performers
- Second highest run-scorer for Bangladesh in Tests and ODIs
- Known for his fighting spirit and match-winning knocks

**Mustafizur Rahman:**
- Pace bowler known for his " cutter" variation
- Made his debut in 2015 and quickly became a star
- Took a 5-wicket haul on IPL debut for Sunrisers Hyderabad

**Bangladesh Premier League (BPL):**
The BPL, launched in 2012, is Bangladesh's domestic T20 league. It attracts international stars and has become one of the world's major T20 leagues.

#### 2. Football in Bangladesh

Football (soccer) is the second most popular sport in Bangladesh after cricket.

**Bangladesh National Football Team:**
- Nickname: **Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujib Cup Bangladesh**
- Has never qualified for the FIFA World Cup
- Best achievement: South Asian Games gold medal (2010, 2019)
- SAFF Championship: Won in 2012 (defeating India in the final)

**Bangladesh in AFC Asian Cup:**
Bangladesh qualified for the AFC Asian Cup for the first time in **2023** (held in Qatar in 2024). This was a historic achievement.

**Club Football:**
**Bangladesh Premier League (Football):** The top tier of Bangladeshi club football. Clubs include ** Dhaka Abahani**, ** Dhaka League**, and others.

**Dhaka Derby:**
The rivalry between ** Dhaka Abahani** and ** Sheikh Jamal Dhanmondi Club** is Bangladesh's biggest football rivalry.

#### 3. Olympic Participation

**First Olympic Games:**
Bangladesh made its Olympic debut at the **1992 Barcelona Olympics**. Bangladesh's team consisted of 5 athletes across athletics, shooting, and swimming.

**Olympic History:**
- **1992 Barcelona:** First participation
- **1996 Atlanta:** 6 athletes
- **2000 Sydney:** 6 athletes
- **2004 Athens:** 6 athletes
- **2008 Beijing:** 6 athletes
- **2012 London:** 6 athletes
- **2016 Rio:** 7 athletes
- **2020 Tokyo (held 2021):** 7 athletes
- **2024 Paris:** 7 athletes

**Medal Success:**
Bangladesh is yet to win an Olympic medal. The closest was in ** boxing** and **weightlifting** — Bangladeshi athletes have competed in these sports at multiple Olympics.

**Commonwealth Games:**
Bangladesh has participated in the Commonwealth Games since 1998 (Kuala Lumpur). Bangladesh won its **first Commonwealth Games medals in 2018 at the Gold Coast Games:**
- **Sashikala (weightlifting):** Bronze medal
- **Other medals in boxing and wrestling**

Bangladesh also won medals in the **2010 Delhi Commonwealth Games** (Gold in men's cricket was a demonstration sport; officially recognised in later years)

#### 4. Athletics and Other Sports

**Athletics:**
Bangladesh's athletics achievements are modest but include:
- **Siddikur Rahman:** Golfer — first Bangladeshi to win a professional Asian Tour event (2010)
- **Syed Abdul Kader:** Olympic shooter
- **Gias Uddin:** Athlete — represented Bangladesh internationally in sprints

**Chess:**
Bangladesh has a surprisingly strong chess tradition:
- **Niaz Murshed:** Grandmaster — first Grandmaster from Bangladesh and South Asia (1987)
- Bangladesh has produced several Grandmasters since

**Kabaddi:**
Bangladesh has a strong kabaddi team, winning medals in international competitions including the Asian Games.

#### 5. The 2018 ICC U-19 World Cup — Bangladesh's Future Stars

Bangladesh's Under-19 team reached the **final of the ICC U-19 World Cup in 2018** (held in New Zealand), losing to India. This generation of players — including **Tanzid Hasan, Towhid Hridoy, and Mahmudul Hasan Joy** — went on to become stars of the senior national team.

#### 6. Cricket Administration and Infrastructure

**Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujib International Cricket Stadium:**
Bangladesh's main cricket stadium is named after the founding president. The stadium is located in Dhaka and has hosted many international matches.

**BCB (Bangladesh Cricket Board):**
Formerly the Bangladesh Cricket Board, now officially Bangladesh Cricket Board, was founded in 1972. It became an ICC full member in 2000.

#### 7. FIFA and International Recognition

Bangladesh is a member of **FIFA** and the **Asian Football Confederation (AFC)**. The Bangladesh Football Federation (BFF) is the governing body.

**SAFF Championship Wins:**
Bangladesh won the **SAFF Championship in 2012**, defeating India 2-1 in the final in Kathmandu. This remains Bangladesh's greatest football achievement. They won again in the South Asian Games.
