# Important Days, Events, and Observances of Bangladesh

Bangladesh's national calendar is rich with commemorations that reflect its history, culture, and values. Understanding these days is essential not only for general knowledge but also because they are frequently tested in the DU Admission examination. Questions about national days, their significance, and associated historical events appear regularly.

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)

> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

#### 1. Language Martyrs' Day — 21 February

This is Bangladesh's most sacred national day and one of its most internationally recognised.

**Historical Background:**
The Language Movement of 1952 was a political campaign to establish Bengali as an official language of East Pakistan. When the Pakistani government declared Urdu as the only national language, Bengali-speaking East Pakistanis protested. On 21 February 1952, students at Dhaka University protested this decision.

**The Event:**
Police opened fire on the protesters. Those killed included:
- **Abul Barkat** (student, University of Dhaka)
- **Rafiq Uddin Ahmed** (student)
- **Abdul Jabbar** (worker)
- **Ahmed Jabber**
- **Shafiur Rahman**

**21 February 1952** became Bangladesh's Language Martyrs' Day. The movement eventually succeeded — Bengali was recognised as an official language in 1956.

**International Recognition:**
In 1999, UNESCO declared **21 February as International Mother Language Day**. It is observed in over 100 countries. This is Bangladesh's most significant cultural contribution to the world.

**How Bangladesh Observes 21 February:**
- Schools and government offices remain closed
- People visit the **Muktijuddho Smriti Soudho** (National Martyrs' Memorial) at Savar
- The **Ekushey Padak** is awarded annually for contributions to Bengali language, literature, and culture
- The historic **Amar Bhighan** (Martyrs' Monument) at Dhaka University was built in 1963 and demolished during the Pakistan army crackdown in 1971

#### 2. Independence Day — 26 March

**The Declaration:**
On 26 March 1971, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman formally declared Bangladesh's independence at the Gonobhaban in Dhaka. He had been arrested by the Pakistani army the night before (Operation Searchlight began on 25 March). He issued a written proclamation of independence before being taken away.

**From 26 March 1971:**
The date marks the beginning of the Liberation War. On this day, the **Mujibnagar Government** was also announced as the provisional government of Bangladesh, with Tajuddin Ahmad as the first Prime Minister and Sheikh Mujibur Rahman as President.

**National Holiday:**
26 March is observed as Independence Day and is a public holiday. The day is marked by flag-hoisting ceremonies, cultural events, and parades.

#### 3. Victory Day — 16 December

**The Surrender:**
On 16 December 1971, General A.A.K. Niazi, the commander of Pakistani forces in East Pakistan, formally surrendered to the joint Bangladeshi-Indian forces at the **Ramna Race Course** (now Suhrawardy Udyan) in Dhaka. This ended the 9-month Liberation War.

**Who Accepts the Surrender:**
General Jagjit Singh Arora (Indian Army) and General M. A. G. Osmani (Bangladesh Army) accepted the surrender. This day marks the physical end of the war.

**Observance:**
Victory Day is one of the most important national holidays. The day features:
- Military parades and air shows
- Cultural programmes
- War veterans' ceremonies
- The **Muktijuddho Mela** (Liberation War Fair) is held annually near Victory Day

#### 4. National Mourning Day — 15 August

**The Assassination:**
On 15 August 1975, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman and most of his family were assassinated in a military coup at his personal residence (Tonany). Those killed included:
- Sheikh Mujibur Rahman
- His wife, **Fazilatunnesa Mujib**
- His sons: **Sheikh Kamal**, **Sheikh Jamal**, **Sheikh Russell**
- Several other family members and staff

- Two daughters — **Sheikh Hasina** and **Sheikh Rehana** — survived because they were abroad in Germany and the UK at the time.

**Observance:**
15 August is observed as National Mourning Day. The national flag is flown at half-mast. The day includes memorial ceremonies, discussion programmes, and Quran recitations. The **Assassination Day of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman** is a day of national mourning.

#### 5. Pohela Boishakh — 14 April

**Bengali New Year:**
Pohela Boishakh marks the first day of the Bengali calendar year. It falls on 14 April each year (13 April in leap years). It is the most important secular festival in Bangladesh:

**Traditions:**
- **Mangal Shobhajatra:** The iconic colourful procession organised by Dhaka University's Faculty of Fine Arts — depicts flora, fauna, and cultural themes. This tradition has UNESCO intangible cultural heritage status (2016).
- People wear new clothes (Pohela Boishakh shopping is a major commercial event)
- Traditional foods: **Pitha** (rice cakes), **Hilsha fish**, sweets
- Rabindra Sangeet and Nazrul Sangeet are performed
- **Bhashan Char** (poetry recitation) sessions

**UNESCO Recognition:**
Dhaka University's **Mangal Shobhajatra** was inscribed on UNESCO's Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity in 2016.

#### 6. 7 March — National Revolutionary Day

**Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's Historic Speech:**
On 7 March 1971, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman delivered a historic speech at the Race Course Ground (now Suhrawardy Udyan) in Dhaka. In the speech, he announced a non-cooperation movement against the Pakistani government and effectively mobilised the masses for the independence struggle.

**Key Lines from the Speech:**
- *"The struggle this time is a struggle for our emancipation..."*
- He called for non-payment of taxes and to prepare for the coming war

**UNESCO Recognition:**
On 11 October 2017, **"Mujibnagar Government and the 7 March Speech"** were inscribed on UNESCO's Memory of the World Register.

#### 7. Other Significant Observances

**Bangladesh's Other National Days:**
- **12 May (National Day of Solidarity with Palestine):** Bangladesh has observed this day in support of the Palestinian cause
- **June 6 (Day of Great Urban Uprising):** Marks the 1962 student movement against Ayub Khan's Education Commission
- **December 1 (World AIDS Day):** Observed nationally
- **National Tourism Day:** Observed in January

**International Days Observed in Bangladesh:**
- **International Women's Day (8 March):** Bangladesh has achieved significant progress in women's empowerment and observes this day
- **International Day for the Preservation of the Ozone Layer (16 September):** Relevant given Bangladesh's climate vulnerability
- **World Environment Day (5 June):** Bangladesh is highly active in this observance

#### 8. The Mujib Year (2020–2021)

**Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's Birth Centenary:**
The period from 17 March 2020 to 17 March 2021 was celebrated as **Mujib Year** (মুজিববর্ষ), marking 100 years since the birth of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman. The celebrations were inaugurated by Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina.

**Key Events During Mujib Year:**
- Massive tree-planting campaign: 100 million trees planted
- Publication of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's complete works
- International conferences on Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's political philosophy
- The **Mujib Memorial Lecture** series at the University of Dhaka

#### 9. International Mother Language Day — 21 February (Global)

**Why It Matters Beyond Bangladesh:**
UNESCO declared 21 February as International Mother Language Day in 1999, following a proposal by Bangladesh. The proposal was initiated by Rafiq Ahmed (brother of Language Martyr Shahidullah). This is Bangladesh's most significant cultural export to the world.

**Global Observance:**
International Mother Language Day is now observed in over 100 countries. It promotes linguistic diversity and multilingual education worldwide.

> **Exam Watch:** The DU exam frequently tests knowledge of Bangladesh's national days. Key pairs to remember: 21 February = Language Martyrs' Day + International Mother Language Day (UNESCO 1999); 26 March = Independence Day; 16 December = Victory Day; 15 August = National Mourning Day. Also know that 7 March 2021 was inscribed on UNESCO's Memory of the World Register.
