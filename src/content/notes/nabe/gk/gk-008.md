### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)
> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

## Seven Continents — Overview

### Asia
- **Largest continent** by area and population (4.7 billion people)
- **Highest point:** Mount Everest (8,849 m) — Nepal/Tibet border
- **Longest rivers:** Yangtze (China), Yellow River (China), Mekong
- **Notable features:** Himalayas, Gobi Desert, Arabian Desert, Dead Sea (lowest point on Earth at -430m)
- **Fastest growing economies:** China, India, Southeast Asia

### Africa
- **Second largest continent** by area
- **Longest river:** Nile (6,650 km) — flows north through Egypt to the Mediterranean
- **Largest desert:** Sahara Desert (~9M km²) — world's largest hot desert
- **Highest point:** Kilimanjaro (5,895 m) — Tanzania
- **Congo River** (Africa's second longest) — flows through the Congo rainforest

### Europe
- **Smallest continent** by area (after Australia)
- **Longest river:** Volga (3,530 km) — Russia, flows to the Caspian Sea
- **Highest point:** Mount Elbrus (5,642 m) — Russia (Caucasus)
- **Notable features:** Alps, Scandinavian Peninsula, Mediterranean coast

### North America
- **Largest country by area:** Canada (9.98M km²)
- **Longest river:** Missouri River (tributary of Mississippi) — 3,768 km
- **Notable features:** Rocky Mountains, Great Lakes (Superior, Michigan, Huron, Erie, Ontario), Mississippi River

### South America
- **Largest country:** Brazil (8.5M km²)
- **Longest river:** Amazon (6,400 km) — carries ~20% of world's river flow; largest river by discharge
- **Highest point:** Aconcagua (6,961 m) — Argentina
- **Notable features:** Andes Mountains (longest continental mountain range), Amazon rainforest (largest rainforest)

### Australia & Oceania
- **Largest country:** Australia (7.7M km²)
- **Smallest continent by area**
- **Notable features:** Great Barrier Reef (coral), Outback, Murray-Darling Basin

### Antarctica
- **Governed by Antarctic Treaty (1959):** No national sovereignty claims
- **Highest point:** Vinson Massif (4,892 m)
- No permanent population; research stations only

## Major Oceans

**Pacific Ocean:**
- Largest ocean on Earth; ~165 million km²
- Separates Asia/Australia from the Americas
- **Mariana Trench** — deepest point (Challenger Deep, 10,994 m)
- **Ring of Fire** — volcanic arc around the Pacific

**Atlantic Ocean:**
- Second largest; ~106 million km²
- Separates Europe/Africa from North/South America
- **Mid-Atlantic Ridge** — tectonic plate boundary running north-south

**Indian Ocean:**
- Third largest; ~70 million km²
- Bordered by Africa, Asia, Australia
- **Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, Red Sea** — notable sub-regions

**Arctic Ocean:**
- Smallest; ~14 million km²
- Arctic ice cap; surrounded by Russia, Canada, Greenland, Alaska
- **North Pole** is located here

## Important Straits and Canals

### Suez Canal (Egypt)
- **Opened:** November 17, 1869
- **Length:** ~193 km; no locks (sea-level canal)
- **Significance:** Connects the Mediterranean Sea to the Red Sea
- **Reduces journey:** Ships avoid routing around Africa (Cape of Good Hope), saving ~8,000 km
- **Owned by:** Egypt (since 1956, when Nasser nationalized it)
- **Traffic:** ~12% of global trade passes through; ~19,000 vessels per year
- **Recent expansion (2015):** New parallel channel expanded capacity

### Panama Canal (Panama)
- **Opened:** August 15, 1914 (American construction from 1904–1914)
- **Length:** ~82 km
- **Key feature:** System of **locks** (Gatun, Pedro Miguel, Miraflores) to raise/lower ships across the Isthmus of Panama
- **Significance:** Connects Atlantic (Caribbean Sea) to Pacific Ocean
- **Saves:** ~12,000 km route between New York and San Francisco
- **Expansion (2016):** New Panamax locks allow larger vessels (~14,000 TEU container ships)
- **Administered by:** Panama Canal Authority (ACP) since December 31, 1999

### Strait of Hormuz
- Connects the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman
- **Width:** ~39 km at its narrowest
- **Strategic importance:** ~21 million barrels of oil per day flow through (roughly one-third of global seaborne oil trade)
- **Claimed by:** Iran (northern half) and Oman/UAE (southern half)

### Strait of Malacca
- Connects the Indian Ocean to the South China Sea/Pacific
- **Width:** ~2,800 km long; ~2.8 km wide at its narrowest (Singapore Strait)
- **Strategic importance:** One of the world's busiest shipping lanes; ~16% of global trade
- **Countries:** Malaysia, Indonesia, Singapore

### Strait of Gibraltar
- Connects the Atlantic Ocean to the Mediterranean Sea
- **Width:** ~13 km at its narrowest
- **Strategic importance:** Controls access to the Mediterranean for Atlantic powers
- **Countries:** Spain, Morocco, Gibraltar (British territory)

## Major Mountain Ranges

**The Himalayas:**
- Length: ~2,500 km; Width: ~250–300 km
- Contains: Mount Everest (8,849 m), K2 (8,611 m — actually in Karakoram), Kanchenjunga (8,586 m)
- Sub-ranges: Karakoram, Hindu Kush, Pamir Knot
- Formed by the collision of the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates (~50 million years ago)

**The Alps:**
- Length: ~1,200 km (France, Switzerland, Italy, Austria, Slovenia)
- Mont Blanc (4,810 m) — highest in Western Europe
- Glaciers: Matterhorn, Alpine lakes (Como, Geneva, Zurich)

**The Andes:**
- Length: ~7,000 km — longest continental mountain range
- Runs along the western edge of South America
- Aconcagua (6,961 m) — highest peak outside the Himalayas
- Active volcanoes: Cotopaxi, Chimborazo

**The Rocky Mountains:**
- Length: ~4,800 km (USA, Canada)
- Highest: Mount Elbert (4,401 m, Colorado)

**The Ural Mountains:**
- Traditionally considered the boundary between Europe and Asia
- Length: ~2,500 km; run north-south through Russia

## Lines of Latitude and Longitude — Key Reference Points

- **Equator (0°):** Divides North and South hemispheres; passes through Ecuador, Colombia, Brazil, Congo, Kenya, Indonesia
- **Tropic of Cancer (23.5°N):** Northern limit of the Sun's direct rays; passes through Mexico, Sahara, India, China
- **Tropic of Capricorn (23.5°S):** Southern limit; passes through Chile, South Africa, Australia
- **Arctic Circle (66.5°N):** Southern limit of polar day/night
- **Antarctic Circle (66.5°S):** Northern limit of Antarctic polar phenomena
- **Prime Meridian (0°):** Passes through Greenwich, London, UK

## Geographical Facts for NABE

- **Caucasus vs. Carpathians:** The Caucasus separates the Black Sea from the Caspian Sea; the Carpathians are in Eastern Europe
- **Dead Sea:** Salt lake between Jordan, Israel, Palestine; lowest point on Earth's surface at ~-430m; salinity 9.6× greater than the ocean
- **Caspian Sea:** Actually the world's largest lake (not a sea); contains ~18% of world's lake water; bordered by Russia, Kazakhstan, Turkmenistan, Iran, Azerbaijan
- **Bering Strait:** Separates Asia (Russia) from North America (Alaska); 88 km wide at its narrowest; was a land bridge during Ice Age (Beringia)
- **Haiti and Hispaniola:** Hispaniola island is shared by Haiti (west) and Dominican Republic (east)
- **Luzon:** Largest island in the Philippines; contains Manila
