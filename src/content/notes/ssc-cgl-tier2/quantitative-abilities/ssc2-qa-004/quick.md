## Profit, Loss & Discount — Quick Reference

### Key Rule / Formula
Profit % = (SP − CP)/CP × 100. Loss % = (CP − SP)/CP × 100. Discount % = (Marked Price − Selling Price)/Marked Price × 100.

### Memory Trick
**SP = CP × (1 + Profit%)** or **SP = CP × (100 + Gain) / 100**. For loss: **SP = CP × (100 − Loss) / 100**.

### 1-Sentence Summary
SSC tests the relationship between cost price, selling price, marked price, and discount — always identify which two you're comparing before calculating.

### Quick Example
Q: An article is sold for ₹360 at a loss of 10%. Find the cost price.
A: CP = 360 / 0.90 = **₹400** — SP = 90% of CP, so CP = SP / 0.90.
