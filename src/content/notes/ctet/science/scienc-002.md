# Topic 2: Nutrition in Plants and Animals

## Modes of Nutrition

### Autotrophic Nutrition
- Organisms **make their own food** using simple inorganic substances
- **Photoautotrophs** use sunlight (green plants, algae)
- **Chemoautotrophs** use chemical energy (certain bacteria)
- Raw materials: **CO₂ + H₂O** → Glucose + O₂ (via photosynthesis)

### Heterotrophic Nutrition
- Organisms **cannot make their own food** — depend on other organisms
- Types: **Holozoid** (whole food), **Saprotroph** (dead/decaying matter), **Parasitic** (living on host)
- All animals, fungi, and non-green plants are heterotrophs

### Symbiotic Nutrition
- Two organisms live **together in a close, permanent relationship**
- **Rhizobium** (bacteria) in legume root nodules: fixes atmospheric N₂ → plant gets amino acids; bacteria gets carbohydrates
- **Mycorrhizae** (fungi + plant roots): fungi helps plant absorb water/minerals
- **Lichens** (fungus + algae): alga photosynthesises, fungus provides protection

## Photosynthesis

**Definition:** The process by which green plants make glucose using **sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water**, producing oxygen as a by-product.

### The Equation
```
CO₂ + H₂O  ──(sunlight/chlorophyll)──>  C₆H₁₂O₆ + O₂
Carbon dioxide + Water                 Glucose + Oxygen
```

### Two Stages of Photosynthesis

**Stage 1: Light Reaction (in thylakoid membranes)**
- Chlorophyll **absorbs light energy**
- Water is split: **2H₂O → O₂ + 4H⁺ + 4e⁻**
- ATP is produced (photophosphorylation)
- NADPH is produced (reduced NADP)
- Oxygen is released as a **by-product**
- Occurs only in **presence of light**

**Stage 2: Dark Reaction / Calvin Cycle (in stroma)**
- Does NOT require light directly (can occur in dark)
- Uses ATP and NADPH from light reaction
- **CO₂ is fixed** into organic molecules (3-carbon compound: 3-PGA)
- Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆) is finally synthesised
- Occurs in the **stroma** of chloroplasts

### Factors Affecting Photosynthesis
1. **Light intensity** – More light → more photosynthesis (up to a point)
2. **CO₂ concentration** – Higher CO₂ increases photosynthesis
3. **Temperature** – Optimal 25–35°C; enzymes denature outside this range
4. **Water** – Essential raw material; deficiency reduces yield
5. **Chlorophyll** – Required pigment; lack of it (e.g., variegated leaves) reduces photosynthesis

> **CTET Question Pattern:** "Which gas is released during photosynthesis?" → **Oxygen (O₂)**
> "Which gas is used during photosynthesis?" → **Carbon dioxide (CO₂)**

## Human Digestive System

The human digestive system breaks down food into **absorbable units**:

### Full Pathway (Mouth to Anus)

1. **Buccal cavity / Mouth**
   - **Mechanical digestion:** Chewing (mastication) breaks food into small pieces
   - **Salivary amylase** (in saliva) begins starch digestion
   - Tongue helps in mixing and swallowing (deglutition)

2. **Oesophagus**
   - 25 cm long muscular tube
   - **Peristalsis** (wave-like contractions) moves food to stomach
   - No digestion occurs here

3. **Stomach**
   - J-shaped sac; capacity ~1–1.5 litres
   - **HCl** (hydrochloric acid): kills bacteria, creates acidic medium
   - **Pepsin:** digests proteins → peptides
   - **Mucus:** protects stomach wall from HCl
   - Food stays 2–4 hours, becomes **chyme**

4. **Small Intestine** (~6 metres long)
   - **Duodenum** (first 25 cm): bile (from liver) emulsifies fats; pancreatic enzymes act here
   - **Jejunum + Ileum:** absorption of digested nutrients
   - Villi and microvilli increase surface area 600× for absorption
   - Digested nutrients enter **blood capillaries** (carbohydrates, proteins) and **lacteals** (fats)

5. **Large Intestine / Colon**
   - Water and mineral salt absorption
   - No digestive enzymes — only bacterial action
   - Faeces stored in rectum before elimination

### Associated Glands

| Gland | Secretion | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Salivary glands | Saliva (amylase) | Starch → Maltose |
| Liver | Bile | Emulsifies fats (物理 breakdown) |
| Pancreas | Pancreatic juice (amylase, lipase, trypsin) | Digests starch, fats, proteins |
| Intestinal glands | Intestinal juice (maltase, peptidase) | Completes digestion |

### Products of Digestion

| Nutrient | Digested Product | Absorption Site |
|---|---|---|
| Carbohydrates | Glucose, fructose | Small intestine |
| Proteins | Amino acids | Small intestine |
| Fats | Fatty acids + Glycerol | Small intestine (lacteals) |
| Vitamins | Directly absorbed | Small intestine |
| Minerals | Directly absorbed | Small intestine |

## Nutrition in Plants

- Plants are **autotrophs** — they synthesise their own food via photosynthesis
- They also need **mineral nutrients** from the soil (nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, etc.)
- **Root hairs** absorb water and minerals from the soil
- **Transportation:** Xylem carries water/minerals (root → shoot); Phloem carries sugars (leaf → all parts)

## Minerals and Fertilizers

### Essential Mineral Nutrients for Plants

| Element | Role | Deficiency Symptom |
|---|---|---|
| **Nitrogen (N)** | Leaf growth, chlorophyll | Yellowing of leaves (chlorosis) |
| **Phosphorus (P)** | Root development, flowering | Stunted growth, purple leaves |
| **Potassium (K)** | Overall plant health, enzyme activation | Weak stems, leaf edge burn |
| **Iron (Fe)** | Chlorophyll synthesis | Interveinal chlorosis (young leaves) |
| **Magnesium (Mg)** | Central atom in chlorophyll | Chlorosis in older leaves |

### Fertilizers
- **Organic fertilizers:** Farmyard manure, compost, vermicompost
- **Synthetic/NPK fertilizers:** Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), Potassium (K) compounds
- **Biofertilizers:** Rhizobium (nitrogen fixation), Azolla (water fern)
- Excessive use of chemical fertilizers causes **water pollution** (eutrophication) and soil degradation

### ⚡ CTET Exam Tips

- Photosynthesis occurs in the **chloroplast** — specifically in the **grana** (light reaction) and **stroma** (dark reaction)
- Stomata are the **pores** on leaf surfaces through which CO₂ enters and O₂ exits
- The liver produces **bile** which **emulsifies** (not digests) fats — a common confusion point
- **Villi** in the small intestine are the primary absorption sites — questions about their function are frequent
- **Root cap** protects the growing tip of the root; **root hairs** increase surface area for water absorption
- Remember: **HCl in the stomach** has two functions — kills bacteria AND activates pepsinogen → pepsin
