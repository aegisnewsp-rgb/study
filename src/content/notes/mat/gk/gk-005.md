#### Indian Space Programme — ISRO

**Organisational Structure:**
- Department of Space (DoS) under PM; ISRO is its main arm
- Chairman: S. Somanath (as of 2024)
- Headquarters: Bengaluru
- Major facilities: Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre (Thiruvananthapuram), U R Rao Satellite Centre (Bengaluru), Satish Dhawan Space Centre (Sriharikota), ISRO Telemetry Tracking and Command Network (ISTRAC, Bengaluru)

**Major Satellite Series:**
- *IRS (Indian Remote Sensing):* Land and water resource monitoring; Cartosat series (high-resolution imaging); RISAT (radar imaging)
- *INSAT (Indian National Satellite System):* Communication, broadcasting, meteorology; joint civilian-military use
- *GSAT (Geosynchronous Satellite):* Communication; some built with foreign cooperation
- *METS (Megha-Tropiques):* Joint India-France; atmospheric research

**Key ISRO Missions:**

*Chandrayaan-1 (2008):* First Indian lunar probe; discovered water molecules on Moon; 11 instruments; operated for 312 days

*Chandrayaan-2 (2019):* Included orbiter, lander (Vikram), rover (Pragyan); lander crashed near lunar surface but orbiter is functional and continues to relay data

*Chandrayaan-3 (2023):* Successfully soft-landed on Moon (23 August 2023) near southern polar region; India became 4th country to soft-land on Moon and first to land near south pole; Pragyan rover confirmed presence of sulphur, oxygen on lunar surface

*Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan, 2013):* India's first interplanetary mission; launched by PSLV; entered Mars orbit on 24 September 2014 (first attempt success); cost ~₹450 crore; India became first Asian country to reach Mars orbit

*Aditya-L1 (2023):* First Indian solar mission; placed at L1 point (1.5 million km from Earth); studies Sun's corona, solar wind, solar flares; 7 payloads

*Gaganyaan (planned):* Indian human spaceflight programme; target 2025 for first uncrewed flight; will make India 4th nation to send human to space after USSR/Russia, USA, China

**Launch Vehicles:**
- PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle): Most reliable; used for Chandrayaan-1, Mars Orbiter, multi-satellite missions; 4 stages; called "workhorse"
- GSLV Mk III (Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle): Heavy-lift; used for communication satellites; LVM3
- Small Satellite Launch Vehicle (SSLV): New; designed for small satellite market; rapid turnaround

#### Missile and Defence Technology

**DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation):**
- Founded 1958; headquartered in New Delhi
- Chairman: G. Satheesh Reddy (as of recent years)
- Key achievements: Missiles, radars, electronic warfare, missiles, armaments

**Missile Systems — Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP):**

*Prithvi:* Short-range surface-to-surface; 150–300 km; liquid fuel; tactical battlefield missile; developed by DRDO; inducted into Indian Army

*Agni-I:* Short-range ballistic missile; 700–1,200 km range; solid fuel; can be launched from road mobile launcher; India-specific requirements

*Agni-II:* Medium-range; 2,000–2,500 km; nuclear capable; 2-stage solid propulsion

*Agni-III:* Intermediate-range; 3,000–4,000 km; submarine launched variant developed

*Agni-IV:* 3,000–4,000 km; improved accuracy; canisterised (can be launched from underground silo or road)

*Agni-V:* Intercontinental; 5,000–8,000 km; enables reaching targets in Asia-Pacific; 3-stage solid; canisterised; MIRV capable (multiple independently targetable reentry vehicles)

*BrahMos:* Supersonic cruise missile; Mach 2.8; range 290 km (extended to 400+ km); joint venture with Russia (NPOM, formerly NPO Mashinostroyeniya); can be launched from land, sea, submarine, aircraft; fastest cruise missile in world in its class

**Other Important Missiles:**
- Nag: Anti-tank missile; third generation; fire and forget; developed by DRDO
- Helina: Air-launched version of Nag
- Shaurya: Short-range surface-to-surface; can be stored in canisters; hypersonic
- Prahaar: Short-range surface-to-air; 150 km range; designed to supplement Pinaka multi-barrel rocket system
- Pinaka Mk-II: Enhanced multi-barrel rocket launcher; 90 km range; replacing older BM-21 Grad

#### Nuclear Programme

**India's Nuclear Doctrine (2003):**
- No first use (NFU): India will only use nuclear weapons in response to a nuclear attack on Indian territory or forces
- Massive retaliation: Credible minimum deterrence
- Civilian control: Nuclear weapons under civilian leadership
- Non-proliferation: Supporting global nuclear disarmament

**Nuclear Installations:**
- Nuclear Power Corporation of India (NPCIL): Operates nuclear power plants
- Boron: Rajasthan, Gujarat, Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh, Karnataka
- Bhilai (Chhattisgarh), Kudankulam (Tamil Nadu — Russia's VVER reactors)
- Fast Breeder Test Reactor (FBTR): Kalpakkam (Tamil Nadu); India's only fast breeder reactor

**Nuclear-capable delivery systems:** Agni, Prithvi, Shaurya, Sukhoi Su-30MKI (airdrop capability)

#### Information Technology

**India's IT Story:**
- Bangalore: "Silicon Valley of India"; home to major tech MNCs and Indian IT giants (Wipro, Infosys, TCS)
- Hyderabad: IT hub; major companies; also pharma hub
- Chennai: Emerging hub; software parks; automotive manufacturing
- Pune: Growing hub; startups, IT, manufacturing

**Major Indian IT Companies:** Tata Consultancy Services (TCS), Infosys, Wipro, HCL Technologies, Tech Mahindra, LTIMindtree, Persistent Systems

**Government Technology Initiatives:**
- Digital India: Aims to transform India into a digitally empowered society; key pillars: Broadband highways, Universal access to phones, e-Governance, Public internet access programme
- Common Service Centres (CSCs): Village-level access points for government and commercial services
- Aadhaar: Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI); world's largest biometric ID system; 1.4 billion+ enrollments; enables direct benefit transfers
- UPI (Unified Payments Interface): Real-time payment system; developed by NPCI; globally recognised; enables instant money transfers; Bhim app as interface

**Start-up Ecosystem:**
- Unicorns: India has 100+ unicorns (startups valued at $1 billion+); major sectors: E-commerce, fintech, edtech, healthtech
- CoWin: Digital platform for COVID-19 vaccination; globally praised for scale
