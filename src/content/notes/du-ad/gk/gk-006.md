# Science and Technology Achievements of Bangladesh

Bangladesh may be a developing country, but it has achieved remarkable milestones in science and technology that far exceed what its GDP per capita would suggest. From pioneering satellite launches to breakthroughs in medical research and a growing ICT sector, Bangladesh has emerged as an unexpected innovator in South Asia. These achievements are frequently tested in the DU Admission examination.

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)

> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

#### 1. The Bangabandhu-1 Satellite

Bangladesh's entry into the space age was a landmark achievement:

**Launch Details:**
- Named after Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman
- Launched on **12 December 2018** aboard a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket from Cape Canaveral, Florida
- Manufactured by Thales Alenia Space (France) and Orbital ATK (USA)
- Positioned at 119.1° East longitude
- It is a **geostationary communications satellite** operating from this orbital slot

**Capabilities:**
- Broadcast television and radio services across Bangladesh and surrounding regions
- Provides internet connectivity to remote areas
- Disaster management and emergency communications
- Estimated cost: approximately $295 million

**Significance:**
- Made Bangladesh the 57th country in the world to own a satellite
- First SAARC country to have its own satellite (India had INSAT series, Nepal and Sri Lanka relied on others)
- Reduces dependency on foreign satellite operators for broadcasting and communications
- A source of national pride — projected Bangladesh's emergence as a "space nation"

#### 2. Digital Bangladesh and the ICT Revolution

The "Digital Bangladesh" initiative was launched in 2009 and transformed the country's technology landscape:

**Key Components:**
- **A2i (Access to Information):** Won the UN Public Service Award in 2013 for its digital service delivery platform. A2i established Union Digital Centres (UDCs) in every union parishad, providing government services online.

- **Sheikh Hasina's Vision:** The government aimed to build a knowledge-based economy by leveraging ICT

- **ICT Parks:** Bangladesh has established ICT parks in Dhaka and Chittagong to incubate technology companies

**Software and BPO Industry:**
- Bangladesh's software and IT services export industry grew from near-zero to over $1 billion annually by 2022
- Key companies: **TigerIT**, **Brain Station 23**, **Relish Technology**, **Niche Telecom**
- Growing reputation in cybersecurity and fintech
- Bangladesh has a large pool of English-speaking, cost-competitive IT professionals

**Mobile Manufacturing:**
Bangladesh has emerged as a significant mobile phone manufacturing hub in South Asia:
- Companies like **Nokia** (via local partners), **Samsung**, and local brands (Symphony, Walton) manufacture phones in Bangladesh
- Bangladesh aims to become a major electronics manufacturing centre

#### 3. Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant

Bangladesh's first nuclear power plant is being constructed at Rooppur in Pabna district:

**Details:**
- Being built with Russian cooperation (Rosatom, Russia's state atomic energy corporation)
- Two VVER-1200 reactors of 1,200 MW each
- Total capacity: 2,400 MW (planned)
- Expected to become operational by 2024–2025

**Significance:**
- Will significantly reduce Bangladesh's dependence on fossil fuels for electricity
- A major milestone in Bangladesh's scientific infrastructure
- Raises Bangladesh's profile in nuclear science and technology
- Bangladesh Atomic Energy Commission (BAEC) is the regulatory body

#### 4. Medical and Scientific Research

**Bangladesh's Medical Achievements:**
Bangladesh has achieved remarkable health outcomes relative to its income level:
- Smallpox eradication was led by Dr. R. A. Gye — a Bangladeshi physician who was the Director of WHO's Smallpox Eradication Programme
- Dr. Firaz Rahman, a Bangladeshi-American scientist, made breakthroughs in cancer research and was nominated for the Nobel Prize (this fact is contested — verify before citing)
- Bangladesh successfully managed cholera outbreaks and developed oral rehydration therapy (ORT) which has saved millions of children's lives globally
- The icddr,b (International Centre for Diarrhoeal Disease Research, Bangladesh) is a world-renowned research institution in Dhaka

**Bangabandhu Science and Technology University:**
Bangladesh has established specialised science and technology universities, including Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman Science and Technology University (BSMRSTU) in Gopalganj.

#### 5. Space Science and the Future

**Bangabandhu-2 Plans:**
Bangladesh is planning a second satellite, Bangabandhu-2, which would further expand its communications and remote sensing capabilities.

**Space Research:**
Bangladesh's space ambitions are coordinated through the Bangladesh Satellite Company Limited (BSCL) and the ICT Division. The government has announced plans to establish a Space Research Centre.

**Remote Sensing:**
Bangladesh's satellite has remote sensing capabilities used for:
- Agricultural crop monitoring
- Disaster management and flood prediction
- Forest cover monitoring (especially the Sundarbans)
- Urban planning

#### 6. Agricultural Science

Bangladesh has achieved extraordinary gains in agricultural productivity through science:

**The Green Revolution in Bangladesh:**
- The adoption of high-yielding varieties (HYV) of rice transformed Bangladesh from a food-deficit to a near self-sufficient nation
- Dr. Muzammal Hoque, a Bangladeshi agricultural scientist, was instrumental in developing Boro rice varieties
- Bangladesh is now largely self-sufficient in rice production (producing over 38 million tonnes annually)

**BRRI Rice Research:**
The Bangladesh Rice Research Institute (BRRI) has developed numerous improved rice varieties:
- **BR-11, BR-22, BR-28, BR-29, BR-34, BR-39, BR-41, BR-42, BR-46, BR-47, BR-49, BR-50, BR-51, BR-52** and others
- These varieties are adapted to different growing conditions (flood, drought, salinity)
- BRRI has developed **"Golden Rice"** (vitamin A-enriched rice) in collaboration with international partners

**Fisheries Research:**
Bangladesh is a world leader in carp fish breeding and cultivation. The **Bangladesh Fisheries Research Institute** has pioneered techniques for breeding indigenous fish species.

#### 7. The ICT Act and Digital Governance

**ICT Act 2006 (amended 2013 and 2023):**
Bangladesh enacted one of South Asia's most comprehensive ICT laws to regulate digital transactions, cybercrime, and electronic governance.

**Digital National ID:**
Bangladesh's national ID system is one of the world's most comprehensive, covering over 100 million citizens and integrated with electoral rolls, banking, and government services.

> **Exam Watch:** The DU exam often asks about Bangabandhu-1 satellite: the launch date (12 December 2018), the launch vehicle (SpaceX Falcon 9), the manufacturer (Thales Alenia Space), and Bangladesh being the first SAARC country to launch its own satellite. Also know that "Digital Bangladesh" was a government initiative launched in 2009, and A2i won a UN Public Service Award.
