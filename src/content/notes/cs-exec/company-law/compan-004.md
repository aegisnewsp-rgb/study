# Share Capital & Debentures

### 🟢 Lite — Quick Review (1h–1d)
> Rapid summary for last-minute revision before your exam.

**Share Capital & Debentures** — Key Facts for CS Executive
Types of share capital: Authorized (maximum in MoA — can be increased), Issued (offered to public), Subscribed (applied and allotted), Paid-up (amount actually received); Called-up (amount called but not yet paid)
Kinds of shares: Equity shares (voting rights, dividend varies), Preference shares (fixed dividend, priority on winding up — cumulative vs non-cumulative, participating vs non-participating)
Issue price: Face value (nominal value, e.g., ₹10) + Share premium (amount above face value credited to Securities Premium Account under Section 52)
Bonus shares: Issued from free reserves (Securities Premium, General Reserve, CRR); requires 200% average profit for last 3 years + no default in filing; ratio decided by shareholders
⚡ Exam tip: Securities Premium can be used ONLY for: writing off preliminary expenses, providing premium on redemption of shares/debentures, buyback of own shares, issuing fully paid bonus shares — not for paying dividends!

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)
> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

**Share Capital & Debentures** — CS Executive Study Guide
Alteration of capital: Company can — increase capital (ordinary resolution), reduce capital (special resolution + NCLT approval), subdivide/consolidate shares (ordinary resolution), convert loans into shares (special resolution)
Buyback of shares (Section 68): Company can buy back its own shares from — existing shareholders on proportionate basis, open market, tender offer; conditions: must have free reserves, can buy back up to 25% of paid-up capital, debt-equity ratio must be ≤2:1 post-buyback, must complete within 12 months
ESOP — Employee Stock Option Plan: Company can grant options to employees to subscribe at future date at predetermined price; vesting period minimum 1 year; per employee limit 5% of paid-up capital; tax implications at exercise
Debentures: Debt instruments — holder is creditor, not owner; types: Convertible (can convert to equity after fixed period, at fixed ratio) vs Non-convertible (pure debt); Secured (backed by assets) vs Unsecured; Redeemable (fixed maturity date) vs Perpetual
Debenture trust: For debenture issues >₹5 crore, a Debenture Trust Deed must be created; Trustee (usually a bank/financial institution) protects debenture holders' interests
Charge registration: Any charge on company assets (debentures, loans) must be registered with RoC within 30 days via CHG-1; failure makes charge void against liquidator and creditors
⚡ Exam tip: If a company allotts shares at a discount, it is void — but a company can issue sweat equity shares (at discount to fair value) to employees under Section 54, subject to shareholder approval and SEBI guidelines!

### 🔴 Extended — Deep Study (3mo+)
> Comprehensive coverage for students on a longer study timeline.

**Share Capital & Debentures** — Comprehensive CS Executive Notes
Further issue of capital: Existing shareholders have right of pre-emption (right to be offered new shares first in proportion to existing holdings) — can be excluded by Special Resolution or AoA provision; rights issue: offer to existing shareholders at fixed price within 15 days of offer
Forfeiture of shares: When shareholder fails to pay call money — board can forfeit shares after proper notice (not less than 14 days); forfeited shares can be reissued at any price; amount originally paid is not refundable; reissue price below face value is allowed (capital loss)
Surrender of shares: Voluntary surrender to company — only permitted if AoA allows; treated similar to forfeiture; board cannot accept surrender except in case of forfeiture
Variation of shareholding rights: If a class of shareholders is adversely affected by variation, they can apply to NCLT under Section 48 within 21 days; NCLT can prohibit variation if it is oppressive to that class
Debenture redemption: Redeemable debentures — typically 7-10 years; can be redeemed at par, premium, or discount; company must maintain Debenture Redemption Reserve (DRR) — for NBFCs, DRR = 100% of debentures; for other companies, DRR = 50% of debentures before redemption
Charge types: Fixed charge (specific asset — e.g., machinery, land), Floating charge (whole class of assets — e.g., stock, receivables — crystallizes on default), Equitable mortgage (pledge of title deeds), Legal mortgage (transfer of interest)
Stock split: Converting one ₹100 share into ten ₹10 shares — increases liquidity, does not change paid-up capital; consolidation: reverse of split; requires ordinary resolution + RoC intimation
Minimum subscription: In a public issue, the minimum amount that must be subscribed before the company can allot shares — typically 90% of offer; if not met within 30 days, money must be refunded within 10 days; company cannot use the money
