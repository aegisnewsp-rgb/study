## Geometry & Mensuration — Quick Reference

### Key Formula/Rule
Area of rectangle = length × breadth; Perimeter = 2(length + breadth); Circle area = πr²

### Memory Trick
**"A-Rect = LB"** — just like the buttons on a remote control! For a circle, remember **"Pie Are Squared"** (πr²) — someone mispronounced it as "pi are square" but it's actually pi × r².

### 1-Sentence Summary
Geometry tests your ability to calculate areas, perimeters, and volumes of 2D and 3D shapes.

### 30-Second Example
Q: Find the area of a circle with radius 7 cm.
A: 154 cm² — π × 7² = 22/7 × 49 = 154
