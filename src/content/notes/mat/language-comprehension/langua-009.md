# Para Completion & Assertion

### 🟢 Lite — Quick Review (1h–1d)
> Rapid summary for last-minute revision before your exam.

Para Completion and Assertion questions test whether you can recognise how a paragraph naturally ends or whether a statement about a passage is valid. MAT includes both para-completion (where does the paragraph go?) and true/false/cannot be determined (assertion-based) questions.

**Key strategies:**
- Para Completion: Identify the topic, tone, and direction of the paragraph; look for logical closure
- Assertion questions: Check if the statement is directly supported, directly contradicted, or unrelated to the passage
- The correct completion should flow naturally from the preceding sentences

**Signs of the right answer:**
- Maintains consistent tone (formal, analytical, persuasive)
- Addresses the same topic throughout
- Provides a sense of conclusion or resolution

**Exam tip:** The correct ending often restates or concludes the main idea introduced in the first sentence, without introducing entirely new information.

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)
> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

#### Para Completion Questions

**Format:** A paragraph with 4-5 sentences, missing the final sentence. You must choose which option best completes it.

**How to approach:**

1. **Read the existing sentences** — identify the topic, the development pattern, and the tone

2. **Identify the paragraph's trajectory** — where is the author heading?
   - Is it building towards a conclusion?
   - Is it introducing a problem and offering a solution?
   - Is it presenting a contrast or contradiction?
   - Is it providing examples to illustrate a point?

3. **Look for discourse markers** that signal completion:
   - Therefore, hence, thus, consequently (logical conclusion)
   - In conclusion, to sum up, finally (summary)
   - Nevertheless, however (contrast)

4. **Eliminate options that:**
   - Introduce new topics not discussed earlier
   - Contradict the direction of the paragraph
   - Are too vague or too specific to fit
   - Repeat exactly what's already said

**Types of paragraph development:**

*Cause-Effect:*
"Studies show that prolonged exposure to screen light before bedtime disrupts sleep patterns. The blue light suppresses melatonin production. Consequently, [COMPLETION]"

Answer should state the effect of disrupted sleep patterns.

*Problem-Solution:*
"Urban congestion has worsened across major Indian cities. Commute times have increased by 30% over the past decade. [COMPLETION]"

Answer should suggest solutions or acknowledge the severity.

*Example-Illustration:*
"Different cultures mark important life transitions differently. In Japan, the Coming of Age Day celebrates young adults turning twenty. In India, many communities observe elaborate ceremonies at different ages. [COMPLETION]"

Answer should draw a conclusion about cultural diversity or the variety of transition markers.

*Comparison-Contrast:*
"Traditional classrooms rely on in-person instruction, while online learning allows flexibility. However, online formats may lack [COMPLETION]"

Answer should highlight limitations of online learning.

#### Assertion-Based Questions

**Format:** A statement followed by a passage. You must determine if the statement is TRUE, FALSE, or if you CANNOT BE DETERMINED from the passage.

**Framework:**
- **TRUE:** The passage directly supports the statement
- **FALSE:** The passage directly contradicts the statement
- **CANNOT BE DETERMINED:** The passage neither supports nor contradicts, or insufficient information is provided

**Common trap:** Choosing "TRUE" when the statement is merely plausible or reasonable but not stated in the passage. If it isn't in the passage, it cannot be determined from the passage.

**Example:**

Passage: "A recent report indicates that 60% of students who used digital learning tools for mathematics showed improved test scores compared to the previous year. However, the report cautions that other variables—such as smaller class sizes and revised curricula—may have contributed to the improvement."

Statement: "Digital learning tools are the only reason for improved test scores."

Answer: FALSE — The passage explicitly states other variables may have contributed.

Statement: "The report recommends replacing traditional textbooks with digital tools."

Answer: CANNOT BE DETERMINED — The passage doesn't mention any recommendation.

Statement: "Most students using digital tools showed improvement."

Answer: TRUE — "60% showed improved test scores" supports this.

#### Specific MAT Question Patterns

**Pattern 1: Author's View Questions**
"The author would most likely agree with which of the following?"
- Identify the author's tone and argument
- Eliminate options that contradict the passage
- Choose the one that best aligns with the overall message

**Pattern 2: Best Title/Heading Questions**
"The most appropriate title for the passage would be:"
- The title should capture the central theme
- Avoid titles that are too narrow (covering only one example) or too broad (covering topics not in passage)

**Pattern 3: Tone/Mood Questions**
"The tone of the passage can be described as:"
- Identify emotional flavour: critical, analytical, neutral, humorous, persuasive
- Watch for double negative constructions

### 🔴 Extended — Deep Study (3mo+)
> Comprehensive coverage for students on a longer study timeline.

#### Advanced Para Completion Techniques

**Understanding discourse coherence:**

Para completion tests your understanding of how ideas connect. The coherence devices that connect sentences include:

- **Reference:** Pronouns, demonstratives (this, that) pointing back to previous content
- **Ellipsis:** Omitting repeated words (e.g., "The blue team won, the red team didn't")
- **Lexical cohesion:** Repeating key words or using synonyms
- **Discourse markers:** Connectors like however, moreover, therefore

The correct completion will naturally integrate with these coherence devices.

**Working with complex paragraph structures:**

*Multiple idea paragraphs:*
When a paragraph presents several points before concluding:
- "Democracy faces several challenges in the modern era. Voter turnout is declining in established democracies. Polarisation is increasing between political parties. Populist leaders are gaining ground. [COMPLETION]"

The completion should synthesize these challenges into a conclusion about democracy's challenges.

*Cause-effect chains:*
- "The construction of the dam displaced thousands of residents. Their relocation disrupted social networks. Community bonds frayed. [COMPLETION]"

The completion should connect to this chain of effects.

**Evaluating whether the passage has sufficient information:**

Sometimes no option perfectly completes the paragraph because the passage doesn't provide enough information for a natural conclusion. In such cases:
- Choose the option that is most consistent with the paragraph's direction
- Avoid options that introduce new, unrelated topics
- Prefer options that acknowledge the limitation raised in the passage

**Avoiding common traps in para completion:**

1. **The "new topic" trap:** Options that introduce entirely new topics the paragraph hasn't prepared for
2. **The "opposite direction" trap:** Options that contradict the established argument
3. **The "too narrow" trap:** Options that only address one small part instead of the whole
4. **The "too broad" trap:** Options that discuss topics beyond the scope of the paragraph
5. **The "vague filler" trap:** Options that are true statements but don't specifically complete the paragraph

#### Assertion Reasoning — Advanced

**Distinguishing between different levels of support:**

- **Directly stated:** The passage explicitly says it
- **Indirectly supported:** The passage implies it but doesn't state it outright
- **Not addressed:** The passage is silent on this point
- **Contradicted:** The passage says the opposite

For assertion questions, "TRUE" requires direct or strongly implied support. "CANNOT BE DETERMINED" covers anything not directly supported.

**The "most supported" principle:**

When multiple options could be true, choose the one most clearly supported by the passage.

Example: Passage discusses rising smartphone usage among teenagers.
- Option A: All teenagers have smartphones — NOT supported (overgeneralisation)
- Option B: Most teenagers use smartphones frequently — Supported
- Option C: Teenagers prefer smartphones to computers — NOT addressed

Answer: B

**Reasoning by elimination in assertion questions:**

Instead of proving each option true/false, eliminate those that are clearly false or undeterminable:
1. Find options the passage directly contradicts → FALSE
2. Find options the passage clearly supports → TRUE
3. Remaining → CANNOT BE DETERMINED

#### Practice Questions

**Para Completion:**

Passage: "India's startup ecosystem has grown rapidly over the past decade. The government has introduced policies supporting entrepreneurship. Venture capital funding has increased significantly. Major tech companies have set up offices in India. _________."

Options:
(A) Consequently, India's economy is now the fastest growing in the world.
(B) As a result, India has become a hub for innovation and technology.
(C) However, startups continue to face challenges in raising funds.
(D) Meanwhile, traditional industries have struggled to adapt.

Answer: (B) — The passage builds a positive picture of startup growth; (B) naturally concludes this upward trajectory. (A) overstates; (C) contradicts with "however"; (D) introduces new topic.

**Assertion:**

Passage: "Research conducted across 15 countries found that employees who worked from home at least three days per week reported higher job satisfaction. However, these same employees also reported challenges in maintaining work-life boundaries."

Statement 1: Working from home always leads to higher job satisfaction.
Answer: FALSE — The passage says "at least three days" and "reported challenges" showing it's not universal.

Statement 2: Work-from-home policies may have some drawbacks.
Answer: TRUE — Explicitly supported by "challenges in maintaining work-life boundaries."

Statement 3: Employers should mandate five days in-office work.
Answer: CANNOT BE DETERMINED — The passage doesn't make any recommendation.

#### MAT Para Completion Checklist

1. Read all given sentences; identify topic and tone
2. Predict what kind of sentence would logically conclude
3. Use discourse markers to identify the direction (however → contrast; therefore → conclusion)
4. Eliminate options that introduce new topics
5. Eliminate options that contradict the passage direction
6. Choose the option that integrates naturally with existing coherence devices
7. For assertion: direct support = TRUE; direct contradiction = FALSE; everything else = CANNOT BE DETERMINED
