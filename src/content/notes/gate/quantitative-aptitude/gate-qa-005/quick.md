## Simple & Compound Interest — Quick Reference

### Key Formula/Rule
Simple Interest = (P × R × T)/100. Compound Interest subtracts the previous interest from the principal each time.

### Quick Memory Trick
Simple Interest = flat, same amount each year. Compound Interest = interest on interest, grows faster over time. CI > SI when T > 1 year (for positive rate).

### 1-Sentence Summary
Simple interest charges on the original principal only; compound interest charges on accumulated principal (principal + previous interest).

### Quick Example
Q: Rs. 1000 at 10% per annum for 2 years. Find SI and CI.
A: SI = (1000 × 10 × 2)/100 = Rs. 200. CI Year 1: 1000 × 0.10 = 100. Year 2: 1100 × 0.10 = 110. Total CI = Rs. 210.

### Must Remember
- Amount (SI) = P + (P × R × T)/100 = P(1 + RT/100)
- Amount (CI) = P(1 + R/100)^T
- CI is always greater than SI for T > 1 year at same rate
- Convert annual rate to per-period rate for fractional years
