# "Trigonometry: Sine, Cosine and Tangent"

### ūüüĘ Lite ‚ÄĒ Quick Review (1h‚Äď1d)
> Rapid summary for last-minute revision before your WAEC exam.

**Right-Angled Triangle Definitions:**

For angle $\theta$ in a right-angled triangle:
$$\sin\theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$$
$$\cos\theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$$
$$\tan\theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}} = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$$

**SOHCAHTOA:**
- **S**ine = **O**pposite √∑ **H**ypotenuse
- **C**osine = **A**djacent √∑ **H**ypotenuse
- **T**angent = **O**pposite √∑ **A**djacent

**Remember:** $\tan\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$, so $\sin$ and $\cos$ are NEVER zero in the denominator.

**Special Angles:**

| Angle | sin | cos | tan |
|-------|-----|-----|-----|
| 0¬į | 0 | 1 | 0 |
| 30¬į | 1/2 | ‚ąö3/2 | 1/‚ąö3 = ‚ąö3/3 |
| 45¬į | ‚ąö2/2 | ‚ąö2/2 | 1 |
| 60¬į | ‚ąö3/2 | 1/2 | ‚ąö3 |
| 90¬į | 1 | 0 | undefined |

‚ö° **WAEC Tip**: For 30¬į and 60¬į, remember: $\sin 30 = \cos 60 = \frac{1}{2}$, $\cos 30 = \sin 60 = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$. Draw an equilateral triangle split in half to derive these values.

> For students who want genuine understanding.

**Solving Right-Angled Triangle Problems:**

**Worked Example:**
A ladder 5m long leans against a wall making 60¬į with the ground. Find:
1. Height of the wall
2. Distance of ladder foot from wall

Solution:
1. $\sin 60¬į = \frac{h}{5}$
   $h = 5 \times \sin 60¬į = 5 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = \frac{5\sqrt{3}}{2} ‚Čą 4.33\,\text{m}$

2. $\cos 60¬į = \frac{d}{5}$
   $d = 5 \times \cos 60¬į = 5 \times \frac{1}{2} = 2.5\,\text{m}$

**Angles of Elevation and Depression:**
- **Angle of elevation**: Angle measured UP from horizontal
- **Angle of depression**: Angle measured DOWN from horizontal (equal to angle of elevation if looking at same point from opposite direction)

**Worked Example:**
A man 1.8m tall stands 10m from a tree. The angle of elevation to the top is 30¬į. Find tree height.

$\tan 30¬į = \frac{h}{10}$ where $h$ is height above man's eyes
$h = 10 \times \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{10}{\sqrt{3}} ‚Čą 5.77\,\text{m}$
Tree height = $5.77 + 1.8 = 7.57\,\text{m}$

**The Trigonometric Ratios in All Quadrants:**

| Quadrant | Angles | sin | cos | tan |
|----------|--------|-----|-----|-----|
| I | 0¬į to 90¬į | + | + | + |
| II | 90¬į to 180¬į | + | - | - |
| III | 180¬į to 270¬į | - | - | + |
| IV | 270¬į to 360¬į | - | + | - |

**Reference Angles:**
The acute angle made with the nearest x-axis.
- Reference angle for 150¬į = 30¬į (since 180¬į - 150¬į = 30¬į)
- $\sin 150¬į = \sin 30¬į = +\frac{1}{2}$ (QII, sin positive)
- $\cos 150¬į = -\cos 30¬į = -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ (QII, cos negative)

‚ö° **Common Mistake**: Forgetting that $\sin 30¬į = \frac{1}{2}$ but $\sin 150¬į$ is NOT the same. Always check the quadrant for the correct sign.

### ūüĒī Extended ‚ÄĒ Deep Study (3mo+)
> Comprehensive theory for serious exam preparation.

**The Sine Rule:**

$$\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$$

Use when you know:
- Two angles and one side (AAS or ASA)
- Two sides and an angle opposite one of them (SSA)

**Worked Example:**
Find side $a$ if $A = 30¬į$, $B = 45¬į$, and $b = 10\,\text{cm}$.

$\frac{a}{\sin 30¬į} = \frac{10}{\sin 45¬į}$
$a = 10 \times \frac{\sin 30¬į}{\sin 45¬į} = 10 \times \frac{0.5}{0.707} ‚Čą 7.07\,\text{cm}$

**The Cosine Rule:**

$$a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc \cos A$$
$$\cos A = \frac{b^2 + c^2 - a^2}{2bc}$$

Use when you know:
- Two sides and the included angle (SAS)
- All three sides (SSS) ‚ÄĒ find angles

**Worked Example:**
Find angle $A$ if $a = 7$, $b = 5$, $c = 6$.

$\cos A = \frac{5^2 + 6^2 - 7^2}{2 \times 5 \times 6} = \frac{25 + 36 - 49}{60} = \frac{12}{60} = 0.2$
$A = \cos^{-1}(0.2) ‚Čą 78.5¬į$

**Area of a Triangle:**

$$K = \frac{1}{2}ab \sin C$$

Or using $K = \frac{1}{2}bc \sin A = \frac{1}{2}ca \sin B$

**3D Trigonometry:**

For angles between lines and planes in 3D:
1. Find the angle the line makes with the horizontal (ground)
2. Project the line onto the plane
3. Use right-angled triangle trigonometry

**Worked Example:**
A pole is on a slope inclined at 15¬į to the horizontal. A wire is attached from a point 20m up the slope to the top of the pole (vertical). The wire makes 50¬į with the slope. Find height of pole.

In triangle: wire = 20m, angle at base = 50¬į
$h = 20 \times \sin 50¬į ‚Čą 15.3\,\text{m}$

**Trigonometric Identities:**

1. $\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$
2. $1 + \tan^2\theta = \sec^2\theta$
3. $1 + \cot^2\theta = \csc^2\theta$
4. $\sin 2\theta = 2\sin\theta\cos\theta$
5. $\cos 2\theta = \cos^2\theta - \sin^2\theta = 2\cos^2\theta - 1 = 1 - 2\sin^2\theta$
6. $\tan 2\theta = \frac{2\tan\theta}{1 - \tan^2\theta}$

**Solving Trigonometric Equations:**

Example: Solve $\sin x = \frac{1}{2}$ for $0¬į \leq x \leq 360¬į$

$\sin x = 0.5$ gives $x = 30¬į$ or $x = 180¬į - 30¬į = 150¬į$

Example: Solve $2\cos x - 1 = 0$ for $0¬į \leq x \leq 360¬į$

$2\cos x = 1$, so $\cos x = 0.5$
$x = 60¬į$ or $x = 360¬į - 60¬į = 300¬į$

**Radians:**

$$\pi \text{ radians} = 180¬į$$
$$1 \text{ radian} = \frac{180¬į}{\pi} ‚Čą 57.3¬į$$

Arc length $s = r\theta$ (where $\theta$ in radians)

**Double Angle Formulae Derivation:**
$\sin(A+B) = \sin A \cos B + \cos A \sin B$

For $\sin 2A$: Let $A = B$
$\sin 2A = \sin A \cos A + \cos A \sin A = 2\sin A \cos A$
