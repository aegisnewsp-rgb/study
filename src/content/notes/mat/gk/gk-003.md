# Geography & Environment

> Rapid summary for last-minute revision before your exam.

Geography and Environment form a significant part of MAT's General Knowledge section. Focus on: physical geography (mountains, rivers, deserts), Indian climate and monsoons, biodiversity hotspots, major mineral belts, and current environmental issues. India as a land of geographical diversity is a recurring theme.

**High-yield points:**
- Himalayas: Longitudinal divisions (Himadri, Himachal, Shivalik); passes (Khardung La, Rohtang, Nathu La)

#### Physical Geography of India

**Geological Background:**

India's geological history spans over 4 billion years. The peninsular block (comprising the stable continental crust) is one of the world's oldest landmasses. The Himalayas are young fold mountains (Tertiary period, roughly 25–50 million years ago), created by the collision of the Indian Plate with the Eurasian Plate — still tectonically active, as evidenced by regular earthquakes in the NE region.

**Three major mountain ranges of India:**

*Himalayan Mountains:*
- Greater Himalayas (Himadri): Highest, continuously snow-covered; peaks above 6,000m; includes Mt. Everest (8,849m — Nepal/China border), K2 (Pakistan-administered Kashmir), Kangchenjunga (India-Nepal border)
- Lesser Himalayas (Himachal): Altitude 3,500–4,500m; major hill stations (Shimla, Mussoorie, Nainital)
- Shivalik Hills (Outer Himalayas): Altitude 900–1,500m; youngest range; consists of unconsolidated sediments

*Western Ghats (Sahyadri):*
- Runs along western edge of Deccan Plateau; 1,600 km long
- Altitude: 900–1,800m; continuous chain except at Palakkad Gap (pass connecting Kerala and Tamil Nadu)
- UNESCO World Heritage site (Western Ghats biodiversity hotspot)
- Important peaks: Anamudi (Kerala, highest peak of peninsular India, 2,695m), Doddabetta (Tamil Nadu)

*Eastern Ghats:*
- Discontinuous chain along eastern edge of Deccan
- Lower altitude than Western Ghats; highly eroded
- Important peaks: Mahendragiri (Odisha), Arma Konda (Andhra Pradesh)

#### Major Rivers and Drainage

**Himalayan Rivers (Perennial):**
- *Indus:* Originates in Tibet (Kailash Mansarovar area); enters India via Ladakh; tributaries — Zanskar, Suru, Shyok; total length ~2,900 km
- *Ganga:* Sources of Bhagirathi (Gangotri glacier, Uttarakhand) and Alaknanda (confluence at Devprayag); major tributaries — Yamuna (Tons, Betwa, Ken), Ghaghara, Gandak, Kosi; enters Bengal as Hooghly (tributary) and Padma (main channel)
- *Brahmaputra:* Originates in Tibet (chemayungdung glacier); enters India in Arunachal Pradesh (called Dihang); major tributaries — Dibang, Lohit, Subansiri; joins Ganga in Bangladesh to form the Meghna

**Peninsular Rivers:**

*East-flowing rivers (into Bay of Bengal):*
- Mahanadi: Odisha; Hirakud Dam
- Godavari: Second longest river of peninsular India; originates near Nashik; tributaries — Purna, Indravati, Sabari, Manjira; also called "Dakshina Ganga"
- Krishna: Originates near Mahabaleshwar (Maharashtra); tributaries — Koyna, Tungabhadra, Bhima
- Kaveri (Cauvery): Originates in Karnataka (Brahmagiri range, Coorg); Hogenakkal Falls; hydro-power projects — KRS Dam (Krishnarajasagara)

*West-flowing rivers (into Arabian Sea):*
- Narmada: Largest west-flowing river; origin at Amarkantak (Madhya Pradesh); tributaries — Kolar, Tawa, Sher; flows through marble rocks of Bhedaghat; Sardar Sarovar Project
- Tapti: Parallel to Narmada; tributaries — Purna, Bori, Panzara
- Sabarmati: Flows through Ahmedabad; Sabarmati Ashram connection

#### Climate and Monsoons

**India's Monsoon System:**

India's climate is dominated by the monsoon — seasonal reversal of wind direction caused by differential heating of land and sea. The intertropical convergence zone (ITCZ) plays a critical role.

*Southwest Monsoon (June–September):*
- Originates from the Arabian Sea branch and Bay of Bengal branch
- Arabian Sea branch: Hits Western Ghats — causes orographic rainfall; Western coasts receive very high rainfall (Mawsynram, Cherrapunji — world's wettest places, >11,000 mm annually)
- Bay of Bengal branch: Strikes Northeast; enters Assam; Brahmaputra valley; causes heavy rainfall in Meghalaya; moves westward along Himalayan foothills

*Retreating Monsoon (October–November):*
- Monsoon trough shifts southward
- Northeast monsoon affects Tamil Nadu coast (October–December rains)
- Cyclones form in Bay of Bengal; Odisha and Andhra Pradesh are cyclone-prone

**Köppen Climate Classification for India:**
- Am (Tropical monsoon): West coast south of Goa, parts of Assam
- As/Aw (Tropical savanna): Most of peninsula, much of interior
- BSh/BWh (Semi-arid/Hot desert): Thar Desert, parts of Rajasthan, Gujarat
- Cwg (Monsoon with dry winter): Parts of Odisha, West Bengal
- E (Alpine/Tundra): High Himalayas above snowline

#### Biodiversity and Conservation

**Bio-geographic Zones of India:**
1. Trans-Himalayan (Ladakh, parts of Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand)
2. Himalayan (Jammu & Kashmir to Arunachal Pradesh)
3. Desert (Rajasthan, Gujarat — Thar and Rann)
4. Semi-Arid (Punjab, Haryana, Gujarat)
5. Western Ghats (Sahyadri; Western Karnataka, Goa, Maharashtra, Kerala, Tamil Nadu)
6. Deccan Peninsula (Central India; Satpura, Vindhya ranges)
7. Gangetic Plain ( Haryana, Delhi, UP, Bihar)
8. North-East (Assam, Meghalaya, Nagaland, etc.)
9. Coastal (Western and Eastern coasts; islands)
10. Andaman & Nicobar Islands

**Biodiversity Hotspots — India:**
India has four global biodiversity hotspots (among 36 worldwide):
1. Himalayas (including Trans-Himalaya)
2. Western Ghats and Sri Lanka
3. Sundarbans (Mangroves — Bengal delta)
4. Indo-Burma region (Northeast India, Andaman & Nicobar)

**Protected Areas:**
- 104 National Parks
- 566 Wildlife Sanctuaries
- 18 Biosphere Reserves (11 included in UNESCO World Network)
- Project Tiger: 54 tiger reserves; India has ~3,167 tigers (2023 census)
- Project Elephant: 33 Elephant reserves

**Major National Parks:** Jim Corbett (Uttarakhand, oldest), Kaziranga (Assam, one-horned rhinoceros), Bandipur (Karnataka), Kanha (Madhya Pradesh), Periyar (Kerala), Silent Valley (Kerala), Hemis (Ladakh), Sundarbans (West Bengal)

#### Agriculture and Soil Types

**Major Soil Types of India:**

1. **Alluvial Soil:** Indo-Gangetic Plain (Punjab, Haryana, UP, Bihar, West Bengal); alluvium deposited by rivers; very fertile; supports wheat, rice, sugarcane; two types — Khadar (new, sandy) and Bhangar (old, clayey)

2. **Black Soil (Regur):** Deccan Trap (Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Telangana, Karnataka); volcanic origin; rich in iron, magnesium, calcium; ideal for cotton; self-ploughing due to cracking

3. **Red Soil:** Eastern and southern India (Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Odisha); derived from crystalline rock; deficient in nitrogen, phosphorus, humus; rice, wheat, sugarcane grow here

4. **Laterite Soil:** Western Ghats, Eastern Ghats, Odisha, Assam; red in colour due to iron oxide; heavily leached; supports plantation crops — tea, coffee, rubber, coconut

5. **Mountain Soil:** Himalayan region; immature; mixed with stones; supports forests

6. **Desert Soil:** Rajasthan, Gujarat; sandy; low organic matter; only drought-resistant crops

**Agricultural Seasons of India:**
- **Kharif (June–October):** Monsoon crops — rice, cotton, jute, maize, groundnut, soybean
- **Rabi (October–March):** Winter crops — wheat, barley, mustard, peas, chickpeas
- **Zaid (March–June):** Summer crops — watermelon, cucumber, fodder crops

#### Mineral Resources

**Major Mineral Belts:**

1. **Chota Nagpur Plateau (Eastern India):**
   - States: Jharkhand, West Bengal, Odisha, Chhattisgarh
   - Minerals: Iron ore (Hazaribagh, Singhbhum — one of the richest in world), mica (Koderma, Gaya), copper (Singbhum), coal (Jharia, Raniganj), bauxite (Odisha)

2. **Deccan Plateau:**
   - Minerals: Manganese (Nagpur, Balaghat — Madhya Pradesh), coal (Godavari valley), limestone (Madhya Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh), iron ore (Bellary, Karnataka)

3. **Aravalli Range (Rajasthan):**
   - Minerals: Copper, lead, zinc, marble, sandstone, tungsten (Degana)

4. **Coastal Belt:**
   - Minerals: Beach sands (Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh) — ilmenite, rutile, zircon, monazite

**Distribution of Major Minerals:**

#### Environmental Issues

**Climate Change and India:**
- India is among the top 5 countries in terms of CO2 emissions (but per capita emissions are very low)
- National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC): 8 missions including Jawaharlal Nehru National Solar Mission, National Mission for Enhanced Energy Efficiency
- International commitments: Paris Agreement (2015) — India pledged to reduce emission intensity of GDP by 33–35% by 2030 (from 2005 levels); to achieve 40% cumulative electric power installed capacity from non-fossil sources by 2030

**Air Pollution:**
- Delhi-NCR: Among world's most polluted cities; stubble burning (Punjab, Haryana), vehicular emissions, industrial pollution
- Graded Response Action Plan (GRAP): Staged measures based on AQI
- National Clean Air Programme (NCAP): 2019; aims to reduce PM2.5 and PM10 concentrations in 102 cities

**Water Pollution:**
- Major pollution sources: Industrial effluents, agricultural runoff (pesticides, fertilisers), sewage
- Ganga cleaning: Namami Gange programme (2014); 5 sewage treatment plants under construction
- Marine pollution: Oil spills, plastic waste; 60% of India's coastline affected

**Waste Management:**
- Solid Waste Management Rules (2016): Segregation at source (dry, wet, hazardous, domestic hazardous)
- Plastic waste: Single-use plastics banned in several states; Extended Producer Responsibility under CPCB
- E-waste: India ranks 3rd globally in e-waste generation

#### India's Neighbours and Boundaries

**International Boundaries:**
- India shares 15,106 km land border with 7 countries: Pakistan (3,323 km), China (3,488 km), Nepal (1,751 km), Bhutan (699 km), Bangladesh (4,096 km), Myanmar (1,643 km), Afghanistan (106 km)
- Maritime boundaries: Sri Lanka (Palk Strait), Maldives, Indonesia, Thailand, Myanmar

**Key Boundary Disputes:**
- India-Pakistan: Kashmir region (Line of Control since 1949, after UN Resolution); Siachen Glacier (highest battleground on earth)
- India-China: Aksai Chin (Ladakh — disputed; administered by China); Arunachal Pradesh (claimed by China as "South Tibet")
- India-Bangladesh: Enclaves (complicated border with 162 enclaves; resolved by 2015 Land Boundary Agreement)
- India-Nepal: Open border; Kalapani dispute
