# Histology — Epithelium, Connective Tissue & Organ Systems

### 🟢 Lite — Quick Review (1h–1d)

**Epithelial Tissue — Classification**

**Simple epithelium:**
- **Squamous:** single layer flattened cells; diffusion/permeability ( endothelium, mesothelium); RBCs visible through lining
- **Cuboidal:** cube-shaped cells with central nuclei; secretion/absorption (thyroid follicles, kidney tubules)
- **Columnar:** tall cells with basal nuclei; absorption/secretion (intestinal epithelium with brush border, goblet cells)
- **Ciliated columnar:** respiratory tract, oviduct; coordinated ciliary beating moves mucus/ovum

**Stratified epithelium:**
- **Squamous (keratinized):** skin — surface dead cells filled with keratin
- **Squamous (non-keratinized):** mouth, esophagus, vagina — living surface cells
- **Cuboidal:** sweat gland ducts, ovarian surface epithelium
- **Columnar:** conjunctiva — only in some regions
- **Transitional (urothelium):** urinary tract — cells can change shape (umbrella cells on surface); 4–6 cell layers

**Glands:**
- **Exocrine:** duct system present; merocrine (salivary, pancreas — secreted by exocytosis), holocrine (sebaceous gland — whole cell breaks), apocrine (breast — decapitation secretion)
- **Endocrine:** no ducts; hormones into bloodstream; follicular (thyroid) or clusters (adrenal cortex)
- **Goblet cells:** unicellular exocrine glands; secrete mucin (mucus)
- **Clinical:** Bronchial goblet cell hyperplasia → chronic bronchitis (COPD)

**Connective Tissue Proper**
- **Loose (areolar):** beneath epithelium, wrapping blood vessels; most common; fibroblasts, mast cells, plasma cells
- **Dense regular:** parallel collagen bundles; tendons, ligaments; poor blood supply
- **Dense irregular:** random collagen bundles; dermis, periosteum; better blood supply
- **Elastic:** elastic fibers predominant; ligamentum nuchae, elastic arteries
- **Reticular:** reticular fibers; bone marrow, lymph nodes, liver sinusoids

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)

**Connective Tissue — Cells & Matrix**

**Cells:**
- **Fibroblasts:** most common; produce collagen and ground substance
- **Mast cells:** IgE-mediated anaphylaxis; histamine (vasodilator), heparin (anticoagulant), proteases
- **Plasma cells:** antibody-producing; derived from B lymphocytes; seen in chronic inflammation
- **Macrophages (histiocytes):** phagocytosis; derived from monocytes; antigen presentation
- **Adipocytes:** fat cells; unilocular (white fat) or multilocular (brown fat — many droplets)
- **Mesenchymal stem cells:** undifferentiated; can form fibroblasts, adipocytes, osteoblasts

**Matrix:**
- **Fibers:** collagen (most abundant — tensile strength), elastic (elastin + fibrillin — elastic tissues), reticular (type III collagen — scaffolding)
- **Ground substance:** glycosaminoglycans (GAGs), proteoglycans, glycoproteins (fibronectin, laminin)

**Specialized Connective Tissues:**
- **Bone:** osteoblasts (forming), osteocytes (trapped), osteoclasts (resorbing); inorganic calcium phosphate (hydroxyapatite) + organic matrix
- **Cartilage:** chondrocytes in lacunae; avascular (nutrition from perichondrium diffusion)
  - **Hyaline:** most common; tracheal rings, costal cartilage, fetal skeleton
  - **Elastic:** ear, epiglottis; elastic fibers in matrix
  - **Fibrocartilage:** intervertebral discs, menisci, pubic symphysis; type I collagen predominates
- **Blood:** erythrocytes, leukocytes, platelets, plasma (fibrinogen, albumin, globulins)

**Muscle Tissue**

**Skeletal muscle:**
- Long cylindrical fibers with peripheral nuclei; striated (alternating A and I bands)
- **Motor unit:** single motor neuron + all muscle fibers it innervates; smallest functional unit
- **Neuromuscular junction:** motor end-plate; acetylcholine release → muscle contraction
- **Clinical:** Myasthenia gravis (autoantibodies against ACh receptors → fatigable weakness); Duchenne muscular dystrophy (X-linked, absent dystrophin)

**Cardiac muscle:**
- Branched cells with centrally located nuclei; striated; intercalated discs (gap junctions, desmosomes, fascia adherens)
- **Autorhythmic cells:** pacemaker (SA node), conducting cells
- **Clinical:** Myocardial infarction — cardiomyocyte death → troponin release

**Smooth muscle:**
- Spindle-shaped cells with single central nucleus; non-striated; involuntary
- Found in viscera, blood vessels, iris, ciliary body
- **Clinical:** Leiomyoma (benign smooth muscle tumor of uterus); smooth muscle hypertrophy in hypertension

**Nervous Tissue**

**Neurons:**
- Cell body (soma), dendrites (receive signals), axon (conducts away from cell body)
- **Types:** multipolar (most CNS neurons), bipolar (retina, olfactory), pseudounipolar (dorsal root ganglion)
- **Neuroglia:** astrocytes (BBB formation, support), oligodendrocytes (myelin in CNS), microglia (macrophage), ependymal cells (line ventricles)

**Synapse:**
- Presynaptic terminal (vesicles with neurotransmitter), synaptic cleft, postsynaptic membrane
- **Types:** axodendritic, axosomatic, axoaxonic
- **Clinical:** Alzheimer's disease (amyloid plaques, neurofibrillary tangles — tau protein); Parkinson's (Lewy bodies — alpha-synuclein)

### 🔴 Extended — Deep Study (3mo+)

**Organ Histology**

**Thyroid Gland:**
- **Follicles:** colloid-filled spheres lined by simple cuboidal epithelium; thyroglobulin stored in colloid
- **Parafollicular C cells:** between follicles; calcitonin secretion; derived from neural crest
- **Clinical:** Hashimoto's thyroiditis (autoimmune destruction → hypothyroidism); Graves' disease (TSI antibodies → hyperthyroidism, orbital involvement)

**Adrenal Gland:**
- **Cortex (3 zones, all steroid-producing):** zona glomerulosa (aldosterone — RAAS), zona fasciculata (cortisol — stress response), zona reticularis (androgens)
- **Medulla (chromaffin cells):** catecholamine-secreting; derived from neural crest; innervated by sympathetic preganglionic fibers
- **Clinical:** Pheochromocytoma (medullary tumor — episodic hypertension with headaches, sweating, palpitations); Conn's syndrome (zona glomerulosa adenoma → primary hyperaldosteronism)

**Pancreas:**
- **Exocrine (99%):** serous acini; centroacinar cells; ductal cells secrete bicarbonate-rich fluid
- **Islets of Langerhans (endocrine):** insulin (beta cells — central), glucagon (alpha cells — peripheral), somatostatin (delta cells), pancreatic polypeptide (PP cells)
- **Clinical:** Type 1 DM (autoimmune destruction of beta cells); Type 2 DM (insulin resistance + relative beta cell failure)

**Skin (Integument):**
- **Epidermis (keratinized stratified squamous):** layers: stratum basale (stem cells), spinosum (prickles — desmosomes), granulosum (keratohyalin granules), lucidum (only in thick skin — palms/soles), corneum (dead keratinized cells)
- **Melanocytes:** neural crest origin; in basale; produce melanin; transfer to keratinocytes
- **Dermis:** papillary (areolar + capillaries) + reticular (dense irregular)
- **Appendages:** hair follicles, sebaceous glands, sweat glands (eccrine — most numerous, apocrine — axillae/genitalia), nails
- **Clinical:** Melanoma (melanocyte malignancy — ABCDE criteria); basal cell carcinoma (most common skin cancer, slow-growing); squamous cell carcinoma (UV-induced); psoriasis (epidermal hyperproliferation)

**Lung Histology:**
- **Bronchi:** cartilaginous airways; pseudostratified ciliated columnar with goblet cells
- **Bronchioles:** no cartilage; Clara cells (non-ciliated secretory — protect against toxins)
- **Respiratory bronchioles:** transitional; alveolar openings begin
- **Alveoli:** type I pneumocytes (gas exchange — 95% of surface), type II pneumocytes (surfactant production — prevent atelectasis); alveolar macrophages (dust cells)
- **Blood-air barrier:** alveolar epithelium + fused basal laminae + capillary endothelium (~0.5 μm)
- **Clinical:** ARDS (diffuse alveolar damage — hyaline membranes); emphysema (alveolar destruction); COPD

**GI Tract Histology (General Pattern — Mouth to Anus):**
- **Mucosa:** epithelium (varies), lamina propria (connective tissue + muscularis mucosae)
- **Submucosa:** dense connective tissue; Meissner's plexus; contains submucosal glands ( Brunner's glands in duodenum)
- **Muscularis externa:** inner circular + outer longitudinal; Auerbach's plexus between layers
- **Serosa/adventitia:** serosa (peritoneum-covered) vs adventitia (no peritoneum — retroperitoneal)
- **Clinical:** Hirschsprung's (absent Meissner's and Auerbach's plexuses); chronic gastritis (H. pylori); Barrett's esophagus (intestinal metaplasia of distal esophagus)

**Liver Histology:**
- **Classic lobule:** hepatocytes radiating from central vein; portal triads at corners (portal vein, hepatic artery, bile duct)
- **Portal acinus:** based on blood supply (zone I near portal triads = best oxygenated, zone III near central vein = most susceptible to toxins)
- **Hepatocytes:** polar cells; sinusoidal surface (blood side), basolateral surface (bile canaliculi side)
- **Kupffer cells:** hepatic macrophages in sinusoids (part of RES)
- **Sinusoids:** discontinuous endothelium; fenestrated
- **Clinical:** Cirrhosis (regenerative nodules + fibrosis); portal hypertension (reversal of blood flow → portosystemic shunts); hepatitis

**Kidney Histology:**
- **Cortex:** glomeruli, proximal and distal tubules, collecting ducts
- **Medulla:** renal pyramids; loops of Henle (countercurrent multiplier system)
- **Nephron:** Bowman's capsule → proximal tubule → loop of Henle → distal tubule → collecting duct
- **Juxtaglomerular apparatus:** macula densa (distal tubule cells), juxtaglomerular cells (modified smooth muscle — renin secretion)
- **Clinical:** Glomerulonephritis (hematuria, proteinuria); polycystic kidney disease; renal cell carcinoma (clear cell type — most common)

**Bone Histology:**
- **Compact bone:** Haversian systems (osteons); central Haversian canal with blood vessels, surrounded by concentric lamellae and lacunae with osteocytes, connected by canaliculi
- **Spongy bone:** trabeculae with lamellae, no Haversian systems; red marrow in spaces
- **Bone formation:** intramembranous ossification (flat bones) vs endochondral ossification (long bones — cartilage model first)
- **Clinical:** Osteoporosis (reduced bone mass); osteomyelitis (bone infection); osteosarcoma (most common primary bone malignancy in adolescents)
