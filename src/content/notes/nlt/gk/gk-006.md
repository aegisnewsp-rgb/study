## Chapter: Pakistan's Culture, Heritage and Society

### 1.1 Demographics and Society

#### Ethnic Groups
- Punjabis (~44%), Pashtuns (~15%), Sindhis (~14%)
- Seraikis (~10%), Muhajirs (~8%), Baloch (~4%)

#### Religion
- **97% Muslim**: Sunni majority, Shia minority (~15-20%)
- Hindus (~2%), Christians (~1.5%)

### 1.2 Ancient Heritage

#### Mohenjo-daro (Sindh)
- UNESCO World Heritage (1980)
- Period: 2500-1500 BCE
- Features: Great Bath, grid-pattern streets, drainage

#### Taxila (Punjab)
- UNESCO World Heritage (1980)
- One of world's oldest universities (6th century BCE to 5th century CE)
- Buddhist center under Mauryan, Indo-Greek, Kushan kings

### 1.3 Mughal Heritage

#### Shalimar Gardens (Lahore)
- UNESCO World Heritage (1981)
- Built 1641-42 by Shah Jahan
- 3 terraced gardens with 400+ fountains

#### Badshahi Mosque (Lahore)
- Built 1671-73 by Aurangzeb
- One of largest mosques in world
- Capacity: 100,000 worshippers

### 1.4 Sufi Heritage

#### Major Sufi Figures
- **Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai**: Wrote "Shah Jo Risalo" (Sindhi Sufi poetry)
- **Data Ganj Baksh**: Founded Chishti order in South Asia (Lahore)

#### Sufi Music
- **Qawwali**: Devotional music (Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan — international fame)

### 1.5 Traditional Crafts

- **Phulkari**: Punjabi floral embroidery (shawls, dupattas)
- **Ajrak**: Sindhi block-printed shawls with geometric patterns
- **Truck Art**: Colorfully painted trucks (national tradition)
- **Blue Pottery**: Multan (famous)
- **Sports Goods**: Sialkot (soccer balls, cricket bats)

### 1.6 Folk Traditions

#### Punjabi
- **Bhangra**: Harvest dance
- **Melas**: Harvest festivals

#### Pashtun
- **Pashtunwali**: Honor code (Nang, Badal, Melmastiya)
- **Attan**: Traditional dance
