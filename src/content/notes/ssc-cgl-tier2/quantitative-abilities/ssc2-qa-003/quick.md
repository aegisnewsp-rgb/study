## Percentage & Simple/Compound Interest — Quick Reference

### Key Rule / Formula
Simple Interest: SI = (P × R × T) / 100. Compound Interest: CI = P(1 + R/100)^T − P. Percentage change: New value = Old × (1 ± change%).

### Memory Trick
**CI vs SI over 2 years at same rate:** CI = SI × (1 + R/200). Over 2 years, the extra earned in CI over SI = P × (R/100)².

### 1-Sentence Summary
SSC tests percentage as a multiplier in comparisons, and CI/SI as pure formula recall with word problems involving time, rate, and principal.

### Quick Example
Q: What is 25% of 480 minus 20% of 350?
A: 120 − 70 = **50** — 25% of 480 = 120; 20% of 350 = 70.
