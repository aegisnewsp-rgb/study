# Meetings & Proceedings

### 🟢 Lite — Quick Review (1h–1d)
> Rapid summary for last-minute revision before your exam.

**Meetings & Proceedings** — Key Facts for CS Executive
Types of shareholder meetings: AGM (Annual General Meeting — mandatory within 6 months of year-end, discusses accounts + directors' report), EGM (Extra-Ordinary General Meeting — any urgent matter requiring shareholder approval)
Notice period: AGM — 21 clear days' notice (or shorter if consented by 95% members); EGM — 21 clear days (or 14 days with written resolution consent of 95% of members entitled to vote)
Quorum: General meetings — 5 members personally present for public company, 2 for private; Class meetings — members holding 1/3 of issued shares of that class
Voting thresholds: Ordinary resolutions — simple majority (>50%); Special resolutions — 3/4 (75%) majority; some matters require unanimous consent
⚡ Exam tip: Under Section 100, a company can pass a resolution by circulation (without meeting) if all directors sign it — but this cannot be used for items requiring special resolution unless all members sign!

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)
> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

**Meetings & Proceedings** — CS Executive Study Guide
EGM requisition: 10% or more shareholders can requisition an EGM on any valid business; directors must convene within 21 days; if board fails, requisitioners can convene themselves
Types of resolutions: Ordinary resolution (simple majority, 21-day notice), Special resolution (3/4 majority, 21-day notice + clearly stating特殊性 in notice), Unanimous resolution (all members entitled to vote must agree — rarely used, e.g., charity)
Board meetings: Minimum 4 per year, gap ≤120 days; notice: 7 days (or shorter if consented); quorum: 1/3 of board or 2, whichever is higher; can be held via video conference subject to Secretarial Standards
Postal ballot: For listed companies and companies with >200 members, resolutions requiring shareholder approval must be passed via postal ballot (postal ballot rules under Companies (Management & Administration) Rules, 2014) — includes: alteration of AoA/MoA, buyback, reduction of capital, change in registered office across state, election of directors
Secretarial Standard-1 (SS-1): Governs conduct of general meetings; specifies: notice format, quorum calculation, voting methods, proxies, agenda items, minutes — mandatory for all companies
Proxy: Member can appoint another person to attend and vote on their behalf; proxy form must be deposited 48 hours before meeting; corporate members can appoint authorized representative under Section 113
⚡ Exam tip: A resolution passed at an improperly convened meeting is void — even if all shareholders later ratify it, the defect in convening (wrong notice period, no quorum) cannot be cured retroactively!

### 🔴 Extended — Deep Study (3mo+)
> Comprehensive coverage for students on a longer study timeline.

**Meetings & Proceedings** — Comprehensive CS Executive Notes
 AGM rules under Companies Act 2013: Must be held each calendar year; first AGM within 9 months of first financial year end; subsequent AGM within 6 months of year-end (i.e., within 30 September for March 31 year-end companies); business transacted includes: accounts, director report, auditor appointment, dividend declaration, election of directors, appointment of KMP
Calling of AGM: If company fails to hold AGM, any member can apply to Central Government (NCLT after 2018 amendment) to call the meeting — company is also liable to fine up to ₹50 lakh and officer in default up to ₹5 lakh
Extraordinary General Meeting: Convened by — board on its own, board on requisition by members (≥10% paid-up capital for EGM on ordinary business, ≥10% for special business with NCLT approval), NCLT on petition by any member, central government
Class meetings: Required when company has different classes of shares (e.g., preference shares); class meetings pass resolutions specific to that class — e.g., modification of preference share rights requires 3/4 of that class agreeing
Notice of meeting: Must contain — day, date, time, full address of registered office; for EGM, the specific business to be transacted must be clearly stated; explanatory statement for special business required under Section 102
Chairman's role: Usually the chairman of the board; duties — ensure proper conduct, maintain order, put resolutions to vote, announce results, casting vote in case of tie; chairman has residual authority to adjourn if quorum not met after 30 minutes
Voting rights: One share one vote for equity shares; preference shares get dividend + return of capital before equity on winding up; generally no voting on ordinary business unless dividend is in arrears ≥2 years
Minutes of meetings: Recorded within 15 days of meeting; signed by chairman; kept at registered office; conclusive evidence of proceedings; circulate to all directors within 15 days of meeting
Circulating resolutions: Section 175 — resolution can be passed by circulation if all directors (or members for general meeting) sign the draft; valid without meeting if: not objected by any director within 7 days; cannot be used for items requiring special resolution unless unanimous consent obtained
