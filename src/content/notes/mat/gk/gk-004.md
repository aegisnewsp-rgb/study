#### Economic Planning and Development

**Planning Commission vs NITI Aayog:**

The Planning Commission (1950–2014) was the apex body for economic planning in India. It was dissolved on 14 August 2014 and replaced by NITI Aayog (National Institution for Transforming India) on 1 January 2015.

*NITI Aayog:*
- Role: Facilitates cooperative federalism; provides strategic and technical advice to central and state governments
- Chairperson: Prime Minister
- Vice Chairperson: Appointed by PM (currently Suma Sundar)
- Governing Council: Chief Ministers of all states, Lt. Governors of UTs
- States contribute to policy making (unlike Planning Commission's top-down approach)

**Five-Year Plans:**
- 1st Plan (1951–56): Heavy industry; agricultural expansion; achieved targets; community development projects
- 2nd Plan (1956–61): Industrialisation; socialist pattern of society; import substitution
- 3rd Plan (1961–66): Agricultural development; Green Revolution begins; war with China (1962) disrupted economy
- 4th Plan (1969–74): Growth with stability; anti-poverty programmes
- 5th Plan (1974–79): Garibi Hatao; truncated due to 1977 elections
- 6th Plan (1980–85): Employment generation; technology missions
- 7th Plan (1985–90): Infrastructure development; power projects
- 8th Plan (1992–97): Economic reforms (Liberalisation, Privatisation, Globalisation — LPG); fiscal discipline
- 9th Plan (1997–2002): Human development focus;遭遇 Asian Financial Crisis
- 10th Plan (2002–07): Growth rate target 8%; Millennium Development Goals
- 11th Plan (2007–12): Inclusive growth; faster, more inclusive growth
- 12th Plan (2012–17): Growth target 8%; focus on manufacturing, infrastructure

**Economic Reforms (1991):**

The 1991 economic crisis (Balance of Payment crisis, fiscal deficit, high inflation) led to historic reforms under Finance Minister Dr. Manmohan Singh:

1. *Liberalisation:* Deregulation of industries; delicensing; reduction of import tariffs; free convertibility of rupee on current account
2. *Privatisation:* Disinvestment in PSUs; opening of sectors to private participation (telecom, aviation, insurance)
3. *Globalisation:* Integration with world economy; Fema replaced FEMA; SEBI Act; openness to foreign investment (FDI)

#### Major Economic Indicators

**GDP (Gross Domestic Product):**
- Total monetary value of all goods and services produced within a country's borders in a specific period
- India is world's 5th largest economy (nominal GDP ~$3.7 trillion, 2024); 3rd largest by PPP
- Growth drivers: Services (60%+ of GDP — IT, finance, real estate), Manufacturing (Make in India), Agriculture (~18% but declining share)
- GDP growth rate: ~6–7% in recent years; targeted to be world's 2nd or 3rd largest by 2030

**Inflation:**
- Wholesale Price Index (WPI): Tracks wholesale prices; base revised to 2011-12
- Consumer Price Index (CPI): Tracks retail prices; more relevant for common man
- RBI's inflation target: 4% (±2% tolerance band); uses CPI for monetary policy
- Food inflation: Often volatile; vegetables, pulses, edible oils are high weightage items

**Fiscal Deficit and Debt:**
- Fiscal deficit: Gap between government's total expenditure and receipts excluding borrowings
- Revenue deficit: Gap between revenue expenditure and revenue receipts
- FRBM Act (2003): Target — fiscal deficit to 3% of GDP; modified by 2018 amendment to allow 3.5% during normal times and 2.5% by 2025
- Debt to GDP ratio: India's general government debt ~83% of GDP (high by emerging market standards)

#### Financial Institutions

**Reserve Bank of India (RBI):**
- Established 1 April 1935 under RBI Act, 1934
- Governor: Shaktikanta Das (as of 2024); previous governors: Urjit Patel, Raghuram Rajan, D. Subbarao
- Functions: Monetary policy (Repo Rate, Reverse Repo Rate, MSF), currency management, financial system regulation, foreign exchange management
- Monetary Policy Committee (MPC): 6 members (3 RBI, 3 external); meets 6 times a year; RBI Governor is ex-officio chairperson
- Key rates: Repo (6.5%), Reverse Repo (3.35%), MSF (6.75%) as of recent periods

**World Bank Group:**
- International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD) + International Development Association (IDA)
- Provides loans to developing countries; interest rates subsidised
- India is one of the largest borrowers; major projects: National Highways, rural development, urban infrastructure
- President: Ajay Banga (2023 onwards; previous: David Malpass, Jim Yong Kim, Robert Zoellick)

**International Monetary Fund (IMF):**
- Provides balance of payments support; SDR allocations
- India has ~2.7% share; not a major borrower
- Managing Director: Kristalina Georgieva (2019–present)
- World Economic Outlook (WEO): Semi-annual publication; key reference for global economic forecasts

**Asian Development Bank (ADB):**
- Focus on Asia-Pacific development; India is a major borrower
- Provides loans for infrastructure, energy, urban development
- President: Masatsugu Asakawa (2020–present)

**Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI):**
- Principal financial institution for MSME sector (Micro, Small, Medium Enterprises)
- Operations: Refinancing, direct lending, venture capital, skill development

#### Budget and Taxation

**Union Budget Process:**
1. President addresses Parliament (Financial Statement)
2. Finance Minister presents Budget (usually last week of February; since 2017, Budget presented on 1st February)
3. Railway Budget merged with Union Budget (since 2017–18)
4. General discussion in Lok Sabha
5. Standing Committee examination
6. Voting on Demand for Grants
7. Finance Bill passage
8. Appropriation Bill

**Tax Structure in India:**

*Direct Taxes:*
- Income Tax (Slab rates for individuals; corporate tax 30%/25%/22% for different company categories)
- Capital Gains Tax (short-term: at slab rates; long-term: 20% with indexation)
- Securities Transaction Tax (STT): On equity delivery (0.1%), intraday (0.025%), futures (0.01%)

*Indirect Taxes (post-GST):*
- GST (Goods and Services Tax): 5%, 12%, 18%, 28% slabs; 0% on essential items
- CGST: Central GST (goes to Centre)
- SGST: State GST (goes to State)
- IGST: Integrated GST (for inter-state transactions)
- GST Council: Chairman: Union Finance Minister; Vice Chairman: Chosen from states; 3/4th majority required

#### Macroeconomic Concepts for MAT

**Aggregate Demand and Supply:**
- Aggregate Demand (AD) = C + I + G + (X-M)
  - C = Consumer expenditure; I = Investment; G = Government spending; X-M = Net exports
- AD shifts due to: Monetary policy (interest rates), Fiscal policy (taxation, government spending), Exchange rates, Consumer confidence, Trade policies
- Aggregate Supply (AS): Total output; shaped by technology, resources, policy

**Keynesian vs Monetarist Approaches:**
- Keynesian: Government intervention needed; fiscal policy (spending, taxes) is primary tool
- Monetarist: Money supply is key determinant; monetary policy is primary tool; Milton Friedman's influence
- New Classical: Rational expectations; supply-side policies matter most
- India's approach: Mix of both; RBI handles monetary; government handles fiscal

**Phillips Curve:**
- Inverse relationship between inflation and unemployment in short run
- Trade-off: Lower unemployment → higher inflation and vice versa
- NAIRU: Non-Accelerating Inflation Rate of Unemployment
- In long run: Vertical Phillips Curve — no trade-off

#### Government Schemes and Programmes

**Major Government Schemes (Recent):**

1. **PM-KISAN (2019):** Direct income support of ₹6,000/year to farmer families; 3 equal installments of ₹2,000

2. **PM Jan Dhan Yojana (2014):** Financial inclusion; bank accounts for all; ₹5.6 lakh crore deposited; Guinness World Record for most beneficiaries

3. **Ayushman Bharat (2018):** Health coverage up to ₹5 lakh per family per year for bottom 40% of population; 10 crore families covered; 1.5 lakh Health and Wellness Centres

4. **Digital India (2015):** Broadband highways; digital empowerment; ICT for rural areas; BHIM app (Unified Payments Interface)

5. **Make in India (2014):** 25 sectors identified; defence, automobiles, pharmaceuticals, chemicals, textiles; aim: make India a manufacturing hub; FDI norms liberalised

6. **Startup India (2016):** Tax holidays for 3 years; 80% rebate on patent fees; easier compliance; Fund of Funds

7. **Swachh Bharat (2014):** Rural and urban sanitation; construction of toilets; behavioural change campaign; ODisha declared ODF

8. **PM Awas Yojana (2015):** Urban: Interest subsidy on home loans; Rural: Grant for house construction; target: Housing for All by 2022

9. **UDAN (Regional Connectivity Scheme):** Airports development; connecting unserved airports; 1 crore+ passengers flown at ₹2,500 for 1 hour

10. **National Infrastructure Pipeline (NIP):** ₹111 lakh crore infrastructure investment (2019–2025); roads, railways, ports, airports, energy

#### Banking and Financial Sector Reforms

**Financial Inclusion:**
- Jan Dhan, Jan Suraksha (insurance: Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana, Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana), Atal Pension Yojana (pension for unorganized sector)
- PM Vishwakarma (2023): Credit support for traditional artisans; collateral-free loans up to ₹3 lakh

**Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016:**
- Consolidated all previous insolvency laws
- Corporate insolvency resolution process (CIRP): 330 days including litigation (180 days + 90-day extension + 90 days more)
- IBC helped resolve stressed assets of banks; NPAs declined from peak of 11.5% (2018) to ~3.9% (2023)
- Key cases: Bhushan Steel, Essar Steel, Jet Airways, DHFL

**Non-Performing Assets (NPA):**
- Gross NPA ratio: Gross NPAs / Gross advances
- Recovery through: SARFAESI Act (can attach collateral without court), DRT (Debt Recovery Tribunals), IBC
- Write-offs: Banks writing off loans removes them from balance sheet; doesn't mean amount is recovered

**RBI's Prompt Corrective Action (PCA):**
- Framework for banks with weak financial metrics; triggered when Capital Conservation Buffer falls below required levels
- Banks under PCA face restrictions on dividends, branch expansion, executive compensation

#### International Trade

**WTO (World Trade Organization):**
- Founded 1995 (replaced GATT); 164 members
- India and WTO: India is active member; disputes with US, EU on subsidies, tariffs
- Doha Round: Unresolved; developed vs developing country disagreements on agriculture, intellectual property

**India's Trade Profile:**
- Major exports: Petroleum products, IT services, pharmaceuticals, gems and jewellery, chemicals, textiles
- Major imports: Crude oil, gold, electronics, machinery, coal
- Trade deficit: With China (largest); surplus with USA (largest)
- FTAs (Free Trade Agreements): ASEAN, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, UAE (I-CEPA)
- RCEP: India declined to join (concerns about Chinese goods flooding market)
- BRICS: Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa (now expanded to include Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, UAE)

#### Economic Concepts for Exam

**Key definitions to remember:**
- **GDP Deflator:** Nominal GDP / Real GDP × 100
- **Multiplier effect:** Change in output resulting from autonomous spending change; k = 1/(1-MPC)
- **Crowding out:** Government borrowing reduces private investment (if money supply fixed)
- **Exchange rate regimes:** Fixed (Rupee was pegged to pound; now managed float)
- **Special Drawing Rights (SDR):** IMF's reserve asset; India received SDR allocation in 2021 (₹40,000 crore equivalent)

**Current Economic Affairs to Track:**
- GST collection: Monthly figures (target ₹1.5 lakh crore+)
- WPI and CPI inflation: Monthly data
- RBI MPC decisions: Bi-monthly
- IIP (Index of Industrial Production): Monthly
- PMI (Purchasing Managers' Index): Monthly; Services and Manufacturing
