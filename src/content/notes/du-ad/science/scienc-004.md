## Work, Energy, and Power — Complete Study Notes

### Work

**Definition:** Work is done when a force produces motion in the direction of the force.

**Formula:**
**W = F × d × cosθ**

Where:
- F = Force applied (Newton)
- d = Displacement (meters)
- θ = Angle between force and displacement direction

**Important cases:**
| Angle | cosθ | Work |
|-------|------|------|
| θ = 0° | 1 | Maximum (W = Fd) — force and displacement same direction |
| θ = 90° | 0 | Zero — force perpendicular to displacement |
| θ = 180° | −1 | Minimum (W = −Fd) — force opposes motion |

**SI Unit:** Joule (J)
- 1 Joule: Work done when 1 N force moves an object 1 m in the direction of force
- 1 J = 1 N × 1 m = 1 kg·m²/s²

**Other units:**
- Erg: 1 erg = 10⁻⁷ J
- Calorie: 1 cal = 4.184 J
- eV: 1 eV = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J

**Work can be positive, negative, or zero:**
- **Positive work**: Component of force is in direction of displacement
- **Negative work**: Force opposes motion (friction, air resistance)
- **Zero work**: Force perpendicular to displacement (centripetal force)

### Energy

**Definition:** The capacity to do work.

#### Kinetic Energy (KE)

Energy of motion.

**Formula:**
**KE = ½mv²**

Where:
- m = Mass (kg)
- v = Velocity (m/s)

**Characteristics:**
- Always positive (v² ≥ 0)
- Depends on square of velocity (doubling velocity → 4× KE)
- Zero when object is at rest

**Work-Energy Theorem:**
Total work done on an object = Change in kinetic energy
**W = ΔKE = ½mv_f² − ½mv_i²**

#### Potential Energy (PE)

Stored energy due to position or configuration.

**Gravitational Potential Energy:**
**PE = mgh**

Where:
- m = Mass (kg)
- g = Acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s²)
- h = Height above reference point (m)

**Important:** Height is measured from chosen reference point — zero height can be set anywhere.

**Elastic Potential Energy (Spring):**
**PE = ½kx²**

Where:
- k = Spring constant (N/m)
- x = Displacement from equilibrium (m)

**Hooke's Law:** F = −kx (restoring force)

### Conservation of Mechanical Energy

**In absence of non-conservative forces (friction, air resistance):**

**Total Mechanical Energy = KE + PE = Constant**

**E_i = E_f**
½mv_i² + mgh_i = ½mv_f² + mgh_f

**This is the Law of Conservation of Energy.**

### Energy Transformations

- **Falling object:** PE → KE (height decreases, speed increases)
- **Pendulum:** PE ↔ KE (continuous conversion)
- **Arrow released from bow:** PE (stored in bow) → KE (of arrow)
- **Electric heater:** Electrical energy → Heat energy
- **Photosynthesis:** Light energy → Chemical energy (in glucose)

### Power

**Definition:** Rate of doing work or rate of energy transfer.

**Formula:**
**P = W/t = Energy/time**

Also: **P = Fv** (for constant force in direction of velocity)

**SI Unit:** Watt (W)
- 1 Watt: 1 Joule per second
- 1 kW = 1000 W
- 1 MW = 10⁶ W

**Other units:**
- Horsepower (hp): 1 hp = 746 W
- erg/s: 1 erg/s = 10⁻⁷ W

### Efficiency

**η = (Output energy / Input energy) × 100%**

- Efficiency is always less than 100% (due to energy losses as heat, sound, etc.)
- No machine can have 100% efficiency (would violate conservation of energy)

### Work Done by Variable Force

If force varies with position:
**W = ∫F·dx** (area under F-x graph)

### Spring Systems

**Spring constant (k):** Stiffness of spring
- Larger k → Stiffer spring → More force needed for same displacement

**Work done in stretching/compressing:**
W = ½kx²

**Period of oscillation (mass-spring system):**
T = 2π√(m/k)

### Collisions and Energy

#### Elastic Collision
- Both momentum AND kinetic energy conserved
- Objects rebound without permanent deformation
- Example: Billiard balls

**Equations (for 1D):**
- m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ = m₁v₁' + m₂v₂' (momentum)
- ½m₁v₁² + ½m₂v₂² = ½m₁v₁'² + ½m₂v₂'² (KE)

#### Inelastic Collision
- Momentum conserved
- Kinetic energy NOT conserved (some KE → heat, sound, deformation)
- Objects may stick together (perfectly inelastic): v_f = (m₁v₁ + m₂v₂)/(m₁ + m₂)

**Perfectly inelastic:** Maximum KE lost

### Gravitational Potential Energy — General Formula

**For objects far from Earth's surface (g not constant):**
PE = −GMm/r

Where:
- G = Gravitational constant (6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²)
- M = Earth's mass
- m = Object's mass
- r = Distance from Earth's center

### Conservative vs Non-Conservative Forces

| Conservative Forces | Non-Conservative Forces |
|---------------------|-------------------------|
| Path independent | Path dependent |
| Work done stored as PE | Work done dissipated as heat |
| Examples: Gravity, Spring, EM forces | Examples: Friction, Air resistance |
| Mechanical energy conserved | Mechanical energy not conserved |

### Must-Remember Formulas

| Formula | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| W = Fd cosθ | Work done by force |
| KE = ½mv² | Kinetic energy |
| PE = mgh | Gravitational PE |
| PE = ½kx² | Spring potential energy |
| P = W/t | Power |
| P = Fv | Power (force × velocity) |
| η = Output/Input × 100% | Efficiency |
| v_f = (m₁v₁+m₂v₂)/(m₁+m₂) | Perfectly inelastic collision |
| T = 2π√(m/k) | Period of mass-spring |

### Must-Remember Facts

- Work done by gravity is path independent (conservative)
- Work done by friction depends on path (non-conservative)
- Energy can be neither created nor destroyed (Conservation of Energy)
- Doubling velocity → quadruples KE (KE ∝ v²)
- Doubling height → doubles PE (PE ∝ h)
- Power is a scalar quantity (no direction)
- 1 calorie = 4.184 Joule (calorie is used in nutrition)
- 1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J (commercial unit of energy)

### Common DU Admission Questions

1. Work done by force at angle (W = Fd cosθ)
2. Work-energy theorem (W = ΔKE)
3. Conservation of mechanical energy (PE + KE constant)
4. Power calculation (P = W/t or P = Fv)
5. Spring potential energy (½kx²)
6. Collision problems (elastic vs inelastic)
7. Efficiency of machines

### Exam Tips

- For work-energy problems: First find all forces, then find work done by each
- Mechanical energy is conserved ONLY when no non-conservative forces act
- In inclined plane problems: Work done against gravity = mgh (independent of slope)
- Power = Force × Velocity (useful when velocity is constant or given)
- Energy is scalar — no direction, but can be positive or negative
- Friction always does negative work (converts KE to heat)
