# Topic 9: General Science for Competitive Exams

### 🟢 Lite — Quick Review (1h–1d)
> Rapid summary for last-minute revision before your exam.

**Newton's Laws of Motion:**
- **1st Law (Inertia):** An object at rest stays at rest; an object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force
- **2nd Law (F = ma):** Force equals mass times acceleration; the greater the mass, the more force needed to accelerate it
- **3rd Law (Action-Reaction):** For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction

**Key Formulas:**
- **Velocity:** v = s/t (distance/time)
- **Force:** F = ma (Newton's 2nd Law; 1N = 1kg × 1m/s²)
- **Work:** W = F × d (force × distance; measured in Joules)
- **Power:** P = W/t (work/time; measured in Watts)
- **Kinetic Energy:** KE = ½mv²
- **Potential Energy:** PE = mgh (mass × gravity × height)
- **Weight:** W = mg (mass × gravitational acceleration; ~9.8 m/s² on Earth)

**Notable Scientists:**
- Isaac Newton (Laws of Motion, Gravity)
- Albert Einstein (E=mc², Theory of Relativity)
- Archimedes (Buoyancy, Levers)
- Michael Faraday (Electromagnetic Induction)

⚡ **Exam tip:** Physics questions in NABE focus on Newton's Laws and Force/Motion relationships. Always identify the correct formula before solving. Unit conversion (km/h to m/s: divide by 3.6) is frequently tested.

## Newton's Laws of Motion — Detailed

### First Law — Law of Inertia

An object will remain at rest or in uniform straight-line motion unless acted upon by an **external net force**.

**Examples:**
- A passenger lurches forward when a car suddenly brakes (their body continues moving at the car's previous speed)
- A coin placed on card held over a glass falls into the glass when the card is flicked quickly — the coin remains at rest relative to the glass
- Satellites continue moving in orbit without continuous propulsion (inertial motion in space)

**Key concept:** **Inertia** is the property of matter that resists changes in motion. More massive objects have greater inertia.

### Second Law — Force and Acceleration

The net external force on an object equals its mass times its acceleration:

**F = ma**

- **F** = Net force (Newtons, N)
- **m** = Mass (kilograms, kg)
- **a** = Acceleration (meters per second squared, m/s²)

**Example:**
If a 5 kg object is acted upon by a 20 N force:
> a = F/m = 20/5 = **4 m/s²**

**Weight vs. Mass:**
- Mass is constant (intrinsic property of matter)
- Weight is the force due to gravity: **W = mg**
- On Earth: g ≈ 9.8 m/s²
- On the Moon: g ≈ 1.6 m/s² (weight is ~1/6 of Earth weight)
- In orbit (microgravity): g ≈ 9.8 m/s² but astronauts appear weightless because both spacecraft and body are in free fall

### Third Law — Action and Reaction

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

**Examples:**
- Recoil of a gun when fired: bullet goes forward (action), gun pushes backward with equal force (reaction)
- A swimmer pushes water backward (action) and moves forward (reaction)
- Rocket propulsion: hot gases expelled downward (action); rocket moves upward (reaction)

**Important:** Action and reaction forces act on **different objects**, so they do NOT cancel each other out.

## Motion — Kinematics

### Speed and Velocity

- **Speed:** Scalar (magnitude only); s = distance/time (m/s, km/h)
- **Velocity:** Vector (magnitude + direction); v = displacement/time (m/s, with direction)

**Conversion:** To convert km/h to m/s: divide by 3.6
> 72 km/h = 72/3.6 = **20 m/s**

To convert m/s to km/h: multiply by 3.6
> 10 m/s = 10 × 3.6 = **36 km/h**

### Equations of Motion

For uniformly accelerated motion:

1. **v = u + at** (final velocity = initial velocity + acceleration × time)
2. **s = ut + ½at²** (displacement = initial velocity × time + ½ × acceleration × time²)
3. **v² = u² + 2as** (final velocity squared = initial velocity squared + 2 × acceleration × displacement)

Where: u = initial velocity, v = final velocity, a = acceleration, t = time, s = displacement

**Example problem:**
A car accelerates from rest (u=0) at 4 m/s² for 5 seconds. Find:
- Final velocity: v = 0 + 4×5 = **20 m/s**
- Distance covered: s = 0 + ½×4×25 = **50 m**

## Work, Energy, and Power

### Work

**W = F × d × cos(θ)**
- Measured in **Joules (J)** = 1 N × 1 m
- θ = angle between force and direction of motion
- If force is applied in the direction of motion: W = F × d

**Example:** A person pushes a box with 50 N force over 10 m:
> W = 50 × 10 = **500 J**

### Kinetic Energy (KE)

Energy of motion:
**KE = ½mv²** (Joules)

**Example:** A 2 kg ball rolling at 3 m/s:
> KE = ½ × 2 × 3² = **9 J**

### Potential Energy (PE)

Energy due to position (gravitational):
**PE = mgh**
- m = mass (kg), g = 9.8 m/s², h = height (m)

**Example:** A 3 kg mass at a height of 10 m:
> PE = 3 × 9.8 × 10 = **294 J**

### Law of Conservation of Energy

Energy is neither created nor destroyed — only transformed from one form to another.

**Example:** A ball dropped from height h:
- At top: PE = mgh, KE = 0
- At ground: PE = 0, KE = ½mv² = mgh (all potential converts to kinetic)

### Power

**P = W/t** (work done per unit time)
- Measured in **Watts (W)** = 1 Joule/second
- 1 kilowatt (kW) = 1,000 W

**Example:** A motor does 500 J of work in 10 seconds:
> P = 500/10 = **50 W**

## Notable Scientists and Their Discoveries

### Isaac Newton (1643–1727)
- **Laws of Motion** (formulated in *Principia Mathematica*, 1687)
- **Law of Universal Gravitation:** Every mass attracts every other mass; F = G(m₁m₂)/r²
  - G = gravitational constant = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ Nm²/kg²
- **Calculus:** Simultaneously invented with Leibniz (disputed)
- Invented the reflecting telescope

### Albert Einstein (1879–1955)
- **Special Theory of Relativity (1905):** The laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames; the speed of light is constant regardless of observer motion
- **Mass-Energy Equivalence:** E = mc² (energy equals mass times the speed of light squared)
  - This equation explains nuclear energy (fission and fusion)
- **General Theory of Relativity (1915):** Gravity is the curvature of spacetime caused by mass

### Archimedes (287–212 BC)
- **Buoyancy Principle:** A body submerged in fluid experiences an upward buoyant force equal to the weight of the displaced fluid
  - **Formula:** Fb = ρ × V × g (buoyant force = fluid density × volume submerged × gravity)
- **Lever Principle:** "Give me a place to stand and I will move the Earth"
- **Archimedes' Screw:** Device for pumping water still used in some applications

### Michael Faraday (1791–1867)
- **Electromagnetic Induction:** Moving a magnet through a coil generates an electric current
  - Foundation of electric generators and transformers
- **Faraday's Law of Electrolysis:** Relationship between electric current and chemical change

### James Clerk Maxwell (1831–1879)
- **Maxwell's Equations:** Unified electricity and magnetism into electromagnetism
- Predicted electromagnetic waves (radio waves, light, X-rays are all electromagnetic waves)

### Galileo Galilei (1564–1642)
- **Experimental physics:** Used pendulum for time measurement
- **Telescope observations:** Discovered Jupiter's moons, Venus's phases
- **Free fall:** All objects fall at the same rate in vacuum (ignoring air resistance)

## Acceleration and Gravity

### Acceleration due to Gravity

**g ≈ 9.8 m/s²** (near Earth's surface)
- Independent of the object's mass (Galileo's discovery)
- The moon's gravity is ~1/6 of Earth's; g_moon ≈ 1.6 m/s²
- g varies slightly with latitude and altitude

**Free Fall Formula:**
If an object is dropped from rest (u=0):
- v = gt
- s = ½gt²
- With g = 9.8 m/s²: after 5 seconds, v = 49 m/s, s = 122.5 m

### Circular Motion

An object moving in a circle at constant speed has:
- **Centripetal acceleration:** a = v²/r (directed toward the center)
- **Centripetal force:** F = mv²/r

**Examples:**
- Earth orbiting the Sun (gravitational force provides centripetal force)
- Car turning a corner (friction between tires and road provides centripetal force)

## Momentum

**p = mv** (momentum = mass × velocity; vector quantity; unit: kg m/s)

**Law of Conservation of Momentum:** In a closed system, total momentum before collision equals total momentum after collision.

**Example:** Two billiard balls colliding:
- m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ (before) = m₁v₁' + m₂v₂' (after)
- Elastic collision: kinetic energy also conserved
- Inelastic collision: kinetic energy is not conserved but momentum is

**Recoil of a gun:**
- Gun (mass M, velocity 0) fires bullet (mass m, velocity v)
- Gun recoils with velocity V = -(m/M) × v (momentum conservation)

## Pressure

**P = F/A** (Pressure = Force/Area; unit: Pascal = N/m²)

**Fluid Pressure:**
- P = ρgh (density × g × depth)
- Deeper you go in water, greater the pressure
- **Atmospheric pressure:** ~101,325 Pa at sea level (1 atm)

**Important formulas:**
- **Hydraulic pressure:** P = F₁/A₁ = F₂/A₂ — forms basis of hydraulic lifts
- **Buoyant force:** Fb = ρVg (Archimedes' principle)

## Light and Optics

### Laws of Reflection:
- Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection
- Mirror: virtual, upright, laterally inverted image

### Refraction:
- Light bends when passing from one medium to another
- **Snell's Law:** n₁ sin(θ₁) = n₂ sin(θ₂)
- **n** = refractive index of the medium

### Human Eye:
- Convex (convex lens) to focus light on retina
- **Far point:** infinity; **Near point:** ~25 cm
- Defects: Myopia (nearsighted — focal point before retina); Hyperopia (farsighted — focal point behind retina)
- Corrected with concave (myopia) or convex (hyperopia) lenses
