# Indian History & Culture

### 🟢 Lite — Quick Review (1h–1d)
> Rapid summary for last-minute revision before your exam.

Indian History & Culture is a high-yield area in MAT's General Knowledge section. Focus on ancient kingdoms (Maurya, Gupta), medieval dynasties (Delhi Sultanate, Mughals), and modern freedom movement leaders and events. For culture, remember key UNESCO World Heritage Sites in India, major classical dance forms, and important literary works across languages.

**High-yield points for quick revision:**
- Gupta Empire (Golden Age): Samudragupta, Chandragupta II, Nalanda University, Kalidasa
- Mughal Empire: Akbar's policy of religious tolerance, Taj Mahal (Shah Jahan), Aurangzeb's policies
- Freedom Movement: Congress sessions, Swadeshi Movement, Quit India, prominent leaders
- Dance forms: Bharatanatyam (Tamil Nadu), Kathak (North), Odissi (Odisha), Kathakali (Kerala)
- UNESCO sites: Taj Mahal, Khajuraho, Hampi, Ajanta-Ellora, Sun Temple Konark

**Exam tip:** MAT often asks about the cultural aspects — dance forms, languages, UNESCO sites, and important festivals. Don't ignore the freedom movement's key events and leaders.

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)
> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

#### Ancient India

**Indus Valley Civilisation (c. 3300–1300 BCE):**
- Locations: Harappa, Mohenjo-daro, Lothal, Dholavira, Rakhigarhi
- Features: Planned cities, drainage systems, standardised weights and measures, bronze age
- Trade: Extended trade with Mesopotamia; carnelian beads, copper, cotton goods
- Script: Undeciphered — remains the greatest unsolved puzzle

**Vedic Period (c. 1500–500 BCE):**
- Early Vedic: Rigveda, cattle wealth, Agni fire worship
- Later Vedic: Growth of kingdoms, iron usage, social stratification (varna system)
- Epics: Ramayana and Mahabharata — composed and codified in this period

**Mahajanapadas (c. 600–300 BCE):**
- Major states: Magadha, Kosala, Vatsa, Avanti, Kashi
- Magadha emerges dominant under Bimbisara and later his son Ajatashatru

**Maurya Empire (c. 322–185 BCE):**
- Chandragupta Maurya: Founded empire after defeating Seleucus
- Ashoka: Grandson; Kalinga War (转变); embraced Buddhism; spread dhamma
- Administration: Divided into provinces; officials called "mahamatras"

**Post-Maurya — Golden Age:**
- Gupta Empire: Chandragupta I, Samudragupta (greatest warrior), Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya)
- Nalanda University: World's oldest residential university
- Kalidasa: Shakuntala, Meghaduta; golden period of Sanskrit literature
- Ajanta & Ellora caves: Buddhist, Hindu, Jain rock-cut monuments

**Delhi Sultanate (1206–1526):**
- Slave Dynasty: Qutb-ud-din Aibak, Iltutmish, Razia Sultan (India's first female ruler)
- Khalji Dynasty: Alauddin Khalji — market reforms, Mongol repulsion
- Tughlaq Dynasty: Muhammad bin Tughlaq — token currency experiment, shift to Daulatabad
- Lodi Dynasty: Last Sultanate dynasty; ended with Babur's victory at Panipat (1526)

**Mughal Empire (1526–1857):**
- Babur: Central Asian ruler; introduced gunpowder and artillery to India
- Humayun: Defeated at Kanauj; fled to Persia; returned with Persian support
- Akbar (1556–1605): Expanded empire; built Fatehpur Sikri; policy of Sulh-i-Kul (universal peace); abolished jaziya
- Jahangir: Married Nur Jahan; patronised arts; had miniature paintings
- Shah Jahan (1628–1658): Built Taj Mahal for Mumtaz Mahal; shift to Persianate architecture
- Aurangzeb (1658–1707): Expanded to Deccan; reimposed jaziya; sparked rebellions

**Modern Freedom Movement:**

*Phase 1 — Early Nationalism (1885–1905):*
- Indian National Congress founded (1885) by A.O. Hume and W.C. Banerjee
- Prominent leaders: Surendranath Banerjee, Bal Gangadhar Tilak
- Partition of Bengal (1905) by Lord Curzon — sparked Swadeshi Movement

*Phase 2 — Militant Nationalism (1905–1919):*
- Lal-Bal-Pal triumvirate; Gopal Krishna Gokhale as mentor to Gandhi
- Muslim League founded (1906) by Aga Khan III
- Rowlatt Act (1919): "Black Act" — authorized preventive detention; led to Jallianwala Bagh massacre (April 1919)

*Phase 3 — Gandhi Era (1919–1947):*
- Non-Cooperation Movement (1920–1922): Charkha as symbol; Khadi; withdrew after Chauri Chaura
- Civil Disobedience Movement (1930): Dandi Salt March (388 km); 11-point demand
- Quit India Movement (1942): "Do or Die" — Gandhi's call; massive crackdown
- Partition and Independence (1947): Mountbatten Plan; Partition of India; independence on 15 August 1947

**Indian Culture — Key Points:**

*Classical Dance Forms:* Bharatanatyam (Tamil Nadu), Kathak (North India, Lucknow-Gwalior styles), Odissi (Odisha), Kathakali (Kerala), Mohiniyattam (Kerala), Manipuri (Manipur), Kuchipudi (Andhra Pradesh), Sattriya (Assam)

*UNESCO World Heritage Sites in India:* Taj Mahal (Agra), Ajanta Caves, Ellora Caves, Red Fort (Delhi), Qutub Minar (Delhi), Humayun's Tomb (Delhi), Hampi (Karnataka), Khajuraho Group of Monuments (Madhya Pradesh), Sun Temple Konark (Odisha), Mahabalipuram Group of Monuments (Tamil Nadu), Hill Forts of Rajasthan, Rani ki Vav (Gujarat)

*Languages & Literature:* 22 scheduled languages; classical languages: Sanskrit, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Odia; Sahitya Akademi awards; Rabindranath Tagore (Nobel Prize in Literature, 1913 — Gitanjali)

*Fairs & Festivals:* Diwali (festival of lights), Holi (spring festival), Eid (Ramadan/Eid-ul-Fitr, Eid-ul-Adha), Christmas, Baisakhi, Pongal, Onam, Durga Puja, Ganesh Chaturthi

### 🔴 Extended — Deep Study (3mo+)
> Comprehensive coverage for students on a longer study timeline.

#### Deeper Historical Connections

**Why Ancient Trade Routes Matter:**
The Grand Trunk Road (ancient Uttarapatha), starting from Bengal and running across North India to Afghanistan, facilitated commerce and cultural exchange for centuries. Similarly, the Silk Route connected India to Central Asia, China, and beyond — carrying not just goods but ideas, religions (Buddhism to China, Islam into India), and technologies.

**The Bhakti Movement's Social Impact (8th–18th century):**
The Bhakti saints challenged caste rigidity and promoted egalitarianism through personal devotion to God. Key figures include:
- Namdev (Maharashtra): Marathi Bhakti
- Guru Nanak (1469–1539): Founded Sikhism; rejected caste; advocated for women
- Ravidas (15th–16th century): Cobbler saint; questioned social hierarchy
- Kabir (15th century): Weaver; syncretic Hindu-Muslim spirituality; composed verses in Hindi
- Tulsidas (1532–1623): Ramcharitmanas in Awadhi; popularised Rama's story
- Surdas (16th century): BLind poet; composed in Braj Bhasha

**Mughal Administration — Beyond the Stereotypes:**
The Mughal administration was sophisticated. Mansabdari system (ranking every official and military commander), Diwani system for revenue collection (Dasabanda system under Todar Mal), and a postal system (dak) all contributed to imperial efficiency. The Akbar's Ibadat Khana (house of worship) experiment shows genuine intellectual curiosity about religious dialogue.

**British Colonial Impact — Economic History:**

The deindustrialisation of India is a critical concept. In 1750, India was the world's largest manufacturer, contributing about 25% of global industrial output. By 1900, this fell to under 2%:
- 1813: Charter Act — allowed British citizens to trade in India
- 1857: First War of Independence; end of East India Company rule
- 1858: British Crown directly takes over; Viceroy system begins
- Land revenue systems: Permanent Settlement (Bengal), Ryotwari (Madras), Mahalwari (Punjab) — each created different patterns of indebtedness and landlessness

**Modern India — Constitutional & Political Development:**
- 1919: Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms — dyarchy in provinces
- 1935: Government of India Act — provincial autonomy
- 1946–47: Cabinet Mission Plan; Mountbatten Plan (June 1947); Indian Independence Act (July 1947)
- Constitution of India (1950): Dr. B.R. Ambedkar chaired drafting committee; 395 articles, 22 parts; guarantees fundamental rights

**Culture — Living Traditions:**

*Traditional Indian painting:* Madhubani (Bihar), Warli (Maharashtra), Pattachitra (Odisha), Rajasthani Miniatures, Mughal Miniatures, Company style painting

*Indian textile heritage:* Banarasi silk, Kanchipuram silk, Bandhani (Gujarat), Kalamkari (Andhra Pradesh), Chikankari (Lucknow), Phulkari (Punjab), Ikat (Odisha and Telangana)

*Indian classical music:* Hindustani (North) and Carnatic (South); ghazal and thumri traditions; key musicians — Tansen, M.S. Subbulakshmi, Ustad Bismillah Khan, Pandit Ravi Shankar

#### MAT Exam Pattern: History & Culture Questions

The GK section of MAT (typically 8–10 questions on static GK) frequently draws from:
- Dynasty and ruler matching (especially matching works with their creators)
- UNESCO World Heritage Sites (location + significance)
- Dance forms with their states
- Books and authors (especially religious texts and epics)
- Important years and events in Indian history
- Freedom movement timeline

**Common trap:** Questions mixing up rulers from different dynasties, especially between Sultanate period rulers. Watch for Akbar confused with Humayun, or Shivaji confused with other Maratha rulers.

**Preparation strategy:** Create mnemonic stories for dynasties and their key achievements. Use timelines with visual anchors. Practice PYQs from past MAT papers to identify recurring themes.

#### Practice Questions

1. Which Gupta emperor is known as the "Napoleon of India"?
2. The Meenakshi Temple is located in which city?
3. Who was the first woman ruler of Delhi Sultanate?
4. Which battle ended the Mughal Empire?
5. The Jallianwala Bagh massacre occurred in which year?

*Answers: 1. Samudragupta; 2. Madurai (Tamil Nadu); 3. Razia Sultan; 4. Battle of Buxar (1764) / Panipat (1761 for Maratha); 5. 1919*
