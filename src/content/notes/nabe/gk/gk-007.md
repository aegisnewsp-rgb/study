## United Nations — Structure and Functions

The **United Nations** was founded on **October 24, 1945** (UN Day) after World War II to prevent another global conflict. The UN Charter was signed in San Francisco on June 26, 1945, by 50 nations. The organization now has **193 member states**.

### General Assembly

- **Composition:** All 193 member states; each has one vote
- **President:** Elected annually (rotating among regional groups); the President presides over GA sessions for that year
- **Functions:** Main deliberative body; adopts resolutions; approves UN budget; elects non-permanent Security Council members; observes elections for ICJ judges
- **Voting:** One nation, one vote (equal representation); resolutions require simple majority (except budgetary matters — two-thirds majority)

### Security Council

The **UN Security Council** is the most powerful UN body, responsible for international peace and security.

**Composition:**
- **5 Permanent Members (P5):** United States, United Kingdom, France, Russia, China
- **10 Non-Permanent Members:** Elected for 2-year terms by the General Assembly; regional distribution:
  - Africa (3 seats)
  - Asia-Pacific (2 seats)
  - Latin America & Caribbean (2 seats)
  - Western Europe & Others (2 seats)
  - Eastern Europe (1 seat)

**The Veto Power:**
The P5 each have a **veto** — any one can block a substantive resolution regardless of international support. This has paralyzed the Security Council on multiple occasions (Syria, Ukraine, Palestine). Any P5 resolution can be vetoed by another P5.

**Key Resolutions on Kashmir (UNSC):**
- **UNSC Resolutions 38 (1948), 47 (1948), 51 (1948), 80 (1950), 82 (1950), 91 (1951)** — all calling for a plebiscite in Kashmir
- None implemented due to the P5 dynamics (India argued these were pre-ceasefire resolutions, overtaken by events)

### Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)

- **54 members** elected by GA for 3-year terms
- Coordinates economic, social, and environmental work of the UN system
- Oversees specialized agencies: UNDP, UNCTAD, UN Women, WHO, UNESCO

### International Court of Justice (ICJ)

- **15 judges** elected for 9-year terms (5 judges every 3 years)
- Settles legal disputes between states; its decisions are binding
- The ICJ's **Advisory Opinions** are non-binding
- Located in The Hague, Netherlands
- Pakistan vs. India (Kulbhushan Jadhav case, 2019) — ICJ ruled Pakistan must grant consular access

### Specialized Agencies of the UN

## International Monetary Fund (IMF)

**The IMF** was established at the **Bretton Woods Conference (July 1944)** along with the World Bank.

### Purpose:
- Maintain **international monetary stability**
- Provide **financial assistance** to member countries facing balance of payments problems
- Promote **exchange rate stability**
- Foster **global trade** and economic growth

### How the IMF Works:

Member countries contribute **quota capital** based on their economic size. In return, they receive:
- **Drawing rights** — access to foreign exchange if they face balance of payments difficulties
- **Technical assistance** — on monetary, fiscal, and banking issues

**Key IMF Facilities:**
- **Stand-By Arrangement (SBA):** Short-term assistance for countries with temporary balance of payments problems
- **Extended Fund Facility (EFF):** Medium-term assistance for structural adjustment
- **Poverty Reduction and Growth Facility (PRGF):** Low-interest loans for low-income countries
- **Rapid Financing Instrument (RFI):** Emergency assistance

### Pakistan and the IMF

Pakistan has had **multiple IMF programmes** since the 1950s:
- **1958:** First Stand-By Arrangement
- **1970s:** Multiple arrangements after the 1971 war and oil crises
- **1990s:** Several SBA programmes
- **2008–13:** Post-2008 financial crisis; $10.7 billion SBA (2008)
- **2019:** $6 billion Extended Fund Facility (EFF) — programme suspended due to failure to meet conditions
- **2023:** $3 billion Stand-By Arrangement (SBA) — IMF Board approved in 2023 after economic crisis

## World Bank

**The World Bank Group** was also established at the **Bretton Woods Conference (1944)**.

### Purpose:
- Provide **long-term development financing** to developing countries
- Reduce **poverty and inequality**

### Institutions:

**International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD):**
- Lends to middle-income countries
- Uses its high credit rating to borrow from capital markets and on-lend to borrowing countries

**International Development Association (IDA):**
- Lends at concessional (near-zero interest) rates to the **lowest-income countries**
- Soft loan arm of the World Bank
- 173 member countries

**International Finance Corporation (IFC):**
- Invests in private sector enterprises in developing countries
- No sovereign guarantees required

**Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA):**
- Provides political risk insurance to foreign investors

**Pakistan and the World Bank:**
- Pakistan is a founding member
- Major projects in Pakistan: **Indus Basin Irrigation System (1960s)**; road infrastructure; health and education projects; the **Dasu Hydroelectric Project**; urban development

## World Trade Organization (WTO)

**The WTO** was established on **January 1, 1995**, replacing the **General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT, 1947)**.

### Purpose:
- Administer and enforce **international trade rules**
- Negotiate new trade liberalization agreements
- Resolve **trade disputes** between member states through the Dispute Settlement Body (DSB)
- Conduct **trade policy reviews**

### Key Principles:
- **Most-Favored Nation (MFN):** Trade concessions given to one member must be extended to all WTO members
- **National Treatment:** Imported goods must be treated no less favorably than domestically produced goods
- **Bound Tariffs:** Countries commit to ceiling bound rates on imported goods

### WTO Dispute Settlement:
- Countries file complaints with the WTO Dispute Settlement Body (DSB)
- Panel established; Appellate Body (3 judges) hears appeals
- Rulings are binding; non-compliance allows retaliatory trade measures

### Pakistan and the WTO:
- Pakistan joined the WTO on **January 1, 1997**
- Pakistan is an active participant in the **Doha Round** of trade negotiations
- Agricultural subsidies and market access remain contentious

## UN Peacekeeping Operations

The UN deploys **peacekeeping forces** (Blue Helmets) to conflict zones with the consent of all parties.

**Principles of UN Peacekeeping:**
1. Consent of the parties
2. Impartiality
3. Non-use of force except in self-defense

Pakistan has been one of the **largest contributors** to UN Peacekeeping Operations:
- Pakistani military and police personnel have served in Congo, Somalia, Bosnia, Haiti, and many other missions
- Pakistan has contributed more than **150,000 personnel** to UN missions since 1960

## Human Rights Bodies

**UN Human Rights Council (UNHRC):**
- 47 member states (elected by GA for 3-year terms)
- Pakistan served on the Council (2014–16)
- Passes resolutions on human rights situations
- **Universal Periodic Review (UPR):** Each country's human rights record is reviewed every 4.5 years

**UNHCR — The UN Refugee Agency:**
- Manages global refugee crises
- Pakistan hosts ~3 million Afghan refugees (one of the largest refugee populations in the world)
- UNHCR operations in Pakistan since 1979 (Soviet-Afghan war era)

## G-77 and Non-Aligned Movement (NAM)

**Group of 77 (G-77):**
- A coalition of developing nations at the UN
- Founded in 1964 by 77 founding members (now ~134 members)
- Pakistan has been an active member

**Non-Aligned Movement (NAM):**
- Founded in 1961 in Belgrade (Yugoslavia) by Tito, Nasser, and Nehru
- Pakistan was a founding member
- Purpose: Maintain independence from the US and Soviet Union Cold War blocs
- 120 member states
- **19th NAM Summit held in Baku, Azerbaijan (2019)**

## Bretton Woods Institutions — Origins

The **Bretton Woods Conference (July 1–22, 1944)** in New Hampshire, USA, created three institutions:
1. **IMF** (International Monetary Fund)
2. **World Bank / IBRD** (International Bank for Reconstruction and Development)
3. **ITO Charter** (International Trade Organization — which never came into being; GATT filled the gap until WTO was created in 1995)

**Key Architects:** John Maynard Keynes (UK) and Harry Dexter White (USA) designed the post-war monetary system.

The Bretton Woods system established:
- **Fixed exchange rates** pegged to the US Dollar
- **US Dollar as the reserve currency** (gold convertibility at $35/oz)
- Collapse of the system in **1971 (Nixon shock)** when the US ended gold convertibility

## Key Formulas for NABE

- **UN Security Council formula:** 5 permanent veto-holders + 10 rotating elected members = 15 total
- **IMF quota formula:** Quota determines drawing rights, voting power, and access to financing
- **GATT → WTO transition formula:** GATT (1947, provisional) → WTO (1995, formal) = Uruguay Round conclusion
- **World Bank formula:** IBRD (lends to middle-income) + IDA (concessional loans to low-income) = World Bank Group

The Bretton Woods system context is also a frequent question.
