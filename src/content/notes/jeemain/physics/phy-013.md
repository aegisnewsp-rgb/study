### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)
> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

**SHM** — JEE Main / Advanced Study Guide

**Simple Harmonic Motion** represents the most fundamental oscillatory motion where the **restoring force** is directly proportional to displacement from the mean position and directed opposite to it. This gives us the defining equation a = −ω²x, where ω is the angular frequency.

The solution to this differential equation is **x = A sin(ωt + φ)** or **x = A cos(ωt + φ)**, depending on the initial conditions. The **time period** T = 2π/ω depends only on the system's physical parameters, not on amplitude — this is the crucial independence principle.

For a **spring-mass system**, ω = √(k/m), giving T = 2π√(m/k). For a **simple pendulum** (small angles), ω = √(g/L), giving T = 2π√(L/g). The **potential energy** in SHM follows PE = ½kx², while **kinetic energy** = ½mω²(A² − x²). Total mechanical energy remains constant at E = ½kA².

**Solved Example 1:** A 2 kg mass attached to a spring (k = 200 N/m) oscillates with amplitude 0.1 m. Find maximum velocity. Solution: v_max = ωA = √(k/m) × A = √(200/2) × 0.1 = 10 × 0.1 = **1 m/s**

**Solved Example 2:** Time period of a simple pendulum on Earth is 2s. What is its period on Moon (g_moon = g/6)? Solution: T ∝ 1/√g, so T_moon = T_earth × √6 = 2 × 2.45 = **4.9s**

### 🔴 Extended — Deep Study (3mo+)
> Comprehensive coverage for students on a longer timeline.

**SHM** — Comprehensive JEE Notes

**Simple Harmonic Motion** emerges from the second-order linear differential equation d²x/dt² + ω²x = 0, whose solutions form a complete basis for analyzing small oscillations near stable equilibria. This connection to **Taylor expansions** explains why SHM describes virtually all small-amplitude oscillations in nature — any potential well approximated by V(x) ≈ V(0) + ½kx² yields harmonic motion.

**Damped SHM** introduces the more realistic equation d²x/dt² + 2βdx/dt + ω₀²x = 0, where β = b/(2m) is the damping coefficient. The solution reveals three regimes: **underdamped** (β < ω₀) — decaying oscillations; **critically damped** (β = ω₀) — fastest return to equilibrium without overshoot; **overdamped** (β > ω₀) — exponentially decaying without oscillation.
