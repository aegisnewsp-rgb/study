# Incorporation & Formation of Companies

### 🟢 Lite — Quick Review (1h–1d)
> Rapid summary for last-minute revision before your exam.

**Incorporation & Formation of Companies** — Key Facts for CS Executive
Modes of promotion: Promoter can be an individual, a group, or an existing company; 3 ways — self-promotion, professional promotion, financial promotion
Digital filing: All incorporation forms filed on MCA portal (mca.gov.in) — SPICe+ (Simplified Proforma for Incorporating Company Electronically Plus) for most company types
Mandatory registrations post-incorporation: PAN, TAN, GST, EPFO, ESIC, bank account opening
Certificate of Incorporation (COI): Issued by RoC with company's permanent account number — proof of legal existence; does not permit commencement of business for public companies (needs Commencement Certificate)
⚡ Exam tip: Always distinguish COI from Certificate of Commencement — COI proves existence, Commencement proves right to start business (public companies only)!

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)
> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

**Incorporation & Formation of Companies** — CS Executive Study Guide
Stages of incorporation: (1) Obtain DIN for proposed directors, (2) Obtain DSC for filing, (3) Reserve name via RUN, (4) File SPICe+ with MoA/AoA, (5) Pay registration fees, (6) RoC scrutinizes, (7) COI issued
Types of companies by incorporation: Private company (min 2, max 200 members, suffix "Private Limited"), Public company (min 7, no max, suffix "Limited"), One Person Company (OPC — single member/director, suffix "OPC Private Limited"), Section 8 company (non-profit, license under Section 8)
Distinction between holding and subsidiary: Holding company controls >50% equity/voting rights of subsidiary OR appoints majority of board members; subsidiary is a company controlled by another
Promoter's legal position: Promoter is not an agent or trustee of the company before incorporation; fiduciary duty to disclose all material facts; cannot make secret profit without disclosure
Pre-incorporation contracts: Not enforceable against the company (公司不能追认设立前合同); promoter's liability unless novation after incorporation
Capital subscription: Subscribers must sign Memorandum; Each subscriber is deemed to agree to become member; shares allotted must not exceed authorized capital in the Prospectus
⚡ Exam tip: Under Section 7 of Companies Act 2013, if company is incorporated by fraud or misrepresentation, any person affected can apply to NCLT for cancellation of incorporation — this is a favourite exam scenario!

### 🔴 Extended — Deep Study (3mo+)
> Comprehensive coverage for students on a longer study timeline.

**Incorporation & Formation of Companies** — Comprehensive CS Executive Notes
Companies Act 2013 key provisions: Section 3 — definition of company; Section 4 — memorandum; Section 5 — articles; Section 6 — intimation of registered office; Section 7 — incorporation documents
SPICe+ forms: INC-32 (MoA/AoA + incorporation), INC-33 (MoA), INC-34 (AoA), AGILE PRO-S (for GST, EPFO, ESIC, bank account — linked forms); filed as single window
Memorandum of Association (MoA): The charter of the company — defines scope and objects; must contain: name clause, registered office clause, objects clause, liability clause, capital clause, association/subscription clause; each clause signed by ≥7 subscribers (public) or ≥2 (private)
Articles of Association (AoA): Internal regulations governing internal management; must comply with Table F (for companies limited by shares) unless customized; can restrict powers of directors if shareholders choose
Doctrine of ultra vires: Acts beyond the objects clause in MoA are void and cannot be ratified even by unanimous shareholder resolution — money spent on ultra vires acts cannot be recovered
Registered office: Must be intimated to RoC within 30 days of incorporation; can be changed later via SRN; all communications sent to registered office are deemed served on company
Commencement of business: Private companies can start immediately after COI; Public companies must obtain Commencement Certificate under Section 11 (Companies Act 1956 provision — largely removed under 2013 Act but retained for certain businesses like banking, insurance, capital issues)
Effect of registration: Upon registration and COI, the company is deemed a separate legal person distinct from its members; can own property, sue and be sued in its own name; perpetual succession
Corporate veil doctrine: Courts will lift the corporate veil (ignore separate legal personality) in cases of fraud, evasion of legal obligations, tax avoidance, or where the company is a sham/alias for the promoter — Section 212 IPC (fraudulent action) also applicable
