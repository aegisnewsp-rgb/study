# Bangladesh's International Relations and Foreign Policy

Bangladesh's foreign policy is shaped by its geographic position as a small but densely populated country surrounded by India, its historical relationship with Pakistan, and its evolving partnerships with major global powers. Since independence, Bangladesh has pursued a "friendship to all, malice to none" foreign policy, prioritising economic development, regional stability, and multilateral cooperation. This is an important area for the DU Admission examination.

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)

> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

#### 1. Bangladesh-India Relations

India-Bangladesh relations are the most consequential bilateral relationship for Bangladesh.

**Historical Foundation:**
India played a decisive role in Bangladesh's independence:
- India recognised Bangladesh on **6 December 1971** — the first country to do so
- India deployed approximately **90,000–100,000 troops** to support Mukti Bahini forces during the Liberation War
- The Indian Army's Eastern Command was instrumental in the final push that led to Pakistan's surrender on 16 December 1971
- India provided sanctuary, training, and equipment to Bangladeshi freedom fighters

**The Border:**
Bangladesh and India share one of the longest international borders in the world — **4,096 kilometres**. This is a source of constant tension due to:
- **Enclaves:** India had 111 enclaves inside Bangladesh, and Bangladesh had 51 enclaves inside India. These were exchanged in the 2015 Land Boundary Agreement, implemented in 2015–2016 — a major diplomatic achievement.
- **Border killings:** Indian Border Security Force (BSF) has been accused of killing Bangladeshi citizens along the border
- Smuggling across the porous border

**River Water Disputes:**
Bangladesh is heavily dependent on rivers that flow from India:
- **Ganges (Padma):** Farakka Barrage (operational since 1975) has reduced water flow in the dry season
- **Teesta:** The most contentious river — a 2011 framework agreement between Manmohan Singh and Sheikh Hasina was not implemented
- **Mahananda, Muhuri, Feni:** Other disputed rivers
- India has constructed numerous dams on shared rivers, affecting Bangladesh's water security

**Trade and Economic Relations:**
- India is Bangladesh's **LARGEST import partner** (approximately $10–12 billion in imports)
- Bangladesh's exports to India are much smaller (~$1–2 billion)
- India provides duty-free access to Bangladesh under SAFTA (South Asian Free Trade Area)
- Bangladesh benefits from Indian energy exports (electricity, LNG)

**Key Treaties and Agreements:**
- **1972 Treaty of Friendship:** Signed by Indira Gandhi and Sheikh Mujibur Rahman
- **2011 BBIN Motor Vehicles Agreement:** Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Nepal signed the sub-regional agreement for cross-border movement of vehicles
- **Land Boundary Agreement (2015):** Resolved the enclaves issue

#### 2. Bangladesh-China Relations

China is Bangladesh's most important economic partner:

**Trade:**
- China is Bangladesh's **largest import partner** (approx. $15–20 billion in imports)
- Major imports: machinery, electronics, textiles, iron and steel
- Bangladesh exports very little to China (trade deficit is enormous)
- China is a major source of FDI and infrastructure financing

**Belt and Road Initiative (BRI):**
Bangladesh joined China's Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) in 2016:
- China has invested heavily in Bangladeshi infrastructure
- **Key projects:** Padma Bridge (major Chinese financing), Payra Deep Sea Port (Chinese involvement), Chinese-built power plants
- The Padma Bridge, opened in 2022, was built with Chinese financing ($1.9 billion of the $3.5 billion cost)

**Defence Relations:**
China is Bangladesh's largest supplier of military hardware:
- Bangladesh purchases arms, naval vessels, and military equipment from China
- China has provided tanks, fighter aircraft (J-7), and naval patrol boats
- Bangladesh is cautious about appearing too close to China due to India relations

**People-to-People Ties:**
- Large number of Bangladeshi students studying in China
- Chinese cultural centres (Confucius Institutes) in Bangladesh

#### 3. Bangladesh-Myanmar Relations and the Rohingya Crisis

Bangladesh's relationship with Myanmar is dominated by the Rohingya refugee crisis.

**The Rohingya Crisis:**
- The Rohingya are a predominantly Muslim ethnic minority from Rakhine State, Myanmar
- Myanmar denies them citizenship, considering them illegal immigrants from Bangladesh
- Myanmar security forces launched a brutal crackdown in August 2017, driving over **700,000 Rohingya into Bangladesh**
- Bangladesh, led by Sheikh Hasina, opened its borders to the refugees

**Refugee Settlements:**
The Rohingya refugees are housed in **Cox's Bazar** camps — the world's largest refugee settlement:
- Kutupalong camp is the largest single refugee camp in the world
- Over 1 million Rohingya are now in Bangladesh
- Bangladesh has sought to repatriate the Rohingya to Myanmar, but Myanmar has not created safe conditions for return

**International Legal Action:**
Bangladesh has supported international legal proceedings against Myanmar:
- **The Gambia vs Myanmar:** The International Court of Justice (ICJ) case (2019) — The Gambia alleged Myanmar violated the Genocide Convention. Bangladesh submitted evidence supporting the case.
- **ICC investigation:** The ICC authorized an investigation into the Rohingya situation (2020)
- These proceedings have generated international attention for the crisis

**Bangladesh-Myanmar Maritime Boundary:**
In 2008, Bangladesh and Myanmar resolved their maritime boundary dispute at the International Tribunal for the Law of the Sea (ITLOS).

#### 4. Bangladesh-Pakistan Relations

Relations between Bangladesh and Pakistan remain strained due to the 1971 Liberation War:

**Key Issues:**
- Pakistan has never formally apologised for the 1971 war crimes
- Pakistan opposed Bangladesh's war crimes tribunal (ICT-BD)
- Pakistan recalled its high commissioner after the execution of Jamaat-e-Islami leader Abdul Quader Mollah (2013)
- Civilian governments in Pakistan have occasionally made statements questioning the legitimacy of Bangladesh's independence

**Diplomatic Contacts:**
Diplomatic relations exist but are limited. There is minimal bilateral trade and cooperation.

**War Crimes Trials:**
Bangladesh's International Crimes Tribunal (ICT-BD) convicted several Jamaat-e-Islami leaders and a Pakistan army officer for war crimes in 1971. This remains a source of tension between the two countries.

#### 5. Bangladesh-United States Relations

The US has been a significant partner for Bangladesh:

**Historical:**
The United States supported Pakistan during the 1971 war ( Nixon administration), which created resentment. However, post-independence, US-Bangladesh relations normalised.

**Contemporary Relations:**
- **Largest export market:** The US is Bangladesh's largest export destination (primarily garments)
- **US GSP (Generalised System of Preferences):** Bangladesh benefits from preferential trade access to US markets
- **Security cooperation:** US provides military training, equipment, and counter-terrorism cooperation
- **Climate change:** US has provided significant climate finance to Bangladesh
- **Rohingya crisis:** US has provided substantial humanitarian aid for the Rohingya refugees

**US-Bangladesh Tensions:**
- US has raised concerns about human rights and democratic standards
- US has imposed visa restrictions on Bangladeshi individuals involved in election violence
- US has expressed concern about anti-Israel protests in Bangladesh

#### 6. Bangladesh-European Union Relations

The EU is Bangladesh's **largest trading partner**:
- EU absorbs approximately 60% of Bangladesh's garment exports
- Bangladesh benefits from the EU's "Everything But Arms" (EBA) scheme, providing duty-free access
- Bangladesh is negotiating a GSP+ scheme that would extend preferences to new products

**EU-Bangladesh Cooperation:**
- EU is a major donor for development projects in Bangladesh
- EU has supported climate adaptation projects
- EU has been engaged on the Rohingya crisis, providing significant humanitarian aid

#### 7. Bangladesh-Russia Relations

Russia (formerly the Soviet Union) was a key supporter of Bangladesh in 1971:
- The Soviet Union vetoed a US-proposed ceasefire resolution in the UN Security Council in December 1971
- Soviet support was instrumental in India's intervention on Bangladesh's behalf

**Contemporary Relations:**
- **Rooppur Nuclear Power Plant:** Being built by Russia's Rosatom — Bangladesh's largest infrastructure project
- Russia provides wheat aid to Bangladesh
- Bangladesh is cautious about its relationship with Russia due to the Ukraine war (2022) and Western pressure

#### 8. Bay of Bengal and Maritime Issues

**Maritime Boundary with India (2014):**
The landmark resolution of Bangladesh's maritime boundary dispute with India in 2014 was a major foreign policy achievement:
- The dispute involved the demarcation of the territorial sea, Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ), and continental shelf
- The ruling was delivered by the International Tribunal for the Law of the Sea (ITLOS) in 2012 and the Permanent Court of Arbitration (PCA) in 2014
- Bangladesh gained approximately 111,000 square kilometres of maritime area
- The ruling enabled offshore oil and gas exploration in the Bay of Bengal

**BIMSTEC and Regional Cooperation:**
Bangladesh has been a driving force in BIMSTEC (see GK-005), which it sees as a more effective regional forum than SAARC.
