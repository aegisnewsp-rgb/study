In solids, particles are tightly packed, retaining shape and volume; in liquids, particles are close but mobile, giving a definite volume but no fixed shape; gases have particles far apart, filling the container and being highly compressible. The kinetic‑molecular theory provides the framework: gas particles move randomly, have negligible volume and interact only through elastic collisions.
