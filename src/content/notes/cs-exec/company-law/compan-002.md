# Directors & Key Managerial Personnel

### 🟢 Lite — Quick Review (1h–1d)
> Rapid summary for last-minute revision before your exam.

**Directors & Key Managerial Personnel** — Key Facts for CS Executive
Types of directors: Executive (whole-time, day-to-day operations), Non-executive (no management role, shareholder nominee or independent), Independent (no business relationship with company, ≥50% of board should be independent for listed companies)
DIN: Director Identification Number — mandatory for every director; 8-digit unique number; apply via DIR-3 on MCA portal; cannot hold more than 20 directorships across all companies
Woman director: Every listed company and public company with ≥3 directors must have at least one woman director on the board
KMP definition (Section 2(51)): Managing Director, Whole-time Director, Chief Executive Officer, Company Secretary, Chief Financial Officer, such other officer as notified — collectively called Key Managerial Personnel
⚡ Exam tip: A director not attending board meetings for 12 consecutive months vacates office automatically under Section 167 — common exam question on vacation of office!

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)
> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

**Directors & Key Managerial Personnel** — CS Executive Study Guide
Appointment of directors: First directors named in AoA or appointed by subscribers at incorporation; subsequent directors appointed by shareholders in AGM; for public companies, individual director appointment requires shareholder resolution (not ordinary resolution — requires 1 week prior notice to shareholders)
Women director requirement: Unlisted public companies with ≥3 directors — at least 1 woman director; recommended for all large companies
Independent director: Must not have been Whole-time director/KMP in last 3 years; no material pecuniary relationship with company (other than sitting fees); appointed for fixed term (5 years, eligible for re-appointment after 1-year gap); responsibilities under Schedule IV of Companies Act 2013
Director's powers (Section 179): Board collectively has power to exercise all such powers and do all such acts as company is authorized to do — except those reserved for shareholders; power to borrow, invest, grant loans, approve financial statements — all board-level decisions
Limitations on director's powers: Shareholders must approve: related party transactions above threshold, sale of >33% assets, change in nature of business; directors cannot unilaterally pass these resolutions
Company Secretary: Mandatory for every listed company and public company with ≥₹50 lakh paid-up capital; duties include ensuring compliance with Companies Act, maintaining books, filing forms, advising board on legal matters
Disqualifications (Section 164): Director cannot hold office if: Unsound mind, insolvency, convicted of offense involving moral turpitude (5-year cooling period), non-payment of calls for 12 months, removed by court/NCLT, not having DIN
⚡ Exam tip: Related party transactions — any contract with director or relative must be disclosed and approved by shareholders if it exceeds ₹1 crore or 10% of turnover (whichever is lower) — this is a favourite provision testing corporate governance awareness!

### 🔴 Extended — Deep Study (3mo+)
> Comprehensive coverage for students on a longer study timeline.

**Directors & Key Managerial Personnel** — Comprehensive CS Executive Notes
Board composition rules: Listed companies — ≥50% should be independent directors; Non-listed public companies — not mandated but recommended; Private companies — minimum 2 directors, no independent director requirement unless they opt in
Director's liability: Can be held liable for — (1) breach of fiduciary duty (acting in personal interest), (2) ultra vires acts, (3) negligent conduct, (4) misstatements in prospectus (criminal liability), (5) Fraud under Section 447 (imprisonment up to 10 years + fine); liability is personal and not absolved by Articles
Board meetings: Minimum 4 meetings every year (1 per quarter); maximum gap between meetings should not exceed 120 days; quorum is 1/3 of total directors or 2, whichever is higher; decisions at board meetings are by majority vote; casting vote allowed for chairman only
Committees of board: Audit Committee (≥3 independent directors, oversees financials, related party transactions, internal audit), NRC — Nomination and Remuneration Committee (formulates criteria for appointment, reviews compensation), SRC — Stakeholders Relationship Committee (resolves stakeholder grievances)
Remuneration of directors: Sitting fees for board meetings (₹1 lakh per meeting max, as per Companies Act); commission up to 1% of net profits for non-executive directors; executive directors' remuneration approved by shareholders and must be within limits prescribed under Schedule V
Managing Director/Whole-time Director: Appointed for term up to 5 years; cannot hold office in competing company without prior approval; receives remuneration as per agreement and Schedule V limits; vacation of office happens on: resignation, death, bankruptcy, disqualification, removal by shareholders
Company Secretary as KMP: Appears in whole-time practice; duties: file annual returns (MGT-7), financial statements (AoP), event-based filings (INC-28, DIR-12), maintain statutory registers, conduct board/stock holder meetings, ensure compliance with listing agreements (for listed companies)
Ne ratio compliance: As per Companies Act and SEBI LODR, the ratio of median remuneration to highest director remuneration must be disclosed annually — this is a key governance metric for listed companies
