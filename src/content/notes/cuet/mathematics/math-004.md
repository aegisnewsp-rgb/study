# Circles

### 🟢 Lite — Quick Review (1h–1d)
> Rapid summary for last-minute revision.

**Circles** — Key Facts for CUET
• **Standard form**: (x−h)² + (y−k)² = r² with centre (h,k) and radius r
• **Most tested CUET concept**: Finding equation of tangent using perpendicular from centre to tangent line (slope form: y = mx ± r√(1+m²))
• **Common mistake students make**: Forgetting that radius must be positive and incorrectly applying ± sign when taking square root of r²
• **Key technique or method to solve quickly**: For tangent from point (x₁,y₁) to circle x²+y²+2gx+2fy+c=0, use T=0 form: xx₁+yy₁+g(x+x₁)+f(y+y₁)+c=0
• **Important exception or special case**: When point lies ON the circle, the "length of tangent" formula gives zero, indicating exactly one tangent (degenerate case)
• **Most frequent question type in CUET**: Finding equation of tangent at a point on the circle or determining whether a line is tangent/ secant/ external to circle using distance formula
⚡ Exam tip: For any line y = mx + c touching circle x²+y² = r², always verify c² = r²(1+m²) before writing final answer—many options trap you with sign errors.

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)
> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

**Circles** — CUET Study Guide

A **circle** is the set of all points in a plane equidistant from a fixed point (centre). The **standard equation** with centre (h,k) and radius r is (x−h)² + (y−k)² = r². Expanding gives the **general form**: x²+y²+2gx+2fy+c=0, where centre is (−g,−f) and radius = √(g²+f²−c), valid only when g²+f² > c.

For a circle with **diameter endpoints** A(x₁,y₁) and B(x₂,y₂), the equation is (x−x₁)(x−x₂)+(y−y₁)(y−y₂)=0. This directly applies the property that the angle in a semicircle is 90°.

The **tangent** to circle x²+y²=r² at point P(x₁,y₁) on it is xx₁+yy₁=r². For general circle, use **T=0 rule**: replace x²→xx₁, y²→yy₁, 2x→(x+x₁), and so on. The **length of tangent** from external point P to circle x²+y²+2gx+2fy+c=0 is √(x₁²+y₁²+2gx₁+2fy₁+c).

**Director circle** x²+y²=a²+b² is the locus of intersection of perpendicular tangents to ellipse x²/a²+y²/b²=1.

**Example 1**: Find tangent to x²+y²=25 at (3,4). Using xx₁+yy₁=25: 3x+4y=25. ✓

**Example 2**: Point (6,8) is 6 units from centre (0,0). Since 6²<r²=36? Wait—(6,8) gives 36+64=100>36, so external. Length of tangent = √(100−36)=8. ✓
