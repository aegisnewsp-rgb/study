# International Organisations & Summits

### 🟢 Lite — Quick Review (1h–1d)
> Rapid summary for last-minute revision before your exam.

International Organisations and Summits is a significant GK area for MAT. Focus on: United Nations and its specialised agencies, G20 (especially India's G20 presidency 2023), BRICS expansion, SAARC, ASEAN, WTO, World Bank, IMF, and major global summits like COP (climate), Davos (WEF), and Shangri-La Dialogue.

**High-yield points:**
- G20: 19 countries + EU; India held 2023 presidency; New Delhi Declaration; 2024 Brazil (Rio de Janeiro)
- BRICS: Original 5 (Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa); expanded (Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, UAE, Saudi Arabia) from January 2024; 10 members now
- UN Security Council: 5 permanent (Veto) + 10 non-permanent members; P5: USA, UK, France, Russia, China
- SAARC: India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Bhutan, Maldives, Afghanistan (suspended since 2021)
- ASEAN: 10 members; India not a member but dialogue partner

**Exam tip:** G20 and BRICS expansion are hot topics. Know India's role in these forums and recent summit outcomes.

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)
> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

#### United Nations System

**History and Founding:**
- UN Charter signed on 26 June 1945 in San Francisco; came into force on 24 October 1945 (UN Day celebrated annually)
- 51 founding members; now 193 member states (all except Vatican City and Taiwan)
- Aim: Prevent another world war; promote international peace, security, and cooperation

**Principal Organs:**

*General Assembly (UNGA):*
- All 193 member states; each has one vote
- President of UNGA rotates among regional groups annually
- Approves UN budget; elects non-permanent Security Council members, ECOSOC members, judges of ICJ
- Resolutions are non-binding (unlike Security Council resolutions)
- Uniting for Peace Resolution: General Assembly can act when Security Council is deadlocked

*Security Council (UNSC):*
- 15 members: 5 permanent (P5) + 10 non-permanent (elected for 2-year terms, 5 rotate each year)
- P5: United States, United Kingdom, France, Russia (succeeded USSR in 1991), China (succeeded Republic of China in 1971)
- Veto Power: Any P5 can block substantive resolutions (not procedural)
- Responsibility: International peace and security; can impose sanctions, authorise military action
- Reform: India, Japan, Germany, Brazil (G4) have been seeking permanent membership for decades; P5 oppose expansion

*Secretariat:*
- Headed by Secretary-General (appointed by GA on SC recommendation); 5-year term
- Current SG: António Guterres (Portugal, since 2017; reappointed for 2022–2026)
- Previous SGs: Kofi Annan (Ghana), Boutros Boutros-Ghali (Egypt), Javier Pérez de Cuéllar (Peru), U Thant (Myanmar), Dag Hammarskjöld (Sweden), Trygve Lie (Norway)

*International Court of Justice (ICJ):*
- 15 judges elected for 9-year terms; one third replaced every 3 years
- Seat: Peace Palace, The Hague, Netherlands
- Handles disputes between states; advisory opinions on UN legal questions
- Current President: Nawaf Salam (Lebanon, since 2024)
- ICJ's famous case: Nicaragua vs USA (1986); South Africa vs Israel (genocide case, 2023 ongoing)

*Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC):*
- 54 members; coordinates UN's economic and social work
- UNICEF, UNDP, WHO, UNHCR, ILO report through ECOSOC

**Specialised Agencies (Autonomous):**
- ILO (International Labour Organization): Labour standards; founded 1919; HQ: Geneva
- WHO (World Health Organization): Health; HQ: Geneva; Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus (Ethiopia, since 2017); declared COVID-19 a pandemic on 11 March 2020
- UNESCO (United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization): Education, culture; HQ: Paris; withdrawal by USA and Israel in 2017–2018; USA rejoined 2023
- IMF (International Monetary Fund): Financial stability; SDR allocations; Managing Director: Kristalina Georgieva
- World Bank: Development finance; President: Ajay Banga (India-born, 2023)
- FAO (Food and Agriculture Organization): Hunger, agriculture; HQ: Rome; QU Dongyu (China) since 2019
- UNDP (United Nations Development Programme): Human development; sponsors Human Development Index (HDI)

#### G20 and India's Presidency

**G20 (Group of Twenty):**
- Members: Argentina, Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, France, Germany, India, Indonesia, Italy, Japan, Mexico, Russia, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, South Korea, Turkey, United Kingdom, United States + European Union (29 economies total)
- Established: 1999 (finance ministers); elevated to heads of state in 2008 after global financial crisis
- Purpose: Global economic cooperation; accounts for 85% of world GDP, 75% of global trade, 2/3 of world population

**India's G20 Presidency 2023:**
- Theme: "Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam" (One Earth, One Family, One Future) — from Maha Upanishad
- President: 1 December 2022 to 30 November 2023; 18th and largest G20 presidency
- Summit: New Delhi, 9–10 September 2023; 42 leaders attended (first time for all EU and invited countries)
- Key Outcomes:
  - New Delhi Declaration: Consensus on all major issues (143 para agenda)
  - Africa Union (AU) admitted as permanent member (G20 now 21 members)
  - Global Biofuels Alliance (India-initiated); International Cricket Council (ICC) Observer status for Olympics
  - MoU on Circular Economy, Dementia Agreement
  - Green Development Pact
  - Digital Public Infrastructure (UPI, Aadhaar, ONDC) recognition
- 2024 G20: Brazil (Rio de Janeiro); 2025 G20: South Africa

#### BRICS and Expansion

**Original BRICS (2009–2022):**
- Coined by Jim O'Neill (Goldman Sachs, 2001); BRIC (Brazil, Russia, India, China); South Africa added (2010)
- First summit: Yekaterinburg, Russia (2009)
- Purpose: Reform of international financial institutions; multipolar world; South-South cooperation
- 2023 summit: Johannesburg (South Africa); expansion announced for 2024

**BRICS Expansion (2024):**
- At 2023 Johannesburg Summit, BRICS announced expansion to include (effective 1 January 2024):
  - Egypt (Arab Republic)
  - Ethiopia (Federal Democratic Republic)
  - Iran (Islamic Republic)
  - United Arab Emirates (UAE)
  - Saudi Arabia (Kingdom)
- "BRICS+" or "BRICS-11" now
- Purpose: Counter Western dominance in global financial system; increase influence in Global South
- Geopolitical significance: Saudi Arabia and UAE are US allies — their inclusion is notable
- Currency: Discussions on a common BRICS currency remain ongoing; no concrete mechanism yet

**New Development Bank (NDB):**
- BRICS' development bank; established 2014 (Fortaleza Declaration)
- HQ: Shanghai, China
- Purpose: Finance infrastructure and development projects in BRICS and developing nations
- President: Marcos Troyjo (Brazil, 2020–2025); first president: K.V. Kamath (India, 2015–2020)

#### South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC)

**Members:** Afghanistan, Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Maldives, Nepal, Pakistan, Sri Lanka
- Afghanistan suspended since Taliban takeover (August 2021)
- HQ: Kathmandu, Nepal
- Founded: 8 December 1985 (Dacca Declaration) by Zia's Bangladesh
- Summits: 18th summit (2014, Kathmandu); 19th summit planned for 2023 (not held)

**SAARC Development Fund:** $300 million fund; India contributes ~70%

**Indo-Pak tensions:** Bilateral disputes have often blocked SAARC progress; India-Pakistan tension is a major obstacle

#### ASEAN and India's Relations

**ASEAN (Association of Southeast Asian Nations):**
- Members: Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Brunei, Vietnam, Laos, Myanmar, Cambodia
- Founded: 8 August 1967 (Bangkok Declaration)
- HQ: Jakarta, Indonesia
- ASEAN Regional Forum (ARF): 27 members including India, USA, China, Japan, EU

**India-ASEAN Relations:**
- India is not an ASEAN member but a "Dialogue Partner" (since 1992), "Summit Level" partner (since 2005)
- Look East Policy → Act East Policy: Enlarged engagement with Southeast Asia; ASEAN at its core
- ASEAN-India Free Trade Area (AITIGA): Negotiations for review ongoing
- India invests in connectivity: Kaladan Multimodal Project (connecting West Bengal to Mizoram via Myanmar); India-Myanmar-Thailand Trilateral Highway

#### World Trade Organization (WTO)

**Overview:**
- Founded 1995 (replaced GATT — General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade, 1947)
- HQ: Geneva, Switzerland
- 164 members (including China since 2001, Russia since 2012)
- Director-General: Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala (Nigeria, since 2021; first woman and first African); previous: Roberto Azevedo (Brazil)

**Core Principles:**
- Most-favoured nation (MFN): Any advantage given to one member must be extended to all
- National Treatment: Imported goods must be treated no less favourably than domestic goods
- Free trade through progressive reduction of tariffs
- Binding tariffs: Countries cannot raise tariffs beyond bound rates without compensating others

**Major Disputes:**
- India-US: On India's export subsidies (DST); US challenged India's agricultural subsidies at WTO
- India-EU: Trade issues; GSP (Generalised System of Preferences) withdrawal by USA (India lost GSP benefits in 2019)
- Ongoing: Agricultural subsidies, industrial tariffs, services trade

#### Shangri-La Dialogue and Security Forums

**Shangri-La Dialogue (SLD):**
- Annual security forum; organised by International Institute for Strategic Studies (IISS)
- Location: Singapore; started 2002
- Attendees: Defence ministers, military chiefs, security experts from Asia-Pacific
- Named after the novel "Lost Horizon" (1933) by James Hilton
- Not to be confused with any official government dialogue

**Other Security Forums:**
- Raisina Dialogue (India): Annual geo-political conference in New Delhi; organised by ORF (Observer Research Foundation); started 2016; brings together global leaders
- Munich Security Conference: Annual; Germany; Western-centric
- ASEAN Regional Forum (ARF): Includes US, China, India, Japan, EU

#### Global Climate Agreements

**COP (Conference of Parties):**
- Annual UN climate conference under UNFCCC (United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change)
- UNFCCC: Adopted 1992 (Earth Summit, Rio de Janeiro); entered into force 1994
- Secretariat: Bonn, Germany

*Key COPs:*
- COP3 (1997, Kyoto): Kyoto Protocol adopted; legally binding emission targets for developed countries; entered force 2005; USA withdrew (2001)
- COP15 (2009, Copenhagen): Failed to produce binding agreement; "Copenhagen Accord" — voluntary pledges
- COP21 (2015, Paris): Paris Agreement — landmark; 196 parties; limit global warming to 1.5–2°C; voluntary nationally determined contributions (NDCs); entered force 2016
- COP26 (2021, Glasgow): Glasgow Climate Pact; phase-down of coal; first COP to explicitly target fossil fuels
- COP27 (2022, Sharm El-Sheikh, Egypt): Loss and Damage Fund established; breakthrough on compensating vulnerable nations
- COP28 (2023, Dubai/UAE): UAE presidency; first Global Stocktake; fossil fuel "transitioning away" language in final text; Loss and Damage Fund operationalised

**Paris Agreement:**
- NDCs: Countries set own targets; must update every 5 years
- India: 45% emission intensity reduction (from 2005 levels) by 2030; 50% cumulative electric power from non-fossil sources by 2030; Carbon neutrality by 2070

**Climate Finance:**
- $100 billion/year commitment by developed countries (to developing nations); not fully met
- Green Climate Fund (GCF): Established 2010; $9.9 billion pledged (2014); US, EU, Japan largest donors
- India: Vulnerable to climate change despite low historical emissions; floods, cyclones, heatwaves increasing

#### World Economic Forum and Davos

**World Economic Forum (WEF):**
- Founded: 1971 by Klaus Schwab
- HQ: Geneva, Switzerland
- Annual meeting in Davos, Switzerland (January); "Summer Davos" in Tianjin, China (Dalian)
- Purpose: Public-private cooperation; globalisation; 4th Industrial Revolution

**Davos 2024:** Theme: "Rebuilding Trust"; AI governance major topic; India highlighted as fastest-growing major economy

**WEF and India:**
- Klaus Schwab frequently praised India's UPI and digital public infrastructure
- India at Davos: IT sector, demographic dividend, renewable energy commitments

#### NATO and Global Security Architecture

**NATO (North Atlantic Treaty Organization):**
- Founded: 4 April 1949 (Washington Treaty); 12 original members
- HQ: Brussels, Belgium
- Article 5: An attack on one member is an attack on all (collective defence — invoked only once: after 9/11)
- 31 members (as of 2024); Finland (2023) and Sweden (2024) joined recently
- Secretary-General: Jens Stoltenberg (Norway, 2014–2024); Mark Rutte (Netherlands, from 2024)

**India and NATO:**
- India is not a NATO member; historically non-aligned; strategic autonomy
- India buys Russian defence equipment (S-400 Triumf from Russia); USCAATSA sanctions risk
- India has strong defence cooperation with USA, France, Israel, Russia independently
- Quad (with USA, Japan, Australia) is India's primary Indo-Pacific security forum

#### World Bank and IMF Details

**World Bank:**
- IBRD (International Bank for Reconstruction and Development): Loans to middle-income countries
- IDA (International Development Association): Concessional loans to lowest-income countries
- IFC (International Finance Corporation): Private sector investment
- MIGA (Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency): Political risk insurance
- ICSID (International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes): Investor-state disputes

**IMF:**
- Quota system: Based on economic size; determines voting power
- SDR (Special Drawing Rights): Reserve asset; allocated to members in proportion to quota
- 2021 SDR Allocation: $650 billion; India received ~$21 billion (3rd largest after USA, Japan)
- Conditionality: Loans come with structural adjustment conditions

**India and Multilateral Institutions:**
- India is 6th largest quota country at IMF; 7th at World Bank
- India as lender: Contributed to IMF's loan programme for countries in crisis
- BRICS' New Development Bank: Alternative to World Bank

#### MAT Strategy for International Organisations

**High-frequency question types:**
- Headquarters of major organisations
- Countries heading specific bodies (WTO DG, World Bank President, IMF MD, UN SG)
- India's membership and partnership status
- Recent expansion/inclusion decisions (BRICS, AU to G20)
- Veto powers at UNSC
- Outcomes of recent summits (G20, COP, Davos)
- India's positions at these forums (non-aligned, Global South leadership)

**Key facts to memorise:**
- WTO DG: Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala (Nigeria, first woman)
- World Bank President: Ajay Banga (India-born, American)
- UN Secretary-General: António Guterres (Portugal)
- WHO Director-General: Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus (Ethiopia)
- NATO Secretary-General: Mark Rutte (Netherlands, 2024)
