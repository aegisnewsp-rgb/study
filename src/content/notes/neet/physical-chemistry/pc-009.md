**Electrochemistry** ‚ÄĒ Quick Facts

Electrochemistry deals with the interconversion of chemical energy and electrical energy. Galvanic cells convert chemical energy to electricity; electrolytic cells use electricity to drive non-spontaneous reactions.

**Key Definitions:**
- **Anode:** Electrode where oxidation occurs (loss of electrons)
- **Cathode:** Electrode where reduction occurs (gain of electrons)
- **Voltaic/Galvanic cell:** Spontaneous reaction ‚Üí electricity generated
- **Electrolytic cell:** Non-spontaneous reaction ‚Üí electricity consumed

**Essential Formulas:**
- Cell emf: E¬į_cell = E¬į_cathode ‚ąí E¬į_anode
- Nernst equation: E = E¬į ‚ąí (0.0591/n) log Q at 298 K
- For the reaction: aA + bB ‚Üí cC + dD, Q = [C]^c[D]^d / [A]^a[B]^b
- Free energy: őĒG = ‚ąínFE (F = 96500 C mol‚ĀĽ¬Ļ)
- Equilibrium constant: őĒG¬į = ‚ąíRT ln K ‚Üí E¬į_cell = (RT/nF) ln K = (0.0591/n) log K

‚ö° **Exam tip:** When writing Nernst equation, always use n = total electrons transferred (not per species). For Zn + Cu¬≤‚Āļ ‚Üí Zn¬≤‚Āļ + Cu, n = 2.

‚ö° **Common mistake:** Students forget the sign convention. E¬į_cell must be positive for a spontaneous galvanic cell. If E¬į_cathode < E¬į_anode, the cell won't work as written.
