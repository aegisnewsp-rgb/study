# Topic 3: Respiration and Circulation

## Types of Respiration

### Aerobic Respiration
- Occurs in the **presence of oxygen**
- Takes place in the **mitochondria**
- **Complete breakdown** of glucose → maximum energy released
- Equation:
```
C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Energy (38 ATP)
Glucose + Oxygen → Carbon dioxide + Water + Energy
```
- 1 molecule of glucose yields approximately **38 ATP** molecules

### Anaerobic Respiration
- Occurs in the **absence of oxygen**
- Takes place in the **cytoplasm**
- **Incomplete breakdown** of glucose → less energy released
- In muscles during heavy exercise (produces lactic acid):
```
C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₃H₆O₃ + Energy (2 ATP)
Glucose → Lactic acid + Energy
```
- In yeast (alcoholic fermentation):
```
C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₂H₅OH + 2CO₂ + Energy
Glucose → Ethanol + Carbon dioxide + Energy
```
- **Oxygen debt:** The extra oxygen consumed to break down lactic acid after exercise
- Anaerobic respiration in humans causes **muscle fatigue** and **cramps**

> **CTET Key:** Aerobic = with oxygen → CO₂ + H₂O + lots of ATP. Anaerobic = without oxygen → lactic acid/ethanol + CO₂ + few ATP.

## Human Respiratory System

### Structure and Function

| Part | Function |
|---|---|
| **Nostrils** | Filter dust particles, warm and moisten air |
| **Nasal cavity** | Lined with hair and mucus to trap dust/microbes |
| **Pharynx** | Common passage for food and air |
| **Larynx** | Voice box; contains vocal cords |
| **Trachea** | Windpipe; carries air to bronchi; lined with cilia and mucus |
| **Bronchi** | Two branches from trachea; enter left and right lungs |
| **Bronchioles** | Smaller branches within lungs |
| **Alveoli** | Tiny air sacs; site of gas exchange (O₂ enters blood, CO₂ leaves) |
| **Lungs** | Two spongy, elastic organs; site of respiration |

### Breathing Mechanism

**Inhalation (Inspiration):**
1. Diaphragm **contracts** and flattens
2. External intercostal muscles **contract**, ribs move upward and outward
3. Chest cavity **expands**
4. Air pressure inside lungs **decreases** below atmospheric pressure
5. Air **rushes in** from outside

**Exhalation (Expiration):**
1. Diaphragm **relaxes** and domes upward
2. Internal intercostal muscles **contract**, ribs move downward and inward
3. Chest cavity **contracts**
4. Air pressure inside lungs **increases** above atmospheric pressure
5. Air is **pushed out**

**Key Fact:** Breathing is a **physical process** (ventilation); respiration is a **chemical process** (cellular oxidation of glucose).

### Lung Volumes

| Term | Volume |
|---|---|
| Tidal Volume | ~500 mL (normal breathing) |
| Vital Capacity | ~4500 mL (max forced exhalation after max inhalation) |
| Residual Volume | ~1500 mL (air left after forced exhalation) |

## Double Circulation

In mammals, blood passes through the heart **twice** per complete circuit:

### Pulmonary Circulation
- Right ventricle → Pulmonary artery → Lungs (O₂ poor blood releases CO₂, picks up O₂) → Pulmonary vein → Left atrium

### Systemic Circulation
- Left ventricle → Aorta → All body parts (O₂ rich blood delivers O₂, picks up CO₂) → Vena cava → Right atrium

> **Why double circulation?** Blood pressure drops significantly after passing through capillaries in the lungs (pulmonary circuit). The heart must re-pressurise the blood for the systemic circuit. This ensures efficient oxygen delivery to body tissues.

## Blood Components

| Component | Function | Normal Count |
|---|---|---|
| **Plasma** (55%) | Carries nutrients, wastes, hormones, proteins; maintains pH | — |
| **Red Blood Cells (RBCs)** | Carry O₂ to tissues and CO₂ to lungs | 4.5–5.5 million/mm³ |
| **White Blood Cells (WBCs)** | Fight infection (immunity) | 4,000–11,000/mm³ |
| **Platelets** | Blood clotting | 150,000–400,000/mm³ |

### Haemoglobin
- Iron-containing protein in RBCs
- Binds with O₂ to form **oxyhaemoglobin** (bright red)
- Binds with CO₂ as **carbaminohaemoglobin** (dark red)
- Normal haemoglobin: 12–16 g/dL (women), 14–18 g/dL (men)

## Blood Groups

### ABO System

| Blood Group | Antigen on RBC | Antibody in Plasma | Can Receive From | Can Donate To |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | A antigen | Anti-B antibody | A, O | A, AB |
| **B** | B antigen | Anti-A antibody | B, O | B, AB |
| **AB** | A and B antigens | Neither | A, B, AB, O (universal receiver) | AB only |
| **O** | Neither antigen | Both Anti-A and Anti-B | O only | A, B, AB, O (universal donor) |

### Rh Factor
- **Rh⁺ (Rh positive):** Rh antigen present on RBCs (85% of Indians)
- **Rh⁻ (Rh negative):** Rh antigen absent
- **Rh incompatibility:** If an Rh⁻ mother carries an Rh⁺ baby, her body may produce antibodies against the baby's RBCs (especially in second pregnancy) → **Erythroblastosis foetalis**
- Rh⁻ mothers are given **anti-Rh globulin injection** (RhoGAM) to prevent this

## Human Heart

- A **four-chambered**, muscular organ (~300 g, size of a fist)
- Located in the **thoracic cavity** (mediastinum), between the two lungs
- Protected by the **pericardium** (double-layered membrane)

### Chambers

| Chamber | Type | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Right Atrium** | Upper right | Receives deoxygenated blood from vena cava |
| **Right Ventricle** | Lower right | Pumps blood to lungs via pulmonary artery |
| **Left Atrium** | Upper left | Receives oxygenated blood from pulmonary veins |
| **Left Ventricle** | Lower left | Pumps blood to aorta → whole body (strongest wall) |

### Cardiac Cycle
- Heart beats ~**72 times per minute** at rest
- **Systole:** Contraction phase (blood pumped out)
- **Diastole:** Relaxation phase (chambers fill with blood)
- **Lubb-Dubb** sound: Lubb = AV valves closing; Dubb = SL valves closing
- **Pacemaker (SA node):** Sets heart rhythm (~72 bpm); located in right atrium

### Blood Vessels

| Vessel | Function |
|---|---|
| **Arteries** | Carry blood **away** from heart; thick walls; usually carry oxygenated blood (except pulmonary artery) |
| **Veins** | Carry blood **towards** heart; thin walls; have valves to prevent backflow; usually carry deoxygenated blood (except pulmonary vein) |
| **Capillaries** | Microscopic; one cell thick; site of exchange of materials between blood and tissues |

### ⚡ CTET Exam Tips

- **Alveoli** are the functional units of the lung — thin-walled for efficient gas exchange
- **Left ventricle** has the **thickest wall** because it pumps blood to the whole body (highest resistance)
- Blood group **O⁻** is the universal donor; **AB⁺** is the universal receiver
- Platelets are **not true cells** — they are cell fragments; deficiency causes **haemophilia** (bleeding disorder)
- **Heart attack** = death of heart muscle due to blocked coronary artery
- Remember: Arteries usually carry **oxygenated** blood, but pulmonary artery carries **deoxygenated** blood (exception)
- **Diaphragm** is the primary muscle of inspiration — used heavily in breathing
