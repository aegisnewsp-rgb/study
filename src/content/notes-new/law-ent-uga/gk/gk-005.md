# Topic 5: Criminal Law in Uganda — Penal Code, Criminal Procedure Code, and Key Provisions

## 🟢 Quick Review

Uganda's criminal law is primarily codified in the **Penal Code Act (Cap 120)** — a comprehensive criminal statute covering offences against persons, property, and public order. The **Criminal Procedure Code (RPC — Cap 71)** governs criminal procedure. Understanding the elements of key offences, arrest and bail rules, and the trial process is essential for any law student.

---

## 📜 The Penal Code (Cap 120)

### Overview

The Penal Code Act (Cap 120) is Uganda's primary criminal statute, organized into sections covering:
- Part I — General provisions (definitions, interpretation)
- Part II — Offences against public order (treason, sedition, unlawful assembly)
- Part III — Offences against the person (homicide, assault, sexual offences)
- Part IV — Offences against property (theft, robbery, burglary, criminal destruction)
- Part V — Forgery and currency offences
- Part VI — Miscellaneous offences

The Penal Code was originally based on the **Indian Penal Code 1860** (like Kenya and Tanzania), adapted for Uganda's colonial context.

### Homicide — Key Distinctions

#### Murder (Section 203)

A person commits murder who commits culpable homicide with **malice aforethought**, which exists where the accused:
- Intended to cause death
- Intended to cause serious bodily injury knowing it was likely to cause death
- Knew the act was so dangerous that death was a probable consequence

**Aggravated Murder:** Murder committed in the course of certain serious crimes (e.g., robbery with violence, defilement) or with specific intent attracts the **death penalty** (now effectively under challenge).

**Death Penalty Status:** The death penalty was challenged in *Kafuza v Uganda* [2024] where the Constitutional Court held it unconstitutional. The Supreme Court is considering this question. Regardless, Uganda has not carried out an execution in decades — death sentences are typically commuted to life imprisonment.

#### Culpable Homicide (Section 202)

A person commits culpable homicide who causes death:
- By an unlawful act or omission
- By negligence or failure to perform a duty
- By causing bodily injury that leads to death

Culpable homicide not amounting to murder is punishable with **imprisonment for life** or a lesser term.

**Key Distinction from Murder:** The absence of malice aforethought distinguishes culpable homicide from murder.

## 📦 Key Property Offences

### Theft (Section 254)

A person steals who dishonestly:
- Takes movable property belonging to another
- With intent to permanently deprive the owner

**Key Elements:**
- **Actus reus:** Caption (taking) and asportation (carrying away)
- **Mens rea:** Dishonesty + intent to permanently deprive

**Punishment:** Simple theft — imprisonment for **7 years**. Aggravated theft (e.g., theft by servant, from a building, of cattle) may attract longer sentences.

### Robbery (Section 272)

Robbery is theft with **violence or putting in fear**. The offender uses or threatens force to steal.

**Armed Robbery:** If the robber is armed or in company with an armed person, the penalty is **life imprisonment**.

### Housebreaking (Sections 289–298)

- **Burglary:** Entering a building at night with intent to commit an indictable offence — imprisonment for **14 years**
- **Housebreaking with intent to steal:** Entering a building and stealing — imprisonment for **10 years**
- **Housebreaking to commit any other offence**

## 🚨 Sexual Offences

The Penal Code (Cap 120) originally contained all sexual offence provisions, but Uganda enacted the **Sexual Offences Act 2023** (replacing the earlier 1998 version) which substantially updated and expanded the law.

### Key Sexual Offences (Sexual Offences Act 2023)

**Aggravated Defilement (Sexual Offences Act, 2023):**
- Sexual intercourse with a child (under 18)
- **Strict liability** — no defence of reasonable belief as to age in certain contexts
- Punishable with **life imprisonment**

**Defilement (Sexual Offences Act):**
- Sexual intercourse with a person under 18
- The offender must be of or above the age of 18
- **Age of consent is 18** in Uganda

**Rape (Sexual Offences Act 2023):**
- Sexual intercourse without consent
- Includes penetration by force, fraud, or when the victim is unable to consent (incapacitated, underage)
- Penile-vaginal and other forms of penetration

**Sexual Assault:** Any intentional touching of a sexual nature without consent.

**Note on Marital Rape:** The Sexual Offences Act 2023 for the first time explicitly criminalizes marital rape — an important progressive step.

## ⚖️ Criminal Procedure Code (RPC) (Cap 71)

### Investigation and Arrest

**Arrest Without Warrant (Section 23 RPC):**

A police officer may arrest without a warrant if:
- The person commits an offence in the officer's presence
- The officer has reasonable suspicion that the person has committed an offence
- The person has been proclaimed by the President (for certain offences)
- A warrant has been issued by a court

**Arrest with Warrant:** A police officer may also effect an arrest with a court-issued warrant.

**Rights of Arrested Persons (Article 23 of the Constitution):**
An arrested person must be:
- **Informed immediately** of the reason for arrest
- Permitted to contact a lawyer of their choice
- **Brought before a court within 48 hours** (48 hours is the constitutional limit before judicial oversight is mandatory)

**Right to Remain Silent:** An arrested person has the right to remain silent and to be informed of this right.

### Bail

**Right to Bail (Sections 36–45 RPC):**

Bail is the release of an accused person on their undertaking to appear at trial. The CPC distinguishes:
- **Police bail** — for minor offences, a police officer of or above the rank of Inspector may grant bail at the police station
- **Court bail** — at first court appearance, the court may grant or refuse bail

**Refusal of Bail:**
Bail may be refused where:
- The offence is serious (e.g., treason, murder)
- There is risk of the accused absconding
- There is risk of the accused interfering with witnesses
- The accused poses a danger to the public

**Sureties:** A surety (person who guarantees the accused's attendance) may be required.

### Trial Procedure

**First Court Appearance:**
1. **Charge reading** — accused informed of the specific offence(s)
2. **Plea** — guilty or not guilty

**If not guilty:**
3. **Prosecution case** — witnesses testify, evidence is led (examination-in-chief, cross-examination, re-examination)
4. **Defence case** — accused may testify (not obligated — right to silence) or call witnesses
5. **Judgment** — verdict (guilty or not guilty)
6. **Sentencing** — if guilty

**Burden of Proof:** In criminal cases, the **burden is on the prosecution** to prove guilt **beyond reasonable doubt** (Section 101 of the Evidence Act — Cap 6). The accused does not need to prove their innocence.

## 🔑 Exam High-Yield Points

1. **Penal Code (Cap 120)** — Uganda's primary criminal statute, organized by offences against persons, property, public order
2. **Murder (Section 203)** — requires malice aforethought (intent to kill or knowledge death was likely)
3. **Culpable homicide (Section 202)** — unlawful killing without malice aforethought — punishable with life imprisonment
4. **Death penalty** — currently under constitutional challenge (*Kafuza v Uganda* [2024]); not carried out in practice
5. **Age of consent is 18** in Uganda; defilement (sex with under-18) is a strict liability offence under the Sexual Offences Act 2023
6. **Marital rape is now criminalized** under the Sexual Offences Act 2023
7. **Criminal Procedure Code (Cap 71/RPC)** — governs arrest, bail, and trial procedure
8. Accused must be brought before a court within **48 hours** of arrest (constitutional requirement under Article 23)
9. **Right to bail** — police bail for minor offences; court may refuse bail for serious offences
10. **Burden of proof** is on the prosecution to prove guilt **beyond reasonable doubt** (Evidence Act Cap 6, Section 101)
11. **Armed robbery** (robbery while armed or with associates who are armed) — punishment is **life imprisonment**
