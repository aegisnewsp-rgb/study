**Thermal Properties** — Key Facts for JEE Main
• **Core formula or law** — Q = m × c × ΔT (Heat transfer equation)
• **Core concept** — Heat flows from higher to lower temperature until thermal equilibrium is reached, with energy conservation maintained throughout.
• **Most common application of this topic** — Calorimetry problems involving mixing of liquids/solids at different temperatures to find unknown specific heat capacities.
• **Key numerical value or constant to memorise** — Latent heat of fusion of ice = 336 kJ/kg; Latent heat of vaporisation of water = 2260 kJ/kg

**Thermal Properties** — JEE Main / Advanced Study Guide

**Thermal Properties** encompasses the study of heat, temperature, and energy transfer during physical processes. **Heat** is energy in transit due to temperature difference, while **temperature** is a measure of average kinetic energy of molecules.

The fundamental equation for heat transfer is **Q = mcΔT**, where m is mass, c is specific heat capacity, and ΔT is temperature change. **Latent heat** (L) represents heat required per unit mass for phase change: Q = mL. Water has exceptionally high specific heat (4200 J/kgK), making it an excellent coolant.

**Calorimetry** is the experimental technique based on conservation of energy — heat lost by hot body equals heat gained by cold body. The **mixing method** determines unknown specific heats by achieving thermal equilibrium.

**Newton's Law of Cooling** states that rate of cooling ∝ (T - T_surroundings), given by dT/dt = -k(T - T_s). This explains why hot coffee cools faster in a cold room.

**Thermal expansion** follows ΔL = αL₀ΔT for linear expansion, where α is the coefficient of linear expansion. This principle explains railway track gaps and bimetallic strips.

**Solved Example 1:** A 0.5 kg iron block (c = 460 J/kgK) at 100°C is dropped into 1 kg water at 20°C. Find final temperature.
