# International Organisations and Global Affairs

Bangladesh's position in global affairs is shaped significantly by its membership in international organisations, its relationships with major powers, and its active participation in multilateral forums. For the DU Admission examination, students must understand the key international bodies Bangladesh participates in, their roles, and Bangladesh's contributions to and relationships with these organisations.

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)

> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

#### 1. The United Nations and Bangladesh

Bangladesh has been an active and responsible member of the United Nations since joining in 1974. Its participation has been particularly notable in UN peacekeeping operations and climate negotiations.

**UN Peacekeeping:**
Bangladesh is one of the largest and most consistent contributors to UN peacekeeping missions worldwide:
- Bangladeshi peacekeepers have served in Somalia, Rwanda, Bosnia, Kosovo, Sierra Leone, Lebanon, and many other conflict zones
- Bangladesh consistently ranks among the top 3 contributors of uniformed personnel to UN missions
- This has earned Bangladesh significant diplomatic goodwill and international recognition
- Bangladeshi peacekeepers have been involved in protecting civilians, disarming combatants, and supporting elections in post-conflict countries

**Key UN Bodies Bangladesh Engages With:**
- **UN General Assembly:** Bangladesh has served as a non-permanent member of the Security Council (1985–86, 2000–01)
- **UN Human Rights Council:** Bangladesh participates in reviews of other countries' human rights records
- **UN Climate Framework:** Bangladesh is a leading voice in climate negotiations (UNFCCC) as a Climate Vulnerable Forum member

#### 2. SAARC and Regional Cooperation

**South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC):**
- Founded in Dhaka on 8 December 1985
- Founder members: Bangladesh, India, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Maldives, Bhutan, Nepal, Afghanistan (joined later)
- Secretariat: Kathmandu, Nepal
- Purpose: Promote economic and regional integration, cooperation in agriculture, culture, health, and anti-terrorism

**SAARC Summits:**
- 1st Summit: Dhaka (1985)
- 14th (last) Summit: Kathmandu (2014)
- SAARC has been largely paralysed by India-Pakistan tensions, particularly after the 2016 Uri attack and 2019 Pulwama attack

**BIMSTEC:**
BIMSTEC (Bay of Bengal Initiative for Multi-Sectoral Technical and Economic Cooperation) is an alternative sub-regional organisation:
- Members: Bangladesh, India, Myanmar, Sri Lanka, Thailand, Nepal, Bhutan
- Founded in Bangkok (1997)
- Bangladesh hosted the BIMSTEC summit in 2016 under Sheikh Hasina's chairmanship
- BIMSTEC is seen as more functional than SAARC because it excludes Pakistan

#### 3. Commonwealth of Nations

Bangladesh joined the Commonwealth in 1972, shortly after independence, making it one of the newer members:
- The Commonwealth is a voluntary association of 56 independent countries, most of which were formerly part of the British Empire
- Bangladesh benefits from Commonwealth scholarships, training programmes, and sports competitions (Commonwealth Games)
- Bangladesh participated in the Commonwealth Games in 2010 (Delhi) and 2018 (Gold Coast)
- The Commonwealth's head is the British monarch; Bangladesh's representation is through an appointed Governor-General (previously the President)

#### 4. Organisation of Islamic Cooperation (OIC)

Bangladesh joined the OIC in 1974 — the same year it joined the UN:
- The OIC is the second-largest intergovernmental organisation after the UN, with 57 member states
- Bangladesh has sought to use the OIC as a platform to raise issues affecting Muslim-majority nations
- Bangladesh regularly participates in OIC foreign minister conferences
- The OIC has a observer relationship with the UN and represents the Muslim world on issues of peace, security, and development

#### 5. World Trade Organization (WTO)

Bangladesh joined the WTO on 1 January 1995:
- As a least-developed country (LDC), Bangladesh enjoys preferential trade access to developed country markets (EU, US)
- Bangladesh's exports (especially garments) benefit from the WTO's Generalized System of Preferences (GSP)
- Bangladesh has been preparing for LDC graduation from WTO preferences — a significant economic policy challenge
- The EU's "Everything But Arms" (EBA) scheme allows Bangladesh duty-free access for almost all products

### 🔴 Extended — Deep Study (3m+)

> Comprehensive coverage for students on a longer study timeline.

#### 6. Bangladesh's Role in Climate Negotiations

Bangladesh punches far above its weight in international climate diplomacy:

**Climate Vulnerable Forum (CVF):**
Bangladesh is a founding member of the CVF, a group of countries that are disproportionately affected by climate change. Bangladesh has hosted two CVF ministerial meetings and has been instrumental in framing climate change as a security issue.

**UNFCCC and Paris Agreement:**
Bangladesh was one of the first countries to submit its Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC) under the Paris Agreement:
- Committed to reducing emissions by 5% unconditionally and 15% with international support (relative to business-as-usual)
- Bangladesh has established a Climate Change Trust Fund (using its own resources) — unique among developing nations
- Bangladesh successfully advocated for Loss and Damage financing at COP27 (2022) in Sharm el-Sheikh

**Key Climate Finance Mechanisms:**
- Green Climate Fund (GCF)
- Bangladesh has received significant GCF funding for climate adaptation projects
- Adaptation projects include cyclone shelters, flood-resilient housing, and coastal afforestation

#### 7. Bangladesh's Security Council and UN Reform Stance

Bangladesh has been a non-permanent member of the UN Security Council twice:
- **1985–86:** First term
- **2000–01:** Second term during Bangladesh's tenure as SAARC chair

**Bangladesh's Position on UN Security Council Reform:**
Bangladesh supports the expansion of the Security Council to include more permanent and non-permanent members, particularly from developing countries. Bangladesh believes the current P5 structure is undemocratic and unrepresentative of the Global South.

#### 8. Key Diplomatic Initiatives

**LDCs' Voice:**
Bangladesh frequently speaks on behalf of the Least Developed Countries group at the UN, representing 46 nations on issues of trade, climate, and development.

**Rohingya Crisis and International Court:**
Bangladesh has brought the Myanmar Rohingya crisis to the International Court of Justice (ICJ) and the International Criminal Court (ICC), seeking accountability for genocide. The ICJ case (Gambia vs Myanmar) drew international attention to the crisis.

> **Exam Watch:** The DU examination frequently asks about Bangladesh's international memberships. Key facts to remember: Bangladesh is a founding member of SAARC (1985, Dhaka), the 14th SAARC summit was in Kathmandu in 2014, Bangladesh is a top-3 contributor to UN peacekeeping missions, and Bangladesh joined the Commonwealth in 1972. BIMSTEC was founded in Bangkok in 1997 and Bangladesh hosted the 2016 summit.
