## Ratio, Proportion & Partnership — Quick Reference

### Key Formula/Rule
Ratio a:b means a ÷ b. Proportion a:b = c:d means a/b = c/d. Partnership profit share = Capital × Time.

### Memory Trick
A ratio is just a **division** problem in disguise. When you see 3:7, think "3 divided by 7" or "3 parts vs 7 parts total = 10 parts." For partnership, the share is proportional to **capital invested multiplied by how long it was invested**.

### 1-Sentence Summary
Tests your ability to compare quantities fairly and divide things in correct proportions — essential for profit-sharing, mixing, and age problems.

### 30-Second Example
**Q:** Divide ₹500 in the ratio 3:2.
**A:** 3+2=5 parts. Each part = 500÷5 = ₹100. So: 3×100 = ₹300 and 2×100 = ₹200.
