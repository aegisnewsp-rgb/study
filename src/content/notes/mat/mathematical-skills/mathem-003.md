# Time, Speed & Distance

### 🟢 Lite — Quick Review (1h–1d)
> Rapid summary for last-minute revision before your exam.

Time, Speed and Distance is one of the most frequently tested MAT Quant topics. The fundamental relationship is Distance = Speed × Time. Master the three core concepts: average speed, relative speed (trains, boats), and time-distance work problems.

**Core formula:**
- Distance = Speed × Time
- Speed = Distance / Time
- Time = Distance / Speed

**Critical shortcuts:**
- Average speed (equal distances): 2ab/(a+b) where a and b are the two speeds
- Relative speed (opposite directions): Sum of speeds
- Relative speed (same direction): Difference of speeds
- Train problems: Speed = length of train / time to cross

**Exam tip:** Always convert units consistently — km/hr to m/s: multiply by 5/18. m/s to km/hr: multiply by 18/5.

### 🟡 Standard — Regular Study (2d–2mo)
> Standard content for students with a few days to months.

#### Fundamental Relationships

**Basic equation:**
Distance = Speed × Time

If you travel 60 km at 40 km/hr:
Time = Distance/Speed = 60/40 = 1.5 hours = 1 hour 30 minutes

**Unit conversions:**
- km/hr to m/s: Multiply by 5/18
  - 72 km/hr = 72 × 5/18 = 20 m/s
- m/s to km/hr: Multiply by 18/5
  - 15 m/s = 15 × 18/5 = 54 km/hr

**Why 5/18?** Because 1 km = 1000 m and 1 hr = 3600 sec
So 1 km/hr = 1000/3600 m/s = 5/18 m/s

#### Average Speed

**Average speed when distance is same both ways:**
If you travel from A to B at speed a and return from B to A at speed b, covering the same distance each way:

Average Speed = 2ab / (a + b)

Example: Going at 60 km/hr and returning at 40 km/hr:
Average = 2 × 60 × 40 / (60 + 40) = 4800/100 = 48 km/hr

Note: This is NOT the arithmetic mean (50 km/hr) — the harmonic mean applies.

**Average speed when time is same:**
If you travel at different speeds for the same amount of time:
Average Speed = (a + b + c...) / n

**Weighted average speed:**
If 1/3 distance at a km/hr, 1/3 at b km/hr, 1/3 at c km/hr:
Average = 3abc / (ab + bc + ac)

#### Relative Speed

**When moving in opposite directions:**
Relative speed = a + b
Time to meet = Total distance / (a + b)

Example: Two people 120 km apart, walking toward each other at 5 km/hr and 7 km/hr:
They meet in = 120 / (5 + 7) = 120/12 = 10 hours

**When moving in same direction:**
Relative speed = |a - b|
Time to catch up = Initial distance / (a - b)

Example: Car A (60 km/hr) chasing Car B (45 km/hr) that's 30 km ahead:
Time to catch = 30 / (60 - 45) = 30/15 = 2 hours

#### Train Problems

**Time to cross a stationary object (pole, person):**
If a train of length L passes a pole (point):
Time = L / Speed

Example: Train 200 m long, speed 72 km/hr = 20 m/s:
Time to cross pole = 200/20 = 10 seconds

**Time to cross a platform:**
If train of length L passes platform of length P:
Time = (L + P) / Speed

Example: Train 150 m long, platform 300 m long, speed 45 km/hr = 12.5 m/s:
Time = (150 + 300) / 12.5 = 450/12.5 = 36 seconds

**Two trains moving in opposite directions:**
Time to cross each other = (L₁ + L₂) / (a + b)

Example: Two trains 200 m and 150 m, speeds 60 km/hr and 90 km/hr:
Convert to m/s: 60×5/18 = 16.67; 90×5/18 = 25
Relative speed = 41.67 m/s
Total length = 350 m
Time = 350/41.67 ≈ 8.4 seconds

**Two trains moving in same direction:**
Time to cross = (L₁ + L₂) / |a - b|

#### Boats and Streams

**Key concept:** River flow speed adds/subtracts from boat speed.

- Downstream speed = Speed in still water + Stream speed
- Upstream speed = Speed in still water - Stream speed

**To find speed in still water and stream speed:**
- Speed in still water = (Downstream + Upstream) / 2
- Stream speed = (Downstream - Upstream) / 2

Example: A boat goes 30 km downstream in 2 hours and returns upstream in 3 hours:
Downstream speed = 30/2 = 15 km/hr
Upstream speed = 30/3 = 10 km/hr
Speed in still water = (15 + 10)/2 = 12.5 km/hr
Stream speed = (15 - 10)/2 = 2.5 km/hr

#### Circular Motion and Races

**When runners/applications move in circular tracks:**

If two runners start from the same point on a circular track:
- Time to meet again = LCM of their individual times to complete one lap
- Or: Distance covered by faster = LCM of distances where they meet

**Example:** Two persons on a 400 m circular track, speeds 10 m/s and 8 m/s:
Time for faster to gain one full lap = 400 / (10 - 8) = 200 seconds

**Meeting point analysis:**
If they start together and run in opposite directions:
Time to meet = L / (a + b)

**Races:**
- If A gives B a start of x meters in a race of L meters, then A must cover (L + x) while B covers L
- If A gives B a start of t seconds in a time race, then A finishes in (T - t) if B finishes in T

#### Escalators and Moving Walkways

**Escalator problems combine two movements:**

Example: A man walks up an escalator (moving upward). He takes 30 steps. If he walks twice as fast, he takes 20 steps. How many steps are visible on the escalator?

Let escalator speed = e steps/second, man speed = s steps/second, total steps = N
N = 30(s + e) = 20(2s + e)
30s + 30e = 40s + 20e
10e = 10s → e = s
N = 30(2s) = 60 steps

**Clocks and Speeds:**

Minute hand: 360° in 60 minutes → 6° per minute
Hour hand: 360° in 12 hours = 720 minutes → 0.5° per minute
Relative speed = 5.5° per minute

Angle between hands at H:M:
| H > M | Angle = 30H - 5.5M |
| M > H | Use formula but take smaller angle |

#### Time and Work — Advanced

**Basic work formula:**
If A can complete a work in a days, A's work per day = 1/a
If B can complete in b days, B's work per day = 1/b
Together: 1/a + 1/b = 1/T → T = ab/(a+b)

**Pipes and Cisterns:**
- Inlet pipe fills; outlet pipe empties
- Net fill rate = Inlet rate - Outlet rate

Example: Pipe A fills in 10 min, Pipe B fills in 15 min, Outlet empties in 30 min:
Net rate = 1/10 + 1/15 - 1/30 = (3+2-1)/30 = 4/30 = 2/15
Time to fill = 15/2 = 7.5 minutes

**When work varies:**
If A is twice as efficient as B:
- If A takes x days, B takes 2x days
- Together: 1/x + 1/2x = 3/2x

#### Combined Motion Problems

**Multiple segment journeys:**

Example: A person travels from P to Q at 40 km/hr, Q to R at 60 km/hr, and R to S at 80 km/hr. If PQ = QR = RS = 120 km, find average speed.

Total distance = 360 km
Total time = 120/40 + 120/60 + 120/80 = 3 + 2 + 1.5 = 6.5 hours
Average speed = 360/6.5 ≈ 55.38 km/hr

**Shortcut for equal distances at different speeds:**
Average = n / (1/a + 1/b + 1/c...) where n = number of segments

#### Practice Questions

Q1: A train 150 m long passes a man running at 9 km/hr in the same direction in 30 seconds. Find the speed of the train.
Solution: Relative speed = 150/30 = 5 m/s = 18 km/hr
Train speed = 18 + 9 = 27 km/hr

Q2: A boat's speed in still water is 15 km/hr and stream speed is 3 km/hr. How long to go 48 km downstream and return?
Solution: Downstream = 18 km/hr; Upstream = 12 km/hr
Time = 48/18 + 48/12 = 8/3 + 4 = 6.67 hours

Q3: Two trains 200 m and 150 m long run at 60 km/hr and 90 km/hr towards each other. How long to completely pass?
Solution: Speeds in m/s: 16.67, 25; Relative = 41.67 m/s
Total length = 350 m; Time = 350/41.67 ≈ 8.4 seconds

Q4: Average speed if a car goes 200 km at 50 km/hr and returns at 30 km/hr?
Solution: 2×50×30/(50+30) = 3000/80 = 37.5 km/hr

Q5: At what time between 3 and 4 o'clock will the clock hands be at right angles?
Solution: At 3 o'clock, angle is 90°. Hands are at right angles when angle = 90°.
From 3:00: Angle = 30×3 - 5.5×M = 90 - 5.5M
Setting = 90: 90 - 5.5M = 90 or 90 - 5.5M = 270
First gives M=0 (3:00 itself); second gives M = 360/11 = 32.73 min ≈ 32 min 44 sec
Answer: Approximately 3:32:44
