## Lite:

- Core formula: wave equation: v = f λ; also v = √(T/μ) for a stretched string; wave speed in a medium: v = √(B/ρ) for longitudinal waves; wave equation: ∂²y/∂x² = (1/v²) ∂²y/∂t².

- Core concept: a wave is a disturbance that propagates through a medium, transferring energy without net transport of matter.

- Most common application: sound waves, light waves, vibrating strings.

- Key numerical value: speed of sound in air at STP ≈ 343 m/s.

- Most tested concept: relationship between speed, frequency, wavelength and superposition principle.

- Common mistake: forgetting that wave speed depends on medium properties, not on frequency or amplitude.

- Exam tip: For JEE, always check if the wave is traveling in a particular medium; if not given, assume speed of light in vacuum for EM waves; use v = f λ and v = √(T/μ) for strings.
