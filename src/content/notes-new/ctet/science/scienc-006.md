# Matter and Motion

## Matter: States and Properties

Matter is anything that has **mass** and occupies **space**. It exists in three primary states: **solid, liquid, and gas**.

### Properties of the Three States

| Property | Solid | Liquid | Gas |
|----------|-------|--------|-----|
| **Shape** | Fixed | Indefinite (takes container shape) | Indefinite |
| **Volume** | Fixed | Fixed | Indefinite (fills container) |
| **Compressibility** | Very low | Very low | High |
| **Inter-particle space** | Very less | Moderate | Very large |
| **Arrangement** | Regular, closely packed | Random, loosely packed | Random, far apart |
| **Motion of particles** | Vibrational only | Translational + vibrational | Free, rapid |
| **Examples** | Ice, rock, wood | Water, milk, oil | Oxygen, steam, helium |

**Physical change:** Change in physical properties (shape, size, state) without changing chemical composition. Example: Ice → water → steam (still H₂O)

**Chemical change:** Change producing new substances with different properties. Example: Iron + oxygen + water → rust (Fe₂O₃.nH₂O)

### Classification of Matter

- **Element:** Pure substance made of one type of atom (e.g., iron Fe, gold Au, oxygen O₂)
- **Compound:** Pure substance made of two or more elements chemically combined (e.g., H₂O, NaCl)
- **Mixture:** Two or more substances physically combined (not chemically bonded)
  - **Homogeneous mixture:** Uniform composition throughout (e.g., salt solution, air)
  - **Heterogeneous mixture:** Non-uniform composition (e.g., sand + salt, oil + water)

**CTET exam tip:** Elements and compounds are pure substances (fixed composition); mixtures have variable composition.

## Motion and Laws of Motion

### Distance and Displacement

- **Distance:** Total path length traveled; scalar quantity (only magnitude)
- **Displacement:** Shortest path between initial and final position; vector quantity (magnitude + direction)
- **Speed:** Distance traveled per unit time (scalar)
- **Velocity:** Rate of change of displacement (vector)
- **Acceleration:** Rate of change of velocity

### Newton's Laws of Motion

**First Law (Law of Inertia):** A body continues in its state of rest or uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.
- Example: Passengers jerk forward when a bus suddenly stops (their bodies tend to continue moving forward)
- **Mass** is the measure of inertia (more mass = more inertia)

**Second Law:** Force equals mass times acceleration (F = ma)
- The greater the force applied, the greater the acceleration
- The greater the mass, the smaller the acceleration for the same force
- **Unit of force:** Newton (N) = kg × m/s²

**Third Law:** For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
- Example: When you push a wall, the wall pushes back with equal force
- Rocket propulsion: Hot gases expelled downward → rocket moves upward

### Friction

Friction is the force that **opposes relative motion** between two surfaces in contact.

- **Types:** Static friction (when body is at rest), Sliding friction, Rolling friction
- **Rolling friction < Sliding friction < Static friction** (hence wheels reduce friction)
- **Disadvantage:** Wastes energy, causes wear and tear
- **Advantage:** Enables walking, writing, driving (we can only walk because of friction between shoes and ground)

**Methods to reduce friction:**
- Lubricating surfaces (oil, grease)
- Using ball bearings
- Streamlining shapes (reduces air resistance)
- Polishing surfaces

### Work, Energy, and Power

**Work (W):** When a force causes displacement in the direction of the force.
> **W = F × d** (Force × Distance)
> Unit: Joule (J) = Newton × metre

**Note:** If displacement is perpendicular to force, work done = 0 (e.g., carrying a load while walking horizontally — force is upward, displacement is horizontal)

**Energy:** The capacity to do work. Forms include:
- **Kinetic Energy (KE):** Energy of motion
  > KE = ½ mv²
- **Potential Energy (PE):** Energy of position/state
  > Gravitational PE = mgh (where h = height above ground)
  > Elastic PE = ½ kx² (where k = spring constant, x = compression/extension)

**Conservation of Energy:** Energy can neither be created nor destroyed, only transformed from one form to another. Total energy remains constant.

**Power:** Rate of doing work
> **P = W / t** (Work / Time)
> Unit: Watt (W) = Joule/second

**High-yield formula questions in CTET:**
1. A body of mass 5 kg is moving at 10 m/s. Find its KE. → KE = ½ × 5 × 10² = 250 J
2. A 2 kg object is at height 10 m. Find its PE. → PE = 2 × 10 × 10 = 200 J

## Simple Machines

A simple machine makes work easier by either:
- Multiplying the applied force (mechanical advantage)
- Changing the direction of applied force
- Increasing the speed of motion

### Types of Simple Machines

| Machine | Description | Examples |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Lever** | Rigid bar that rotates around a fixed point (fulcrum) | Crowbar, seesaw, scissors, wheelbarrow |
| **Pulley** | Wheel with a groove; changes direction of force | Flagpole pulley, construction cranes |
| **Inclined plane** | Flat surface tilted at an angle | Ramps, sloping roads, wedges (axe) |
| **Wedge** | Combination of two inclined planes | Knife, axe, chisel |
| **Wheel and axle** | Large wheel attached to a smaller axle | Steering wheel, door knob |
| **Screw** | Inclined plane wrapped around a cylindrical shaft | Screw, bolt, jar lid |

### Lever Classes

1. **First class:** Fulcrum between effort and load (e.g., seesaw, scissors, crowbar)
2. **Second class:** Load between fulcrum and effort (e.g., wheelbarrow, nutcracker)
3. **Third class:** Effort between fulcrum and load (e.g., human arm, fishing rod)

**Mechanical Advantage (MA):** 
> MA = Load / Effort (for ideal machines, MA = velocity ratio)

**Velocity Ratio (VR):** 
> VR = Distance moved by effort / Distance moved by load

⚡ **CTET Important:** Remember F = ma, W = Fd, KE = ½mv², PE = mgh. Know the difference between mass (scalar, kg) and weight (force, N = mg, where g = 9.8 m/s²). Understand friction — always opposes motion.
